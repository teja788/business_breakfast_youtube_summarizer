# Kutumba Rao — Reusable Method & Instincts (Dec 2025 – Feb 2026)

> Scope note: In this window Kutumba Rao was absent from many episodes (the panel rotates to Kranthi/Ramakrishna/Vasanth). Everything below is drawn only from episodes where his commentary is explicitly attributed: **2025-12-05, 12-11, 12-17; 2026-01-30; 02-06, 02-13, 02-20, 02-24, 02-27** (with light use of 01-27, whose transcript matches his role but doesn't name him).

## Market-regime logic
- **The core binary he names out loud:** "buy-on-dips market" vs "sell-on-rallies market." He states which regime is active almost every day.
  - *"Buy-on-dips market, NOT a sell-on-rallies market."* (30 Jan) · *"still a buy-on-dips market."* (24 Feb)
- **A single Nifty round-number level is the regime switch.** As long as the index holds/reclaims it, he stays constructive; a decisive break flips him to caution/"bear zone." The level rolls down as the market weakens: 26,000 (Dec) → 25,800 → 25,500 → 25,000 (Feb).
  - *"Sustaining 26,000 is key"* (5 Dec) · *"25,500 is the inflection point... no fresh positions"* until reclaimed (27 Feb).
- **Failure-to-cross = weak trend.** Repeated rejection at a level (not a single dip) is what turns him bearish. *"Failed to cross 26,000, and slipped/corrected from there."* (13 Feb)
- **Asymmetry mental model:** markets *"climbed gently over three days like steps, then dropped in one day like a lift"* (20 Feb) — up slow, down fast.
- **Consolidation/wait regime** when range-bound with no catalyst: he explicitly defers directional conviction to the next event (Budget, trade deal, Fed, war). *"Confidence comes only after a bottom."* (20 Feb)
- **Event-sequencing frame:** he maps the next weeks as an ordered chain of binary catalysts. *"Next one month driven by three events in sequence: Indo-Russian meeting → US Fed → Indo-US trade deal."* (5 Dec)

## Macro inputs & thresholds
- **Rupee (USD/INR) — his most-cited macro variable, watched at round-number thresholds.** Weak rupee = export/IT/pharma positive, importer/durables/fertilizer negative, but broadly a bearish flow signal.
  - *"No relief unless below 90."* (5 Dec) · rupee crossing 91 flagged as persistent weakness (17 Dec); touched 92, *"weakest globally"* (30 Jan); *"itching to cross 91"* (27 Feb).
- **Crude up = fiscal-deficit pressure = bearish** (20 Feb). **Strong dollar index = bearish** (20 Feb).
- **FII vs DII/MF flows:** continued FII selling with no fresh buying = weak; DII/SIP buying is the floor. *"FIIs only Rs 108 cr, DIIs only Rs 277 cr"* = weak support (13 Feb). FII index short ~Rs 38,000 cr read as the crowd being short (27 Jan).
- **Mutual funds are "the biggest players now" — track their entries/exits per stock.** Be cautious in names MFs have *fully* exited; treat sustained MF buying as a strength signal (17 Dec).
- **US/global cues & flow-diversion:** he watches where global money is going. Yen's best week in 15 months → *"funds flow to Japan"* starving India (13 Feb); foreign-market FOMO (South Korea +80%, US +20-40% vs Nifty +5%) (30 Jan).
- **RBI policy** (rate cut, CRR/SLR, GDP revision, commentary) as a single-day decider (5 Dec); notes the rate-cut cycle may have *"temporarily paused"* (6 Feb).
- **Budget = the big trigger** capable of a 500-700pt move; *"clear direction likely only after the Budget."*
- **VIX / breadth** (see below). Also cites monsoon (good = bullish), inflation (soft = benign), GDP prints (Fitch 7.4% positive), and a forward caveat on the **GSDP/CPI base-year change** distorting macro reads (6 Feb).

## Options / positioning signals
- **PCR as a fear gauge with a contrarian floor.** Below 1 = weak/fear building; a collapse toward ~0.7 is oversold and a *buy* trigger for position traders.
  - *"Put-call ratio fell to ~0.82 → fear beginning."* (13 Feb) · *"Below 0.7 PCR is a good bet to buy for position traders."* (20 Feb)
- **VIX bands:** ~10-12 = comfort/bull confidence; >13 = *"discomfort for bulls"*; >14 = elevated risk. *"Anything above 13 is discomfort for bulls."* (20 Feb) · wants ~12 for bull confidence (27 Feb).
- **OI structure = ceiling/floor:** highest call OI strike is resistance; highest put OI strike is the support/"rounding bottom" zone. *"Call OI heavy at 26,500; crossing 26,000/26,100 is key."* (5 Dec)
- **Speculative longs vs shorts:** he reads OI build-up to locate where speculators are crowded (every episode lists specific long/short names), and treats a market where *"only 3 Nifty stocks rose"* as dangerously one-sided (20 Feb).
- **"Delivery-based conviction" vs speculation:** a recurring, near-daily distinction — delivery-backed buying = real institutional accumulation and trustworthy; F&O/speculative moves are not.
- **F&O ban list** tracked each day as a froth/liquidity signal.
- **Options are a hedging tool, not for speculation** (6 Feb); weeklies traded day-to-day with positions cut daily (30 Jan).

## Stock-evaluation checklist (recurring lenses)
- **"Value vs fancy"** — separates fundamental value from momentum/hype ("fancy"). A stock can have value but no fancy (won't move), or fancy with no value (dangerous).
- **Consolidation-band → breakout-above-level price structure.** He frames most stocks as a range that needs a trigger to break. *"Range-bound 120-140 unless game-changing news."* (6 Feb, Standard Glass) · Suzlon *"consolidating between 50 and 59-60... back to 70-75"* (5 Dec). Lower-top/lower-bottom = weak; higher bottoms forming = trend turning.
- **52-week-high vs 52-week-low positioning.** Near 52-wk low = weak/caution (or deep value); near 52-wk high = strength. In uncertain markets he *prefers* 52-wk-high momentum names, and watches the market-wide highs:lows ratio (27 Feb: *"1:2, lows 2-3x the highs → weakness continues"*).
- **PE stance, stated explicitly.** *"Only ~20 PE"* = buy (Vedanta, 17 Dec); *"P/E ~5 (peanuts)"* + high dividend (REC); ~100 PE with negligible profit = avoid (JK Enterprises, 27 Feb).
- **Last-quarter result + how the stock reacted.** Recurringly says "decide only after this quarter's result." Key tell: *"even good results couldn't lift the stock — we are missing out on something"* (6 Feb) = exit.
- **Earnings visibility** as the precondition for adding/pyramiding.
- **Hidden value / SOTP / demerger unlock:** Vedanta demerger, Tata Motors post-demerger SOTP, Morepen medical-devices demerger, MCX turnaround, Capstone's new subsidiary.
- **Promoter/owner quality FIRST.** *"Look at the owner/promoter first (capability), then P&L, balance sheet, earnings visibility"* (27 Jan). Strong founders = Godrej Properties; weak promoter = Ola Electric / Vedanta ("promoter's whim").
- **Order book vs execution** (defence/EMS/infra): a growing order book means nothing without delivery — *"repeated execution failures despite a growing order book"* (Avantel).
- **Dividend yield as an "FD alternative"** — a whole recurring bucket (NTPC, PFC, Canara, PNB, IRFC, NHPC, LIC Housing, REC, Coal India), always paired with a 3-5-year horizon caveat for PSUs.
- **Equity size / face value / paid-up:** large equity = *"hard to service"* (Standard Glass); Rs 1 face value = red flag ("don't go near it," Facor Alloys).
- **Niche / one-of-a-kind moat** = positive (NephroPlus, KIMS, VA Tech Wabag "most patents," Rainbow comparison).
- **Institutional footprint & sector rotation into institutional hands** (hospitals: *"FIIs/PE buying the sector"*).
- **Balance-sheet quality / debt:** favors *"efficient low-debt"* names (dairy, 27 Feb). Notes ASM/surveillance status (Apollo Micro).

## Risk & position management
- **Trailing stops once in profit** are near-mandatory. *"Those in profit must use trailing stops."* (13 Feb) · SJS/CG Power *"hold with a trailing stop."*
- **Book partial profits to make a position "free of cost."** *"Book ~50% profit... make the balance free of cost."* (13 Feb, Avanti Feeds); L&T Finance "book partial."
- **Accumulate on dips slowly, in tranches — but ONLY quality names.** *"Buy any large quantity in tranches"* (WPIL); Laurus *"Accumulate ~920-950"* at multi-month supports; Bharat Forge *"bottom-fish."*
- **Do NOT average down weak/falling stocks** — sharp, repeated distinction from accumulating quality. *"Hold, do NOT average"* (Apollo Micro); *"avoid averaging"* (Suzlon, Tata Motors).
- **"Don't pyramid" (add to winners) in a flat market** — pyramid only after earnings visibility + confirmed uptrend. *"Not the time to pyramid... be very cautious."* (11 Dec)
- **Exit weak stocks INTO rallies, not into weakness.** *"Better to exit on rallies"* (recurring).
- **Mandatory hard stop-loss on speculative / high-level entries.** *"If buying above 600, mandatory trading stop-loss"* (Newjaisa); Vishal Mega Mart *"buy with SL 110."*
- **Horizon discipline stated explicitly.** *"Long term = minimum more than one year"* (20 Feb); turnarounds 2-3 yrs; PSU dividend plays 3-5 yrs.
- **Asset allocation:** keep min ~50% in equities (young investors 75-80%); *"don't chase one hot asset class"* — gold's extra rise is partly rupee depreciation and will unwind (30 Jan).
- **Capital-preservation posture in stock-picker markets:** *"This is a stock-picking, capital-preservation time."* (17 Dec)

## When he says AVOID / SELL
- **Already priced-in / discounted news.** *"The market has largely factored it in"* — Polycab near 52-wk high despite the data-centre story (24 Feb). Also applied to IndiGo/Zomato.
- **Even good results fail to move it** → *"we are missing out on something"* → exit (T&R, 6 Feb).
- **Weak price structure (lower-top/lower-bottom).** HBL Engineering *"Sell on rallies as of now."* (27 Feb)
- **Single-location / regional / single-customer concentration.** Tejas Networks *"~80% revenue dependent on BSNL"* → sell; L&T Finance *"concentrated in suburbs/tier-2"*; Malu Paper (only large paper cos survive).
- **Over-valued with thin profits** (~100 PE, negligible earnings — JK Enterprises).
- **Weak / whimsical promoter.** Vedanta *"runs at the promoter's whim... leave such risky bets"* (20 Feb); Ola Electric *"weak promoter, hence the chaos."*
- **Structural sector decline** (IT under AI disruption) → exit into any technical rebound.
- **Hype-to-reality de-rating** (DCX Systems, Saint DLM "came from hype to reality"); loss-makers; and **news-pop rallies that fizzle** — *"Anil Ambani group news-flow rallies fizzle out quickly"* (Reliance Infra, 13 Feb).
- **Governance flags / Rs 1 face value** (Facor Alloys "don't go near it"; Kaynes governance overhang).

## Recurring structural / sector themes
- **IT & agentic-AI disruption** — the dominant Feb thesis and his most emphatic macro call. *"Wealth creation from now by IT companies is so doubtful."* (6 Feb) · *"AI disruption is one disruption which cannot be ignored."* (13 Feb) He extends it to a Hyderabad/Bengaluru **real-estate corollary** (27 Feb).
- **Data centres / power / energy / cables (Polycab, KEI, Sterlite, KRN, Netweb)** — real beneficiaries, but warns they're largely priced-in; pick 52-wk-high names you can track.
- **Semiconductor** (CG Power, Kaynes, Netweb, MCX). **Defence** — *large-caps only* (Bharat Forge, BEL, HAL, BDL); avoid mid/small-cap defence.
- **Renewables/wind** (Suzlon, Inox Wind, Waaree — incl. AP's giga-factory as "India's No. 1 renewable destination").
- **Pharma** (Laurus his preferred name amid sector churning); **hospitals/healthcare** (KIMS; PE/FII accumulation).
- **PSU dividend / FD-alternative** basket; **dairy & consumption** (Heritage); **realty** (Godrej Properties, Lodha).
- **Structural IPO/RTA shift "from old stocks to new."** *"Structural shift from old stocks to new... IPO boom lasting 3-4 more months."* (17 Dec)
- **Gold/silver caution** (FOMO-driven, rupee-inflated). **India structural bull:** *"Make India Indispensable"* + policy dynamism (GST 2.0, tax relief, labour codes) (30 Jan).

## Verbal tells & mental models
- **"Buy on dips" / "buy-on-dips market"** vs **"sell-on-rallies market"** — his master switch.
- **"Exit on rallies"** — the standard instruction for weak holdings (never dump into weakness).
- **"Has value but no fancy" / "fancy"** — value vs momentum shorthand.
- **"Already discounted / already factored in / priced in"** — his over-valuation tell.
- **"Hero or zero"** (binary high-risk bets — JP Power, Pace Digitek).
- **"Dark-horse bet/counter"** (Reliance Infra, Sterlite Tech, Shree Renuka).
- **"Make it free of cost"** — book enough to remove risk capital.
- **"Best of times/its time are over"** vs **"worst is over, buy on correction."**
- **"Not the time to pyramid."**
- **"Peanuts"** for a very low PE (REC ~5x).
- **"Climbed like steps, dropped like a lift"** (up-slow/down-fast); **"markets dance to Trump's steps"** (event-driven humility).
- **"Release will come, but not by announcement"** (turns aren't pre-signalled) (17 Dec).
- **"Look at the owner/promoter first."**
- **"Stock-picking, capital-preservation time"** / **"stock-specific, sector-specific market"** (his default characterization of this whole window — no runaway rally, moves are idiosyncratic).
