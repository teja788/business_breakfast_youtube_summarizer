## 1. Market-regime logic

He runs an explicit, announced **three-state regime machine** — "buy on dips" / "sell on rallies" / consolidation-wait — and tells viewers when he flips it.

- **Regime = f(index vs OI-defined band, crude direction, FII flow, VIX/PCR).** The daily band is read directly off the option chain: highest call OI = ceiling, highest put OI = floor; break the floor → regime turns defensive.
  - "Sell on rallies / dead-cat bounces (the earlier 'buy on dips' stance is over)." (03-09, day of the oil-shock gap-down)
  - "Short-term stance flipped to 'buy on dips' (from the earlier 'sell on rallies')." (04-15, after crude fell to ~$91)
- **Level triggers are stated as if-then rules**, refreshed daily: "until 23,000 is crossed the market will not improve... if 22,000 is cut there are indications of an even more terrible fall" (04-02); "holding above 23,800 = 'out of the woods' for the medium term" (04-08); "strength comes only above 24,500, and it could retest 24,200" (04-21); "be very, very cautious on fresh buying... unless Nifty stabilises above 24,500" (04-30).
- **Even in the bearish state he almost never says liquidate.** The default is "stay invested" in strong names + exit weak names into rallies. "This is NOT the time to panic / liquidate." (03-02); "stay invested only in good stocks" (04-30).
- **He audits the QUALITY of an up-move before trusting it**: settlement-day bounces = short covering, not buying; he checks *which* stocks led. "Yesterday's bounce [was] largely short-covering... the stocks that rose were not encouraging" (03-18); "The positive open should not be trusted" (04-24). Four successive "open weak, close stable" sessions = accumulation, "something is cooking" (04-08).
- **Known-unknown doctrine**: events telegraphed for weeks are pre-priced; only genuine uncertainty breaks trend. "The uncertainty over whether the strike would happen was more damaging... than the actual event" (03-02); "where there is uncertainty rather than certainty, markets cannot trend" (04-02).
- **Historical analogs bound the expected damage/recovery**: war-correction table (Iraq −10%/37 days; Russia–Ukraine −7%/10 days; Hamas–Israel −4-5%/33 days → "expect at most another ~10% recovery, and not swift," 04-06); inflation-adjusted Brent has never beaten ~$179-180 (03-16).
- **Regime has a stated expiry date**: he timelines the macro overhang ("prepare for six to eight more months of uncertainty-driven markets," 04-02, pegged to US mid-terms).

## 2. Macro inputs & thresholds

His daily macro dashboard, with the bands he actually uses:

- **Crude = the master variable for India** (transmission: crude → CAD/inflation/rupee → GDP → market). Uses the crude India *actually buys* (heavy/high-sulphur, Dubai spot), not Brent — "India barely uses Brent (only ~2-3%)" (03-18). Thresholds: **~$90 = market can stabilise / "if oil drops to ~90, Nifty has scope to rise another 500-600 points"** (03-16, 03-18); $100 = on-and-off resistance (03-16); rule of thumb **"every 10 dollars on crude ≈ ~1% GDP hit"** (03-09). Even post-ceasefire: "$95–96 is still 50%+ above the old ~$60" — not all-clear (04-08).
- **Rupee**: 92-93 = stressed; **watch 94; ~95/record-low "would keep FIIs away"** (04-24, 04-30). A fourth straight down-day = RBI's defence "fizzling," FII selling returning (04-24).
- **FII vs DII, quoted daily to the crore**: FII selling stopping is a *precondition* for a sustained move — "FII selling must stop and global investors must return" (04-08); "Recovery is difficult until FII support returns" (03-09). DII cash is a cushion, not a driver; he flags the *sign flip* ("When FIIs sell at highs, DIIs buy — remember that," 04-21). MF/DII flush-with-cash = downside support (03-02).
- **VIX bands**: 12-13 = bulls' comfort (03-09); 15-16 = comfort zone (03-18, 04-16); **below 18 fine; above 20 = negative for bulls (04-21); above 25 = "very uncomfortable" (04-06)**; three rising sessions in a row = danger (03-16).
- **PCR bands**: **~0.7 = contrarian buying zone** (03-16); 0.84 weak (03-09); above 1 = healthy; 1.1-1.2 = bulls confident (04-08, 04-21); take positional index trades "only below 0.9, closer to 0.7" (04-24).
- **Monsoon = the standing domestic risk**: Skymet ~80% drought probability → "would clearly hit consumer stocks and agri-related stocks" (04-15); kharif/monsoon gates fertilizer calls (03-16).
- **US as weathervane and tail risk**: Dow futures/Nikkei each morning; Mag-7 concentration ($22tn > rest of world combined, 04-30); "If America starts to sink, prepare for a long bear phase" (04-02). Fed *commentary* (dissents, inflation projection, GDP guidance) matters more than the rate move itself (04-08, 04-30).
- **Second-order sentiment gauges he treats as signal**: shrinking MTF book = *positive* ("lower MTF = stronger investors," 04-15); **F&O turnover −25% while cash turnover at a 2-year high = "all the action has shifted to the broader market"** (04-24); market-cap-erosion headlines — "watch for the day such 'wealth evaporated' headlines stop" (03-16); Bitcoin 4-week high = global risk appetite returning (04-15); gold refusing to fall = "fear has settled back into equities" (04-24). Gold itself: without central-bank buying he distrusts highs — "I will be surprised if they hold" (03-02).

## 3. Options / positioning signals

- **The option chain is his primary map**: highest call OI = resistance, highest put OI = support, every single episode (25,000/25,400 on 03-02 → 23,000/22,000 at the bottom on 04-06 → 24,500/24,000 on 04-24). Strike *migration* and *unwinding* are the tell: "heavy unwinding at 25,000 calls... highest monthly calls now at 24,500" (04-24).
- **PCR and VIX read daily against the fixed bands above**; call-vs-put implied vol skew occasionally cited (call IV 11.16 vs put IV 12.74, 03-02).
- **Daily three-bucket stock-positioning scan**: (1) speculative longs, (2) shorts building ("signs of further correction"), (3) **"conviction delivery buying" — his smart-money tell** (e.g., Sun Pharma, HDFC Life, Nestle on 04-30). Shorts building in resilient banks = "be afraid of further correction there" (03-09).
- **F&O ban list checked daily**; a near-empty ban list = "speculators cautious / playing safe" (04-16). Single-stock OI anomalies flagged: Jio Finance OI ~20 crore — "the suspicion arises that something is cooking" (04-24).
- **He trades options himself, small and disciplined**: a straddle into the ceasefire (book at the open, "don't get greedy," 04-08); judges premium *price* vs value ("24,000 put ~457 is very high — would prefer nearer 350-360," 04-08); volatile fortnights are a "festival for options traders" — but strict stop-losses (04-08).
- **Hedging doctrine**: index puts as portfolio insurance for holders (1-crore portfolio ≈ 8 lots of protective puts, "like buying insurance," 03-09); stock-level option overlays "only with experience"; "don't hedge just for the sake of it" (03-09).
- **MTF/leverage = never**: buying ETFs on margin is "'foolishness,' do not do it" (04-08).

## 4. Stock-evaluation checklist

The recurring lenses, roughly in the order he applies them:

1. **52-week-range positioning first.** Near 52w high → never chase; buy the pullback to a named support with a trailing stop ("if in profit, put a trailing stop... to buy fresh, wait for a correction," Sai Life 03-02; "any post-result correction close to 900 is a good entry," Syrma 04-21). Near 52w low → don't knife-catch; wait for a **base** and a specific lower entry ("only below 20 might one take a chance... [wait for it to] base and then grow," HMA Agro 03-02; "buying nearer 200 is better," HLE Glascoat 04-02).
2. **PE sanity even after a crash.** A stock down 60% can still be expensive: "down from 660 to 250, but even after the fall it trades at a 47 PE — not cheap" (04-02). "Hype/80-PE stock, not cheap" (Vishal Mega Mart 03-25). High PE is tolerated only when results keep pacing it (Syrma at ~86 PE, profits Rs 65→94 cr, 04-21).
3. **Top line vs bottom line divergence** — his fastest screen: "top line held but bottom line dipped" recurs as a red flag (Sam Metalics 03-06, Sergility 03-02). One good quarter isn't a turnaround: "the recent turnaround was a seasonal result — don't get carried away" (NFL 03-02). He also strips currency effects: Infosys "21% YoY jump is visible but constant currency grew only 1.5-3%" (04-24).
4. **Business-model quality / margins.** Manufacturers over distributors: a 3%-NPM distribution company is an avoid "unless fundamentally very sound with huge volumes like Redington" (03-06). Fat-margin franchises get held through drawdowns ("one of few cos with ~50%-plus margins," Linde 03-25). Sector test: "only those that sustain margins as costs rise will perform" (pharma, 04-02).
5. **Promoter/insider behaviour.** Promoter buying = accumulate signal (NCC — "promoters buying on and off," 03-18; "in fact average it," 04-06; Jindal Saw 03-16). Inverse: **pure-OFS IPOs = "question the promoters' intent"** — 68 pure-OFS deals, nearly all below listing price (04-06). Government disinvestment/QIP prospects prop PSU prices (NFL, MOIL 03-18); state-government stakes are a supply overhang (MOIL 03-18).
6. **Order book vs execution.** Order book creates the story, execution proves it: "huge order book (execution unknown until results)" (Bondada 04-15); BEL's ~Rs 1 lakh cr backlog = add on dips (04-06); "low-debt infra firms with strong order books will do well" (04-06).
7. **Dividend/FD-alternative bucket.** Some stocks are re-classified as income plays: railway stocks "have become dividend stocks... buy only for the dividend; otherwise nothing else" (IRFC 03-02); Coal India = dividend play for "low-risk-appetite" 2-5-year holders (04-16). He even plays the ex-dividend mechanics: hold for dividend, exit on the ~5-10% post-dividend rally (Canara/SBI/Infosys, 04-30).
8. **Equity structure / free float.** Tiny, undiluted equity is a quality marker: "MNCs like Hitachi Energy don't dilute equity: on Rs 9 cr equity, Rs 7,200 cr turnover" (04-21). Checks pending warrants/convertibles for EPS dilution and compares "total market cap vs free-float market cap — high free-float makes a stock harder to move up" (04-15). Flags paid-up/face-value context on penny-priced names (03-02).
9. **Hidden value / SOTP / corporate actions.** Subsidiary value-unlock (HDFC Bank via Securities/AMC/Life "at not even 11-12 PE," 04-21); demergers traded around the record date ("add slowly before the demerger record date; post-demerger add again based on the resulting entities," Vedanta 03-25); IPL-franchise stakes worth multiples of market cap (RPSG, 03-25); NSE IPO unlocking value for govt insurers (04-21); NCLT/buyback as exit routes (JP Associates 03-25).
10. **Niche moat / optionality kicker.** A beaten stock with a real option gets "hold/watch" not "sell": Time Technoplast's approved hydrogen cylinders (04-30); Cupid's steady niche (04-02); MapMyIndia "could itself become an acquisition target" (03-18); Fractal as "the only listed AI play" (04-30).
11. **Crude-exposure sector map** (applied mechanically in the war phase): oil up → avoid aviation, paints, chemicals, logistics, gas-based fertilizers, ceramics, autos, EMS, OMCs; beneficiaries → upstream oil, defence, sugar (oil-proxy), pharma, essential commodities (03-09). "Stocks insulated from West Asia/crude can see unexpected rallies" (04-24).
12. **Volume forensics.** Volumes too thin → "check for possible insider trading" (03-06); huge volume on a falling stock → determine "whether it's a bottom or an institutional exit before acting" (Cyient DLM 03-18); 5-6x-normal-volume movers logged daily as rotation clues (04-24).
13. **Under-ownership as fuel**: "under-owned stocks rise quickly as fresh buying comes in — watch that one factor" (04-16); the April small-cap melt-up read as "a comeback rally from under-ownership" (04-30).
14. **New listings: never act on the print.** "The market is supreme always — wait two-three days, at least a week" for a band to form (Sai Parenterals 04-02).

## 5. Risk & position management

- **Tranche everything.** "Nobody catches the absolute bottom or sells the absolute top... nibble in the bottom zone 5-10% at a time" (Bajaj Holdings / ITC Hotels, 04-02); "accumulate slowly on weakness rather than buying in one go" (Cupid 04-02).
- **Averaging down is earned, not automatic.** Weak-franchise losers: "Avoid (stop averaging)... don't keep averaging a 20-30% loss; cut exposure on any rally" (Persistent/Coforge 03-18); "don't average / avoid adding now" (Union Bank 04-30). Strong franchise + promoter buying: "Hold; in fact average it" (NCC 04-06).
- **His signature move — exit weak stocks INTO strength**: "keep exiting weaker stocks on any small relief rally" (03-18); "use this rally to dump weak/'dud' stocks... remove the duds ruthlessly" (04-08); "use this rally as the likely last chance to exit weaker stocks" (04-16). Sell targets for losers are pre-named rally levels ("exit on rallies near Rs 100," NFL 03-02; "sell into a small rally to 260-280," GSPL 03-25).
- **Trailing stops on winners; hard index stop for positions**: "keep 24,000 as the stop-loss for Nifty positions, trail it, and protect profits" (04-24).
- **Partial profit-booking so the rest rides free**: book straddle gains at the open, "don't get greedy" (04-08); "reduce partially if selling returns near 330-340" (PCBL 04-30); "no harm booking some high-priced stocks in results season" (04-21).
- **Dark-horse sizing**: "only as a small, risky bet; don't over-allocate" (Ola Electric 04-06).
- **Horizon is always stated with the call**: "could rise another 50-60% in 8 months; one-year view" (Laurus 04-30); "needs patience — wait 3-4 quarters" (04-15); "only buy/hold with a 2-5 year outlook; if you panic over a 10-20 rupee fall, don't touch it" (Coal India 04-16). Retirement money is fenced: PF into equity "only if you have risk appetite... and a 5-year-plus horizon; otherwise don't disturb retirement money" (03-25).
- **In crashes: neither knife-catch nor short.** "Do not catch the falling knife" (03-09) but also "Don't short... once geopolitics improves and an uptrend begins, then go aggressive" (03-16). Doing nothing is a position: on Rs 12 lakh fresh cash — "there's no hurry at all; let's wait and watch" (04-02).
- **No leverage, ever** (MTF = "foolishness," 04-08; repeated caution 04-15). Weekend/holiday sessions discounted (low volumes, premium decay by Monday).

## 6. When he says AVOID / SELL — the rejection tells

- **Structurally thin business**: distributor/trader, ~3% margins, thin volumes → avoid (DC Infotech 03-06).
- **Stock-specific weakness, not market weakness**: "its weakness came from a weak December result, not just the market" → don't average, don't reconsider (ADSL 03-16).
- **Patience exhausted / dead money**: "has tested patience for 5 years, no movement despite good news" → exit on rallies (Yes Bank 04-08).
- **The theme is over**: "Best of times for railway stocks are over" → exit on rallies, ex-dividend (RVNL/IRCTC 03-02).
- **Still expensive after the crash** (the 47-PE test, HLE Glascoat 04-02); **hype/IPO-victim pricing** (Trident: "valuations high... expensive — book profit on momentum," 04-08).
- **Structural margin squeeze in the sector**: IGL — APM gas cut hitting distribution margins → "Avoid (temporarily, until results)" (04-30); OMCs — "assume the worst days are still ahead" (04-02).
- **No catalyst left**: "he doesn't think surprises are going to come in Bata" → sell on rallies (04-24).
- **Wrong-way macro exposure right now**: Gulf-facing exporter in a Gulf war → avoid (HMA Agro 03-02).
- **Promoter cash-out signature**: pure-OFS IPOs (04-06).
- Note the **graded rejection ladder** — he rarely shouts "sell now": *don't buy fresh / hold if held* → *stop averaging* → *exit on rallies at level X* → *avoid*. Hard immediate sells are rare; exits are scheduled into strength.

## The distilled daily algorithm (as an AI would run it)

1. **Overnight scan**: US close/futures, Asia, crude (Dubai/heavy, not Brent), rupee, gold, Bitcoin, any geopolitical newsflow → classify as known-unknown (priced) vs new uncertainty.
2. **Flow check**: yesterday's FII/DII cash numbers; is FII selling stopping, or the sign flipping?
3. **Option-chain map**: highest call OI (ceiling), highest put OI (floor), strike migration/unwinding, PCR vs bands (0.7 buy-zone / >1 healthy), VIX vs bands (<18 ok, >20 bearish, >25 danger), ban list, and the three positioning buckets (spec longs / shorts building / conviction delivery buying).
4. **Set the regime**: buy-on-dips, sell-on-rallies, or consolidation — with explicit if-then index levels for today, and announce any flip against his prior stance.
5. **Standing orders regardless of regime**: stay invested in fundamentally strong names; exit weak/dud names into any rally at pre-named levels; no leverage; tranche all fresh buying; trail stops on winners; size dark horses small; state the horizon with every call.
6. **Per-stock Q&A**: run the checklist — 52w position → PE-after-fall → topline/bottomline & margins → business model → promoter behaviour → order book/execution → dividend value → equity structure/float → hidden value/corporate actions → niche optionality → crude/sector exposure → volume forensics → under-ownership — and phrase the verdict on the graded ladder (Buy staggered / Accumulate on dips / Hold / Watch / Don't-average / Exit-on-rallies-at-X / Avoid).
