## 1. Market-regime logic

- **One benchmark level defines the regime, and he names it daily.** Through this whole slice it is Nifty **24,000** (briefly 23,300/23,800 during the June dip). Above it = safe/buy-on-dips; trading below it = weak. "24,000 is the key point — as long as Nifty trades below it, markets stay weak" (12 May). "As long as the Nifty holds above 24,000 there is no need to be afraid" (13 Jul).
- **Regime flip is explicit and volume-conditional**: default is buy-on-dips; it becomes sell-on-rallies only on a *volume* break of the benchmark. "It remains a buy-on-dips market, not sell-on-rallies; a volume break below 24,000 would turn it into a sell-on-rallies market" (17 Jul).
- **Sell-on-rallies also triggers at the top of the range**: after a sharp run into heavy call-OI resistance, book. "'Sell-on-rallies' only if it closes up to 24,300–500 with a sharp rally → then book profits" (21 May).
- **Default state is a named range** (e.g., 23,600–24,500 on 12 May; 23,300–23,800 on 3 Jun; 23,800–24,600 on 23 Jun). Inside the range: no index trades, only stock-specific work.
- **Breakouts need volume + a macro trigger**, not price alone: "if it can cross 24,000 with volumes" plus West Asia calming / trade-deal news → "indications of a big rally" (3 Jun). A close above the band = "new times" (6 May).
- **Support breaks are treated as non-linear**: a decisive close below support implies a *big* fall, not a drift — "expect a big fall… Nifty could slide 600–700 points in a couple of days" (3 Jun).
- **Rallies must be "digested."** After 5 straight up-days or an 800–900-pt two-day move, a 100–200-pt pullback is healthy consolidation, not a reversal: "the market needs to digest… like a buffet; a 150–200 point correction is no cause for panic" (16 Jun).
- **He audits whether a move was proportionate to the news**: "a 300–400 point cut was justified; 500+ points was unwarranted given how European markets and US futures behaved" (9 Jul). Overshoots get partially retraced next day.
- **Index ≠ market.** He constantly splits index regime from broader-market regime: "range-bound at the index level, but the broader market is strong… stock-specific traders made ample money" (23 Jun); "midcap and smallcap indices are near all-time highs while Nifty and Sensex are still ~8% below" (15 Jul).
- **The index only rises with leadership**: Reliance / HDFC Bank / Bharti / Bajaj must pull, otherwise gains don't stick (23 Jun, 29 Jun). He distinguishes genuine rallies from "index management" — one heavyweight carried late in the day (21 May).
- **Expiry weeks get a special lens**: roll-overs by stock/sector are read as the map of the next series (26 May, 29 Jun).

## 2. Macro inputs & thresholds

- **Crude is input #1 — "the simplest tell on market direction is Brent crude" (18 May).** Working thresholds: refiner break-even **~$85** ("sustained growth only after crude reaches ~$85," 21 May); **$70–75 is a positive** ("we're used to $80+," 19 Jun); **$85 stable = "definitely a negative"** (17 Jul); $100+ = crisis mode (12/18 May).
- **Rupee**: below **94** = psychologically supportive (16 Jun); crossing **96** = "as long as rupee weakness persists, FIIs may not buy aggressively" (15 Jul); a 60-paise one-day fall = panic tell (9 Jul); 3 straight days of strength = FII inflows returning (26 May). He watches forward premiums too (21 May).
- **FII/DII**: reports the daily net numbers; quantifies intensity ("FII selling is intense — almost Rs 400 crore an hour," 3 Jun). **Record FII index shorts are contrarian fuel**: "FIIs in record shorts (~2.6–2.7 lakh contracts)… any small positive trigger could spark a 500–600 pt Nifty rally" (5 Jun).
- **MF/corporate cash as a buffer gauge**: "domestic funds have deployed most cash — MF cash holdings at a 5-year low (a danger if a sudden global crash needs support)" (17 Jul); corporates holding record ~13 lakh crore cash = liquidity hunting opportunity (16 Jun). Retail-dominated ownership (FPI stakes shrinking) = fragile support structure (17 Jul).
- **Monsoon/El Niño is a first-order macro**: "~80% odds on an El Niño effect rather than a rally" (3 Jun); monsoon covering the country = "the big positive" for rural demand/inflation (13 Jul); regional deficits (Telugu states −26%) and reservoir levels tracked (17 Jul). Chains it to FMCG, microfinance stress, hydro (NHPC), fertilizers.
- **Inflation via WPI first**: WPI jumps (8.33% → 9.7% → 9.9% across May–Jul) read as RBI-pressure and market-negative; he tracks index-series revisions and WPI–CPI divergence (18 May, 16 Jun, 15 Jul).
- **Government proactivity is itself a macro variable**: stimulus/reform packages = key positive; "with no state elections for ~10 months, the govt can focus on the economy" (6 May); PM austerity remarks = fear trigger (12 May); fertilizer subsidy >3 lakh crore = fiscal-strain caution (26 May).
- **US/global reads**: Accenture/IBM results drive Indian IT ADRs next day (19 Jun, 15 Jul); AI-trade unwind = potential rotation into "old-school economy" India (3 Jul, 17 Jul); Bitcoin at multi-month lows = risk-aversion signal (5 Jun).
- **Mega-IPOs as a top/drain signal**: huge IPOs historically precede corrections ("Aramco, Reliance Power, Paytm, LIC," 21 May); big weekly IPO supply (>Rs 10,000 cr) subdues the secondary market (13 Jul); SBI Funds' ~Rs 3 lakh crore bids showed where money rushes (17 Jul).
- **Second-order stress tells**: MSME early stress = "a key tell on whether the economy's undercurrent is weakening" (3 Jun); RBI gold sales quantum as a potential "panic trigger" (3 Jun).

## 3. Options / positioning signals

- **PCR band system (his most mechanical rule)**: **~0.7 = positional buy zone; ~1.2–1.3 = book profits; ~1.5 = profit-booking risk zone.** "0.7 is the buying level — take positional trades as PCR nears 0.7; book profits as it nears 1.2" (3 Jun); "PCR 1.28 — bulls in control >1.2; profit-booking risk ~1.5" (3 Jul). **PCR < 1 = "weakness has been generated"** (15 Jul).
- **PCR velocity matters as much as level**: a one-day collapse is a red alert — "the PCR crash from 1.28 to 0.76 in one day is extremely dramatic and bad" (12 May); the 1.43→1.02 drop was pre-flagged as unsustainable (15 Jul).
- **VIX bands**: **<12 = bull comfort**; 12–13 = stabilizing; ~14–15 = caution; **>15 = danger** (15 Jul); ~19 = "danger has begun again" (12 May); a +26% one-day VIX spike = "clear panic/uncertainty" (9 Jul).
- **Max call OI = resistance, max put OI = support**, restated daily with the strikes; *shifts* in OI are the signal (calls unwinding 24,500→24,200 after a fall, 9 Jul; "more put OI than call OI now," 9 Jul).
- **Daily positioning triad**: names with fresh **speculative longs**, fresh **speculative shorts**, and — the one he trusts — **"conviction delivery-based buying"** (every episode). Plus a routine **F&O ban-list check** ("no stocks in the ban list," 6 May).
- **Delivery-vs-volume test for any big mover**: "one must check whether genuine delivery-backed follow-on buying came" — Newgen's 3.5-cr volume had only ~27 lakh delivery, i.e. ~85% speculative (3 Jun).
- **He takes contrarian option trades at extremes** and manages them publicly: the monthly 24,000 contra call bought in weakness (12 May); the 24,000 call that "doubled — 100%-plus returns in three days; those wanting to book at the opening can" (3 Jul).
- **QIP/issue floor prices become the stock's benchmark level**: "check floor prices (which determine moves)" (16 Jun); Belrise QIP ~230.79 as "the likely benchmark price" (15 Jul).

## 4. Stock-evaluation checklist

- **Value vs "fancy" (market affection) are separate axes; you need both.** "Infosys now has value but no fancy" (17 Jul); SBI Cards is the only listed card play "but the market never fancied it — wealth creation doubtful" (5 Jun).
- **Every stock lives in a consolidation band with a named breakout trigger**: Olectra "comes to 1,250–1,240 and runs back to 1,460–1,470… for momentum, buy only after 1,450–1,460 is crossed; for the long term, buy near 1,275–1,280" (13 Jul); Heritage "consolidates 280–350; real activity after it crosses 350" (17 Jul); Tata Tech "if it can cross 800 it won't stop until 1,000" (17 Jul).
- **52-week positioning as triage**: strength near 52-wk highs with results = buy dips only, never chase; a "cheap" stock at 52-wk lows while peers rise = avoid ("Buy Bata footwear, not the share now," 3 Jun). Trend grammar: higher-tops/higher-bottoms vs lower-tops/lower-bottoms.
- **Volume quality**: trust only "sustained 2–3 day volumes forming higher bottoms / higher tops"; avoid "stocks whose volumes appear one day and vanish the next" (12 May).
- **Order book vs execution**: "order-book exuberance ≠ all stocks rising — execution is what matters" (cites Titagarh/Jupiter Wagons fading to 52-wk lows when execution slowed, 9 Jul); NCC: strong book, "key watch is execution timing" (9 Jun).
- **Results forensics**: strip one-off other income (Amara Raja's "flat" quarter once ~Rs 209 cr of insurance claims is removed, 26 May); check operating margins (Data Patterns' 56% OPM = "excellent," 23 Jun); treat concalls/management guidance as primary data (GE Vernova, Yash High Voltage 50–70% growth guide, 21/26 May).
- **Hidden value / SOTP / demerger / subsidiary unlock is a favourite lens**: Vedanta demerger sum-of-the-parts (23 Jun); SBI's "subsidiary IPOs keep lifting SBI's intrinsic value" (9 Jul); Heritage's three subsidiaries the market misses (17 Jul); NTPC disinvestment pipeline (26 May); **old family industrial groups** (Kirloskar, Birla, KCP) re-rating once disputes resolve and a new generation reinvents management (23 Jun).
- **Dividend / FD-alternative bucket**: some stocks are held only for steady yield — NHPC "hold it as a steady dividend player rather than writing it off" (13 Jul); LIC Housing "high-dividend, low-volatility… ~20% medium-to-long-term" (9 Jul); REITs at "10–12% yields, hard to get in uncertain times" (26 May).
- **Niche/one-of-its-kind moat**: Vimta "one-of-its-kind with little competition" (6 May); MosChip "no other such listed name" (21 May) — but moat without market fancy still fails (SBI Cards). He interrogates moats directly: asked the SETL promoters what "unique IP / USP" protects margins in a crowded field (29 Jun).
- **Equity size / face value / float mechanics**: large equity dilutes EPS thrust (Standard Glass, 21 May; Lemon Tree's high PE "due to larger equity/liquidity," 16 Jun); Rs 10 paid-up = split candidate (Amber, 3 Jul); Rs 1 paid-up at Rs 880 = needs study (Sudeep, 3 Jul); low-float/high-price names move violently (Hitachi Energy, 9 Jul).
- **PE is contextual, not absolute**: high PE fine with visible growth (TD Power at ~190 PE = hold with trailing stop, 23 Jun); high PE with no performance = avoid (Jio Financial "PE 100-plus… only Reliance's backing," 15 Jul); low PE supports patient holding (Good Luck, 3 Jun).
- **Entry price is half the trade**: "not-very-bad stocks, but the entry point was extremely wrong" (13 Jul); MTAR buyer "boarded the running bus" (15 Jul); before chasing, he recites the rally's start point (Idea Forge "was at 372 on April 1," 9 Jun).
- **New listings**: no track record = watch, don't buy; in weak tape "avoid new/freshly-listed stocks — stay in time-tested stocks" (18 May); on big IPOs book listing gains, re-enter only at correct valuations (23 Jun).
- **Regulatory-interference discount**: heavily regulated sectors are trading vehicles, not wealth creators — sugar's "regulatory interference too heavy… a trading sector, not wealth-creating" (26 May); OMC/parent-controlled fixed margins (Chennai Petro, 19 Jun).
- **Sector anticipation, not sector chasing**: "think ahead by sector… anticipate the next limelight sector to beat market returns" (monsoon deficit → water-management names, 3 Jul); flags sunrise segments (BESS, data-centre cooling, EMS) before they're consensus.

## 5. Risk & position management

- **Trailing stop is the default tool for every winner**: "book partial profits immediately and trail a stop to secure the rest" (3 Jun); named trailing levels (TD Power "just below 1000," 23 Jun; Deepak Fertilizers 1,475–1,480, 17 Jul). Dual-track stops: tight for momentum holders, wider for medium-term holders of the same stock (Standard Engineering / Capstone, 13 Jul).
- **"Free of cost" (FOC) partial booking**: take out the principal (or book ~25%) and let the remainder run — offered as the standard menu for profitable positions (9 Jun).
- **Tranche accumulation with price ladders**: "start buying in small quantities; can buy aggressively closer to 250" (Viyash, 23 Jun); "start accumulating on dips down to ~13,000" (Apar, 9 Jul).
- **Averaging down is conditional and capped**: "don't average a 10% loss unless the fundamental story changes" (Union Bank, 9 Jul); at most "a partial ~25% averaging is acceptable" if conviction and appetite exist (NCC, 9 Jun); weak names get "exit into rallies rather than holding hopefully" (RVNL, 9 Jun).
- **Never swap a running winner for a new idea**: "Learn to let profits run… interchanging good stocks unnecessarily is pointless; use fresh capital rather than swapping" (Laurus vs CG Power, 16 Jun); don't compare across sectors on price alone (Avantel vs Capston, 17 Jul).
- **Exit-weak-on-rallies is a standing formula**, usually with the rally level named: Birlasoft "exit near ~360" (21 May); MRPL "use a small rally toward ~180 as an exit" (9 Jul); IREDA/HUDCO/DAM Capital "exit on rallies" (17 Jul).
- **Position size scales with regime confidence**: "stay invested, but trade with small positions" (3 Jun); "aggressive buying is not advisable" below the benchmark (12 May); "positional trades without a stop-loss are highly risky" in 5–11% daily-move conditions (17 Jul).
- **Review cadence by bucket**: even 5–10-year holdings get a quarterly audit — "review financial performance every three months" (23 Jun) — while locker stocks (MRF, Honeywell) are "not a share to watch daily… review after a year or two" (3 Jun).
- **Dark-horse sizing = affordable loss**: NCLT names and lottery tickets only with "money you can afford to lose" (9 Jun, 23 Jun).
- **Result-season discipline**: hold stops into results because "any disappointment can correct the stock immediately" (Akums, 13 Jul); repeatedly defers buys until the print (Ipca 18 May; Genus/HPL 3 Jul; Piramal Pharma 13 Jul).
- **Annualize short bursts to force booking**: "20% in a week is a huge annualized return (~1,000%)… book partial profits immediately" (3 Jun).
- Overall temperament: "No need to be afraid, but protect profits" (6 May).

## 6. When he says AVOID / SELL — the rejection tells

- **"The best of times are over"** — his signature terminal verdict: railways/RVNL (9 Jun), Vishal Mega Mart (13 Jul), Tata Elxsi ("I said at 6,000 and 5,000 that the best of the times were over," 15 Jul).
- **Decent business, no wealth creation**: stable-but-fancyless names (SBI Cards, VST, Aptus, HUDCO, D-Mart-type retail) — "steady gains but no scene of creating spectacular wealth" (13 Jul).
- **Cheapness at a 52-wk low with rising competition** = value trap (Bata, Cello — "it could touch even 250," 3 Jun).
- **Thesis built on an unrelated macro or a theme label**: "betting on an unrelated macro tailwind is foolishness" (Cello/China-imports, 3 Jun); blind EV/AI/semiconductor label-chasing "is bubble territory" (13 Jul).
- **Operator/penny/gimmick counters**: IFL a "chaotic, wretched" penny stock "built on bonus/split gimmicks… at best a lottery ticket" (23 Jun); Vintage Coffee an "operator's-mercy stock" (19 Jun); mistaken-identity rushes (Parle Industries, 21 May).
- **Serial result disappointment + fizzling relief rallies**: MapmyIndia/CE Info "relief rallies fizzle out; not a good 2-year investment" (9 Jul); DCX "don't buy without seeing this quarter's results" (3 Jul).
- **Persistent underperformance vs peers is disqualifying even with fine numbers**: SBFC — "when a stock isn't performing, focusing all your attention on it is unnecessary when Shriram Finance and others have given wonderful returns" (15 Jul).
- **News already priced in**: IFCI's NSE angle "already-discounted news… a fully-priced-in event" (17 Jul); HLE Glascoat "fully priced" → route the money to the cheaper quality peer (16 Jun).
- **NCLT/IBC names**: avoid outright, even for trading (9 Jun).
- **Vertical rallies without earnings**: stocks up 100–150% in 30–45 days require "homework on earnings visibility + management commentary before adding" (3 Jul); ~200-PE all-time-high favourites → book partials, don't add (Cupid, 16 Jun).

## 8. Verbal tells & mental models

- **Stance phrases**: "buy on dips / buy on every decline" (default); "stay invested"; "no need to panic… protect profits"; "caution should be the watchword" (9 Jun); "don't get carried away with optimism" (16 Jun).
- **Verdict vocabulary**: "the best of times are over" (terminal sell); "fancy" (market affection as an asset); "dark horse" (optionality bet, affordable-loss sized); "lottery ticket"; "operator's-mercy stock"; "boarded the running bus" (bad entry); "free of cost" (partial booking); "put it in the locker" (untracked compounder); "time-tested stocks" (flight to quality in weak tape); "new times" (regime breakout); "digest the rally… like a buffet."
- **Mental models**:
  - *One number rules the day* — a single index benchmark, a single macro tell (crude), a single stock trigger level. Everything is reduced to a level to watch.
  - *Velocity over level* — one-day swings in PCR/VIX/rupee alarm him more than absolute readings (12 May, 9 Jul).
  - *Proportionality audit* — every big move is graded against what the news "deserved" (9 Jul).
  - *Delivery is truth, volume is noise* — conviction only where deliveries confirm (3 Jun).
  - *Fancy + value* — a stock needs both a cheap/valuable business and market affection; either alone fails.
  - *Anticipate the limelight* — pre-position for the next sector narrative rather than chase the current one (3 Jul).
  - *History rhymes* — Aramco/Paytm/LIC IPO tops, "Treaty of Versailles 2.0" for one-sided peace deals (19 Jun), Covid-package parallels, Reliance Chemotech 2007.
  - *Ownership of picks* — tracks his calls publicly and scores them ("we keep saying — Amara Raja, buy on every decline," 26 May; "almost my stock" — Sai Parenterals, 3 Jul; Netweb "my favourite," 23 Jun), including booking his own option-call wins (3 Jul).
  - *The investor's job is quarterly* — even "forever" holdings face a 3-month results review; holding is active, not passive (23 Jun).

### One-paragraph engine summary (for the bot)

Each morning: (1) fix the Nifty benchmark level and the range; regime = buy-on-dips while price holds above it, flipping to sell-on-rallies only on a volume break; (2) read crude (vs $85), rupee (vs 94/96), FII flow + shorts, WPI, monsoon, and yesterday's US/ADR prints; (3) read PCR (0.7 buy / 1.2–1.3 book / <1 weak, watch one-day velocity), VIX band (<12 comfort / >15 danger), max call/put OI as the range walls, the speculative-long/short/delivery triad, and the ban list; (4) for any stock: place it in its consolidation band and 52-week context, demand delivery-backed volume, strip one-offs from results, check execution vs order book, float/face-value mechanics, promoter quality, hidden/SOTP value, and whether the market "fancies" it; (5) act in tranches on dips toward the band floor, chase nothing, trail stops on all winners, book partials to go free-of-cost, never average losers >10% without a changed story, and exit weak names into named rally levels; (6) refuse penny/operator counters, NCLT names, priced-in news, theme-label bets, and "best of times are over" stories.
