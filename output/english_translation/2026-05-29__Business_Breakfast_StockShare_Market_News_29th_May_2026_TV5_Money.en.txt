Global cues, however, are positive. Korean and Taiwanese markets are both extending gains. These two markets have a special feature — every day we point out that they are led by AI-related/tech stocks. Taiwan's market cap is now almost 5 trillion dollars and South Korea's about 4.8 trillion dollars. In Taiwan, companies like TSMC, MediaTek, Delta Electronics, Hon Hai (Foxconn), Hanwha-type precision-industry and AC technology names are commanding extraordinary market caps. TSMC alone is almost 45% of Taiwan's market cap, with a market cap near 1.9 trillion dollars. Korea is similar — among the top five South Korean stocks, Samsung Electronics has a ~1.1 trillion dollar market cap; SK Hynix (the memory/HBM chip maker we discussed Wednesday morning) is almost 1.1 trillion dollars; SK Square ~108 billion, plus Hyundai Motor, LG Electro-Mechanics and similar top-cap names. So South Korean and Taiwanese markets this morning are again led by tech stocks, because last night the Nasdaq closed at another record high. The Nasdaq run is not stopping anywhere — it's a beautiful rally; people keep saying the AI bull is about to fall, but the momentum has not been reined in, because the AI rally doesn't look like stopping any time soon.

Why? For example, Dell's results came out yesterday — blowout earnings. The stock rose 30% after market hours yesterday because of the guidance they gave. Dell, an old company (desktops, laptops, plus back-end high-end hardware/software), is now in AI servers too. They raised their AI-server revenue guidance for this year from 10 billion dollars to 60 billion dollars — a 6x jump, not just doubling. That tells you AI is not an express that stops any time soon; this express keeps gaining speed and looks set to reach bullet-train speed. Anthropic just did a fund raise of roughly 60-70 billion dollars at a private, unlisted valuation now near 965 billion dollars — almost 1 trillion. Its rival and more senior company, OpenAI, has a market cap of only 860 billion — almost a 100 billion dollar difference, with Anthropic now near the 1-trillion mark. Anthropic says its annual revenue run-rate is now 47 billion dollars (extrapolating last month's revenue 12x to get an annual run rate). Meta too is about to turn its AI subscription into a paid subscription model. A Korean company's 12-layer HBM4(e) memory chip shipments are now completely sold out. So a huge revolution is visible globally; we have fallen behind in it, and there's no use repeating that daily.

Surprisingly, Wipro's ADR rose 18% yesterday because the company struck a deal with ServiceNow — under which ServiceNow will help Wipro increase agentic-AI workflows in its own IT, HR, procurement and cyber-security functions. So our companies have to move forward by leaning on and tying up with American companies. Wipro closed Wednesday near Rs 200/201; let's see if there's a short squeeze in Wipro today. Anyway, India is going to be one of the world's largest AI users — that much we can achieve; everyone here will use AI, but there are no AI-maker companies here. AI has five-six layers, and in none of those layers can we point to a domestic company doing extraordinary work — that's the sad reality. But we are going to be one of the largest AI users in the world; we'll all use it on mobiles, and corporate AI usage will be heavy too.

Which sectors will see the biggest AI impact here? Definitely we are going to see 5 gigawatts of data-centre capacity in the next four-five years. So related plays — our few small server companies, network-technology names, plus electrical-equipment makers, cable companies and gensets — every data centre, small or large, needs gensets, because they cannot afford even a microsecond of power failure; gensets are kept as redundant power capacity. So in such segments we'll see good growth.

The reason, despite the West Asia crisis, is in today's Economic Times: investors have put record money into equity markets over the last few months. We assume valuations are high, yet investors keep coming forward enthusiastically — because earnings are good in a majority of companies.

Second, running your profits is also important, because of global uncertainty there's no other option — the market goes above 24,500 and again we're back. This is not a Warren Buffett market where you take a position and go to sleep — Europe goes 0 to 10 to 100 and back to zero. So investors should be a little vigilant.
