Good morning and welcome to the Business Breakfast show presented by LIC. In a little while CM Revanth Reddy will participate in the Telangana Formation Day celebrations and we'll hear his address; meanwhile let's discuss the markets.

Markets are set to open negative today — the key point — with a roughly 200-point gap-down indicated by Gift Nifty. The main reason is crude: Brent crude touched 98 dollars last night before easing to around 94-95. Today (Tuesday) is a key day with Nifty expiry. US markets closed positive last night as tech stocks led again, trading regardless of Trump's mood swings — in a CNBC/USA interview he said "I don't care" about the US-Iran talks, then on Truth Social shifted again, because the war now is between Lebanon and Israel: Iran is threatening that unless Israel halts its heavy strikes on Lebanon it won't continue talks. Trump alternates between "I don't care" and, an hour or two later, saying Israel and Hezbollah have promised to halt mutual strikes so talks can progress. With his mood swings, crude prices swing too — to 98 last night, back near 94.62/95 by morning. With crude elevated, markets may come down today; Nifty closed near 23,382 yesterday, so a ~200-point gap-down means it may open near 23,180.

Markets are getting weaker by the day — from 24,300 we wanted 23,800 to hold, and now we're at 23,100; about a 700-point fall over the last three days (370, 165, ~200 today at open). Indian markets are clearly underperforming again. South Korea reached 5.04 trillion dollars and is now the sixth-largest equity market globally; India has slipped to about seventh/eighth (we were in the top five until recently), with Taiwan and Korea moving ahead, led by AI stocks (Samsung Electronics, SK Hynix). In Korea's 5-trillion-dollar market, Samsung contributes ~1.5 trillion and SK Hynix ~1.1 trillion — more than half from two companies. We don't have such companies; unfortunately we lack even half-trillion-dollar companies, though many do have 100-billion-dollar-plus market caps. We chant "Mera Bharat Mahan" but fail to demonstrate the strength; other countries are moving toward an economic revolution. Our economy is fine but markets aren't performing — reasons include FII selling and a weak tech-sector contribution.

Why say the economy is fine? Statistics are the best proof. Today's Business Line, page one (top-left): "Growth indicators in green." Gross GST collections rose 3.2% in May to 1.94 lakh crore (lower than April but up year-on-year from 1.88 lakh crore). PMI manufacturing was 57.6 (eased to ~55), but IIP data is still awaited. UPI daily volume averages 761 million transactions. Passenger-vehicle/auto sales: every company except one or two (like Force Motors) announced good numbers — Ather Energy +100% (small EV base), Atul Auto +31%, TVS Motors +31%, Bajaj Auto +21%, Hero Moto +14%, Eicher +15%, Tata Motors PVs +42% YoY, Maruti +35%, M&M +20%, Tata Motors CVs +17%; Escorts, VST Tillers, etc. Only Force Motors fell ~15%. Despite inflation, a weak rupee and high crude, May retail auto sales did not decline — a notable positive. So markets are underperforming mainly due to FII selling — yesterday they sold 7,850 crore across cash and futures.

Despite heavy institutional selling, DIIs are buying — net institutional buying in cash was positive (DIIs +5,109 cr, FIIs −3,912 cr, ~1,200 cr net buying). But overall sentiment stays negative because FIIs sell index stocks — IT pack, banks, FMCG, in large caps — which keeps dragging the index down. Yesterday's 23,380, today ~23,180 at open; expiry factors also at play. The market structure has clearly been punctured; whether that puncture can be repaired and the market climb back is wait-and-watch. FII shorts have also risen sharply — from 1.53 lakh contracts on May 26 to 2.23 lakh now (70,000 short contracts added in just four sessions) — not a good sign. The advance-decline ratio was very positive for two months (April average 1.56, May ~1.03, near 1:1); yesterday we saw negative breadth for the first time — if it continues, selling could spread to mid/small caps too. Be careful about June: over the last 10 years the June series gave positive returns six times, averaging ~4% over the last five years; whether it repeats depends on the monsoon (El Niño being talked about; rainfall may be ~90% of average). In such conditions FMCG and rate-sensitive sectors like banks may underperform because inflation is high; only IT is supporting for a couple of days. HPE (Hewlett Packard Enterprise) results were very encouraging — revenue 9.3 billion dollars, up 18% YoY, with strong networking growth and raised EPS/free-cash-flow guidance — so the IT pack outperformed in the US and may do so here too. But when IT outperforms, the overall market underperforms; IT outperformance doesn't help Nifty much — Nifty needs banks/financials to outperform to move up.

Two US points: Nvidia closed strong on news of an advanced PC chip matched to Windows OS; and Anthropic filed its IPO draft prospectus, eager to list ahead of OpenAI — intense competition between the two (OpenAI promotes ChatGPT; Anthropic introduced the remarkable Claude model that has amazed the world).
