Whatever the reason, our Nifty/Sensex/Bank Nifty are severely underperforming world markets. In May, Nifty fell ~2%, but the Dow rose 3%, S&P 500 +5%, Nasdaq +8%. Some Asian markets are in an inexplicable bull run — Korea's Kospi +26% in May, Nikkei +12%, Taiwan +12%; the MSCI Asia-Pacific index +8.3%. So our −1.9% in May is a severe underperformance, the worst relative to global markets in two decades. Reasons: FII selling, rupee weakness (which FII selling worsens), crude/war effect, and now rainfall worries (El Niño; ~90% of average rainfall expected).

Would cutting taxes on FIIs / capital-gains relief improve sentiment and stop the selling? Can't say — the government must decide soon; if it doesn't, more damage is likely. Two things are clear: (1) Q4 earnings were mostly good — no big complaint there; the complaints are about elevated crude's bad effect on the economy and possibly lower rainfall. Q1 may not be as good (crude/war/supply-chain effects). (2) Mid/small caps still see stock-specific action — the mid-cap index is near its all-time high and continues to outperform, while Nifty/Sensex/Bank Nifty show no enthusiasm.

For the last two-three months it's the same stocks — Hitachi Energy, BSE, MTAR Technologies, HFCL (Himachal Futuristic), Vedanta, cable companies (KEI Industries, Polycab, RR Kabel) and some metals like Vedanta (pre- and post-demerger, both gave great results). So this mid-cap rally is limited to three-four sectors — Pharma, Capital Market, MNC Energy (Hitachi Energy, GE Vernova, Siemens Energy) and some Industrials. The rally keeps circling these. Worldwide the AI trade dominates — semiconductors, hyperscalers (Meta, Amazon, Microsoft), Nvidia, TSMC, SK Hynix. Here, specific sectors and stocks. In pharma we saw good moves — fundamentally strong names (Laurus Labs etc.) near 52-week highs; even a name like OAD/Ocard had stock-specific news (its product "Zenix" got a US FDA approval — a ~$2bn opportunity). Where news drives a sector with tailwinds (energy), the market focuses there. In June, we have AI plays here too — Netweb Technologies traded strong even on Friday's weak day, as management talked up the AI theme on the conference call; it's perhaps the only listed pure-AI-theme player (some like ETV work in cloud infra). Some mid-cap IT (Mphasis, Coforge, Oracle Financial Services) saw good up-moves. Other sectors may perform in coming days, but crude must fall and the war impact fully recede for the rally to broaden so the majority of sectors participate.
