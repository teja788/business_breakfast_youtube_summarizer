## Global backdrop
- **Positive cues from the US overnight**; **GIFT Nifty indicated a positive opening**.
- The **"chip trade reverse"** continued: **Korea down 7%** overnight, with selling concentrated in semiconductor names. US money rotated out of chips and into **mega-caps** — **Apple +4%**, closing near a record high, market cap ~**$4.88 trillion**.
- Apple's driver: an **AI agreement in China** (tie-ups with **Alibaba** and **Baidu**) to expand its iPhone market. Also rewarded for staying out of the data-centre capex race and sticking to careful capital allocation.
- Chip carnage: **AMD -3.5%**, **Intel -4.4%**, **SK Hynix ADR -9%**, **SanDisk -8%**, **Micron -8%**, **Western Digital -9%**.
- Why it matters here: when the **"anti-AI trade"** runs, **Indian IT stocks rise** (FII and domestic institutional buying rotates in). A **Korea-India inverse relationship** is settling in, replacing the old China-India one — a fund-flow bubble built in Korea's two dominating stocks (**SK Hynix, Samsung Electronics**). **Korea trades at a 6-7 PE** vs **Nifty 20-plus**.
- **Brent crude $85.8**, firm; West Asia "simmering with no signs of subsiding".
- **China posted its slowest growth in three years** — read as a positive for India as the fastest-growing large economy.
