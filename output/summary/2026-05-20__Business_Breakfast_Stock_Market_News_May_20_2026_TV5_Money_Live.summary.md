## Global backdrop
- **Crude near $110–112/bbl**, driven by the unresolved **West Asian / Iran crisis**. Mixed signals: Trump and VP **JD Vance** talk of "fruitful" talks, but Iran isn't committing; **NATO** now says it may escort ships through the **Strait of Hormuz** by **early July** if it isn't reopened.
- **US debt at $40 trillion** — roughly equal to the rest of the world's combined. **Bond yields surging**: 5-yr **~4.32%**, 10-yr **4.66%** (highest since Jan 2025), 30-yr **~5.18%** (a **22-year high**, back to 2007 GFC levels). A move to 6% would be disastrous for global equities (seen as low-probability).
- **FPIs exiting most emerging markets** (Korea, Japan, Taiwan) on rising US yields; **not selling China** (AI/semiconductor exposure). Anchor frames rising yields + strong dollar + weak EM currencies as the worst combination for economies like India.
