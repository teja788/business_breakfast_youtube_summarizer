## Featured IPO — NephroPlus Health Services (guest: promoter Vikram Uppala, MD/CEO)
- Hyderabad-based, **India's & Asia's largest dialysis network**; 500+ clinics in 288 towns; serves ~**10% of India's ~3.25 lakh dialysis patients (~32,000 patients)**; also Philippines, Uzbekistan, Nepal.
- **Rs 871 cr IPO** (OFS Rs 517 cr + fresh Rs 353 cr); band **Rs 438-460**; min 32 shares (Rs 14,720); **lists Dec 17**; ~14% subscribed on day 1.
- Financials: FY23 **loss Rs 11.7 cr** → FY24 **profit Rs 35 cr** → FY25 **profit Rs 67 cr**; EBITDA margin ~20-23%; ~20% CAGR for 15 years; PAT growing ~90-95% YoY, revenue 30%+.
- **Promoter Vikram selling ZERO shares** in OFS; sellers are PE/VC (Bessemer, IFC, Quadria, Edorras, etc.), each trimming ~3% of holding — cost bases ~Rs 50-90 (promoter ~Rs 50-60, sitting on ~8-9x).
- Three models: private-hospital tie-ups (revenue-share, 272 clinics), PPP with state governments (176 clinics, 4 contracts in 15 yrs, ~100% renewal), and out-patient standalone (~25 clinics, not yet widely recognised by states).
- **Debt-free** on net basis (~Rs 200 cr gross debt vs ~Rs 200 cr near-cash); Rs 135-136 cr of working-capital debt to be repaid from proceeds; Rs 129 cr fresh issue funds 50-60 new clinics/yr for 2.5 yrs.
- Cost edge from **global procurement, optimal consumption, contract manufacturing**; India price point ~Rs 1,700/session (world's lowest); Philippines ~USD 10 (~Rs 9,500-9,700). ROCE ~24% (H1 FY26), factors in receivable/working-capital cost; receivable days ~110-120.
- Pricing: promoter defends **22x EV/EBITDA** vs peer set 27-54x; concedes trailing P/E ~69x. Both anchor and Kutumba Rao flagged pricing as **a bit high** (Rs 8 face value, only just turned profitable).
