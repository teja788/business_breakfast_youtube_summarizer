## Global backdrop
- **GIFT Nifty** indicated a **gap-up of ~150–180 points** Monday (it had shown 300+ over the weekend before cooling off). Friday's cash session had risen ~350 points.
- **US tariff uncertainty returned.** The **US Supreme Court** ruled Friday that Trump cannot use **IEEPA** (International Emergency Economic Powers Act) to impose his blanket tariffs — a ~10-month-delayed ruling.
- Trump is **routing around it**: invoking **Section 122** from **24 February**, raising the minimum tariff from **10% to 15% on all countries** (India, China, Russia, etc.). Section 122 is valid **only 150 days** (ordinance-like); beyond that he needs another section (**232 / 301 / Section 338 of the 1930 Tariff Act**, which could allow up to ~50%) or a Congress vote.
- **Effective tariff rate** fell from **16%** (pre-judgment) to **~13.7%** post the 15% move (~2.3% lower). Countries previously at 10% (Australia, UK, Singapore, UAE) and the UK's **0% deal rate** now move to **15%** — flattening the **level playing field** for India.
- **India–US interim trade pact meeting deferred** (today's headline). India had braced to pay **18%**; whether it becomes 15% or 18% is unresolved (final deal possibly April–May). Treasury Secretary **Scott Bessent** flagged further tools; ~**$175bn** in collected tariffs may face refund-related court action.
- **Gold ~$5,100; silver ~$87** (off ~90) — rising on renewed trade and geopolitical (Iran–US war) tension.
