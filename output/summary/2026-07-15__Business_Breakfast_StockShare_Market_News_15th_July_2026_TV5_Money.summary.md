## Global backdrop
- **GIFT Nifty indicated a mildly positive open** after yesterday's losses.
- **"TACO Tuesday"** (Trump Again Chickens Out): Trump announced a **20% toll tax on cargo transiting the Strait of Hormuz** on Monday, then **scrapped it within 24 hours**. Substitute demand: Gulf states should instead make **billions of dollars of investment into the US** — framed as his way of justifying the war spend to his own public.
- War posture: **"attacks will continue until I say enough"**, and **"I will save the energy targets for last"** — Iranian refineries/exploration assets spared for now, which is the market's temporary relief.
- **Brent crude cooled to $85-86** (from ~$89 the other day). View: a return to **$120/bbl looks unlikely** unless things worsen; **$89-90 is the realistic ceiling** for now — but nothing is predictable while Trump is around.
- **Iran says the MoU with the US is cancelled** — Tehran waited until the Khamenei funeral rites were done and has now turned aggressive, tit for tat.
- **US June CPI fell unexpectedly to ~3.5%** (from 4.2%) → **July rate-hike expectations receded**, though it's only one data point.
- **Nasdaq +0.9%**, **S&P 500 +0.38%** overnight. But **IBM crashed 25%** — its worst fall in roughly 50 years — after saying clients are spending on **servers/data centres, not software**. Read-across: **software demand is very soft**.
