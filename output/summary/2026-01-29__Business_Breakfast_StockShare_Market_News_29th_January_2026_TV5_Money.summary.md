## Global backdrop
- **US markets closed roughly flat.** Tech-major earnings landed after hours: **Microsoft** numbers were in line but the stock reacted negatively; **Meta** and **IBM** traded positively post-results.
- **Gold and silver are the big story.** Gold crossed **$5,600** (a record; vs ~$1,000 in 2008 around the GFC). Silver near **$118–120**. In India ~10g gold seen near **Rs 65,000** yesterday, likely to open **Rs 70,000–75,000**; MCX silver likely to open **above Rs 4 lakh/kg**.
- **Gold's ascent is accelerating:** $3,500→$4,000 took 118 days, $4,000→$4,500 took 56 days, $4,500→$5,000 took 14 days, $5,000→$5,500 just 2 days. Driver is **central-bank accumulation** since Russia's 2022 invasion of Ukraine (and seizure of ~300t of reserves), not retail.
- **Multi-asset fund gold/silver exposure** flagged: Nippon ~**10.05%** gold; DSP **10.38%**; Aditya Birla Sun Life **9.68%**; Kotak only **5.38%** gold but ~**13–14%** silver (highest combined); ICICI Pru almost no silver (0.35%), gold 7.16%.
- Powell held rates (status quo expected); his commentary stressed Fed independence and warned successors to "stay out of elected politics" after the **Lisa Cook** removal case (Supreme Court negated Trump's order). Read as **dollar-weakness signal** — dollar at 4–5 year lows vs most currencies (though strong vs INR).
- Markets seen as hostage to **"Trump tantrums"** (Greenland "TACO trade", possible US government shutdown).

## Indian markets
- **Nifty +~300 pts over two sessions** (~167 pts yesterday) — partly short-covering, partly pre-Budget positioning. First close above previous day's high in ~25–27 days after 15 weak sessions.
- **Breadth strong:** ~400 of Nifty 500 closed positive yesterday; broad-based with mid/small-caps and beaten-down names recovering; delivery volumes up.
- **Flows turning:** FIIs swung to ~**+Rs 500 cr** (selling paused); DIIs bought ~**Rs 3,300 cr**.
- **Key levels (Ramakrishna):** support **25,000–25,160** (200-day EMA), strong put support **25,300**; resistance small at **25,500**, then **25,440** to extend rally. RSI 40.45 improving, VIX cooled to **13.53** (-6.43%), PCR 0.92. Bank Nifty 59,500 call / 59,000 put highest writing; possible move to **60,000**. Today: monthly Sensex options expiry → slight volatility expected. **Rupee opened near record ~91.99/USD**.
- **Budget (Sun 1 Feb) is the central swing factor.** Bull case (market/economy-friendly Budget + US trade deal) → new highs; failure to sustain 25,400–25,600 → toward **24,500/24,000**. Economic Survey due today.
- **Sectors leading:** energy, metals, defence. **Defence Index +8%** over two sessions; BEL was 2nd most-traded Nifty 500 stock, with breakout; HAL/BDL strong.
- **Earnings reactions:** strong from **L&T** (record Q3 order book **Rs 1.35 lakh cr**), GE Vernova (record), Gland Pharma (+8–8.5%), Hindustan Copper (+7%), TVS Motors, Garden Reach, Manorama Industries, Acutaas Chemicals, SJS Enterprises, Cigniti, SBI Cards, Samhi Hotels, Borosil Renewables. Weak: Cochin Shipyard (weak execution), NSDL, Maruti Suzuki (-3%), Somany (flat).
- **Trade/macro view:** EU–India FTA praised as a win-win; US deal possibly within 1–2 weeks; FTAs progressing serially (Mauritius 2021 → UK → EU).
