## Global backdrop
- Global cues **slightly positive** this morning and **GIFT Nifty positive** — but only after yesterday's Trump-driven rout.
- **Trump cancelled the Iran ceasefire** mid-afternoon yesterday (~1:30 pm), calling Iranians "sick people" and saying it's "a waste of time" dealing with Iran. This single statement erased five days of gains: **Nifty fell ~500 points to 23,800** (July series 23,805) and **~Rs 10 lakh crore of market cap evaporated in minutes**, versus an expected move toward 24,500.
- **Crude spiked back above $80** on the news. **Brent $79.16**, **Nymex ~$74.64** — reviving India's oil-import-burden worry after hopes crude would fall to $60 (Saudi discount + Russian/Iranian barrels).
- **US overnight**: Nvidia positive; **AMD, Broadcom** gained on news **Nvidia resumes H200 chip sales to China** (~half a million chips this year). Nasdaq would have closed positive but for the **SK Hynix US ADR listing** (largest ADR listing in history, ~7x subscribed).
- **Kospi +~0.5%** this morning. Theme flagged: "no war and no peace" — world peace has "never been so fragile."
- **IMF macro outlook** out today: India remains **fastest-growing economy** despite a **1% FY27 growth cut**.
