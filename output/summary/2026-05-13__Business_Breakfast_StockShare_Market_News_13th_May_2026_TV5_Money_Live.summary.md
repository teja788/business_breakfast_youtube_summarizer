## Global backdrop
- **US markets** closed lower last night; recovered somewhat from the lows. **Gift Nifty** signaled a slightly positive open.
- Core driver of the turmoil: **crude oil** disruption at the **Strait of Hormuz**. Travel through it is shifting from "free" to "paid," pushing logistics/transport costs up. Kutumba Rao thinks **80–90 dollars/barrel** could be the "new normal" for crude even if the war stops (old Rs 50–60 won't reset).
- **LNG cost** flagged as the next danger — roughly **70 dollars per MWh**; a sharp rise would hit LNG-using industries.
- **Trump visiting China today** — watched as a potential catalyst; any trade deal / tariff announcement could give global markets some relief.
- "AI fear" (intangible) plus tangible-asset monopolies (crude) seen as a double squeeze on countries like India with little presence in either.

## Indian markets
- **Yesterday: Rs 12 lakh crore (~115 billion dollars)** of wealth wiped out; market down ~**2%**.
- Breadth brutal: **435 advances vs ~2,700 declines (1:6)**. Nifty closed below **23,300** and below the **10/20/50/200-DMA** — described as the **steepest fall this financial year**.
- **Mid- and small-caps fell more than benchmarks** — complacency unwinding after extreme outperformance (15 pages of 52-week highs, none of lows earlier).
- **Inflation**: March **3.4%**, April **~3.48%/3.08%** — still "under control," helped by petrol/diesel prices not being raised; gold/silver and food added pressure.
- **Sectors**: Nifty Bank, Nifty IT, Reliance, gold & jewellery, consumption basket all hit; **only pharma** defensive. **Nifty IT index in "three-year" zone**; OpenAI (now offering enterprise software services, tied up with **Infosys** while also becoming a competitor) re-spooking IT.
- IT companies returned **Rs 1.3 trillion (Rs 1.30 lakh crore)** to investors via buybacks last FY despite AI headwinds.

## Policy / events
- **Gold & silver import duty raised to 15%** (from 6%) — ~9% duty + 1% cess. Gold up ~**Rs 12 lakh/kg** (~Rs 12,000/tola), silver up ~**Rs 22,000/kg**.
- **PM (Sunday, Hyderabad)** called for austerity: cut energy use, use public transport, postpone gold buying for a year, defer foreign travel/destination weddings — advisory, not bans.
- **Fuel price hikes** likely soon (oil marketing companies losing crores/day).
- **MSCI Standard Index** rejig: out — Hyundai, RVNL, Jubilant Foodworks, Kalyan Jewellers; in — Federal Bank, MCX(?), Indian Bank.
- Kutumba Rao expects a **gold policy** from the government — notes PSU banks' ~Rs 1.9 lakh crore profit leans heavily on a ~Rs 13 lakh crore gold-loan book (~Rs 35–40k crore profit), with the interest burden falling on semi-urban/rural borrowers.
