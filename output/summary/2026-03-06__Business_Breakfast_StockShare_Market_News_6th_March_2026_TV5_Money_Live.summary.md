## Global backdrop
- **Geopolitics dominate.** The **Strait of Hormuz** is effectively frozen — no vessel traffic — and may stay disrupted for weeks, breaking supply chains and logistics. Framed as a Trump–Netanyahu-driven escalation now drawing in Qatar, Bahrain and others; the war is in its **7th day**.
- **Crude:** Brent hit **86.28** intraday yesterday, now near **84.15**. A mid-session rumour (via Sky News Arabia) that Iran's deputy foreign minister offered to pause Iran's nuclear program under conditions briefly pushed crude to **82** and lifted equities, before being denied.
- **Positive for India:** The US granted a **temporary 30-day waiver allowing Russian oil purchases**, easing pressure on refiners (Reliance, HPCL, BPCL). India is thought to hold ~25 days of reserves and aims to build 1-2 months' worth. Russia has also agreed to supply somewhat cheaper.
- **One theory** floated: the war is really aimed at **China** — choke Iranian oil (China buys ~90% of it) to weaken China, which holds leverage via **rare-earth magnets / critical minerals**. Renewed talk of US controls on **advanced AI chips**.
- US Supreme Court reportedly struck down Trump's tariff orders and ordered refunds — now largely forgotten amid the war. China growth projected at **4.5-5%**, lowest in decades, a drag on global commodity prices.

## Trading takeaways
- War-time: hour-to-hour swings; intraday only, with strict stop-losses and hedges. **Do not carry weekend positions**; if Nifty closes below **24,500**, avoid fresh positions until Monday.
- Investors: basket approach, lean toward domestic-focused / resilient names (e.g. PSUs); don't be tempted by price into weaker stocks. Precious metals not rallying despite uncertainty because gold/silver trade in dollars and the dollar is strong — a rally needs dollar weakness; risky to trade short-term.
- Markets react strongly to geopolitics but eventually move past it ("speed-breaker" phase); new highs likely, but maybe only after 3-6-9 months.
