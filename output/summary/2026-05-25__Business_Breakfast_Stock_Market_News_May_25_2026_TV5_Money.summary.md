### Sai Parenterals (Anil Kumar interview)
- First post-listing results; first quarter of consolidation with **Newmed** (Australian marketing co, acquired 12 Nov).
- **Consolidated FY25-26**: income **Rs 381 cr (+133%)**; EBITDA **Rs 19.9 → 11.9 cr (-40%)**; PAT ~flat; PAT margin **8.8% → 3.7%**. The drop is a one-off consolidation/merger effect (Newmed runs ~10% EBITDA vs Sai standalone ~22%); not comparable YoY since no Newmed last year.
- **Standalone FY26**: revenue **Rs 162 cr (+31%)**, EBIT **Rs 18.8 cr (+31%)**, PAT **Rs 16.8 cr (+63%)**, PAT margin **8.3% → 10.4%**; EBITDA held ~22%.
- **Backward integration**: Newmed's manufacturing being shifted in-house (captive supply to Australia) → margins to rise toward **21–22%** vs current 10%. Margin upside expected from **Q1/Q2**.
- **Capex Rs 440 cr** total (Rs 303 cr Australia — 80% done, balance by Sep 2026; Rs 130–138 cr India — done by December), to complete before March 2027; revenue impact in **FY28/FY29**. Best quarters Q3/Q4; Q1/Q2 lean.
- **Guidance**: **Rs 750 cr** topline (conservative) and **~17% EBITDA margin** for the year; hopes for a four-figure topline. 88 product dossiers added in regulated/emerging markets, 67 in pipeline, all to be commercialized FY27.
- Balance-sheet note: **Rs 118 cr "other financial liabilities"** is the OFS amount paid 2 April (cash shown but OFS settled post 31 March) — a balancing entry. Higher inventory (Dec–Feb buying) is cushioning raw-material price rises for the next two quarters.
