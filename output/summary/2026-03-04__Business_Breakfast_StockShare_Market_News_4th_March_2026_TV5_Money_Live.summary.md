## Global backdrop
- **War-driven risk-off.** Israel + US "Operation Roaring Lion" against **Iran** has widened — Iran is now reportedly striking targets across **Dubai, Saudi, Qatar**, and US embassies (incl. Pakistan) have closed/burned. Feared to become a **prolonged, multi-dimensional war**.
- **Strait of Hormuz** was closed by Iran, snarling crude/LNG tankers. Brent spiked above **$85**, cooling to **~$82-84** during the show after US pledged tanker insurance (via "USDF") and naval escorts — which the desk called largely a "sham."
- **Crude route dependence:** ~20% of global crude transits Hormuz; **India ~45-50%**, Japan 70-80%, S. Korea 65-70%, Taiwan 60%, China 40-50% — India among the highest-risk.
- **Global equities red.** South Korea **KOSPI hit a 10% circuit** (still +26% YTD after the drop); Taiwan +18%, **Nikkei +9%**, Shanghai +4% YTD. US fell **~1,200 pts** intraday, closed ~400 negative; Asia down 2-4% this morning.
- **Gold** slipped from **$5400 to ~$5100** — even safe havens saw selling. **Dollar strength** is driving markets; "cash is king" regime risk if gold breaks down (margin calls, central-bank reserve drawdowns).
