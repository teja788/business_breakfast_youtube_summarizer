## Global backdrop
- Global cues **clearly positive** on Thursday morning: **Japanese index +~5%**, **Taiwan index +~2%**, gains across Asia. But **GIFT Nifty pointed to a marginally negative/flat open** after yesterday's late surge.

- **US overnight**: **record closes in both S&P 500 and Nasdaq**; AI/chip names ran again on strong **AMD** results and Gulf-resolution hopes. **SoftBank +16%** in Japan on its AI/semiconductor exposure.

Asia's two trillion-dollar companies (**TSMC, Samsung**) are both tech. India market cap **~$4.8-4.9tn** vs **Taiwan $4.61tn**, **South Korea $4.18tn** — "barely a striking distance." Call for the government to be proactive (semiconductor mission pari-passu 50% funding; help Indian firms make global takeovers).
- **Careers aside**: ET front page — **AI-skill pay premium of 30-60%** over non-AI roles; **GCCs pay Rs 58-60 lakh** for AI/ML roles vs **~Rs 5-12 lakh** entry-level at Infosys/TCS. Advice to students: **avoid traditional CSE/IT courses** (outdated syllabus); take electronics, mechanical, or pure-AI courses.
