## Global backdrop
- **War situation continues.** Global equity markets trading very negatively; **Gift Nifty pointing to a 150-160 point gap-down** (later indicated ~270 down).
- **Iran has reportedly closed the Strait of Hormuz** — Supreme Leader announced it, and Iran is said to have planted **more than a dozen underwater mines**, halting ship/vessel movement. US Treasury Secretary **Scott Bessent** said the US would escort vessels "when militarily possible" — the show treats this as not realistically doable.
- **Crude oil above $100** — at **$101.05**; closed above $100 for the first time since **2022**. Touched **$119/$119.5** intraday recently before snapping back to $90. Goldman Sachs and others forecast **$150**; 2008 GFC peak was **$147.5**; Iran is threatening **$200**.
- **Russia is a beneficiary** (sanctions effectively lifted on buyers of Russian oil; US now supplying Iran with arms previously meant for Ukraine; can sell oil at a premium). **China and India are the losers** — China buys ~**90%** of Iran's crude; India already seeing **LPG/gas supply** strain.
- **US tariffs** reimposed against **16 major countries** (India, China, EU, Korea, Vietnam, Taiwan, Bangladesh, etc.); a **global recession** is now seen as "on the cards." US fuel rates **$2.60-$3.90/gallon** in some states.
