## Global backdrop
- **Risk-off / global sell-off.** US closed lower overnight: **S&P 500 -2.1%**, **Nasdaq -2.39%**. European markets also sold off. CBOE **VIX at its highest since November 2025**.
- **Japanese bond bloodbath.** A **snap election** is due next month in Japan; both ruling and opposition parties promised **fiscal expansion + tax cuts**. 30Y/40Y JGB yields jumped **25–30 bps**; the Finance Minister's plea to calm markets didn't help.
- **Spillover to US Treasuries.** US 10Y yield rose **0.07% to 4.29%**. A **Danish pension fund** said it will **exit US Treasuries by month-end**; the EU is preparing retaliatory tariffs — "country by country" hardening against the US.
- **Trump as catalyst.** Took the **Greenland** issue aggressively, warned Denmark; speaks at **Davos / WEF at 6:30pm IST today** — markets watching. The disruption of the "world order" is the framing.
- **Safe havens bid.** **Gold ~$4,800**, **silver ~$94–95**. The presenter flagged these as the main portfolio support right now.
- **US SCOTUS tariff (IEEPA) ruling postponed to February 20** (~1 month). Read as: court unlikely to rule strongly against the tariffs.
