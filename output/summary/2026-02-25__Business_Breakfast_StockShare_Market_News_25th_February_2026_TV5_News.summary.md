# Summary — Business Breakfast, 25 February 2026 (TV5 News)

## Global backdrop
- US markets bounced overnight: **S&P 500 +0.7%**, **Nasdaq +1.05%**, with a tech-sector bounce-back. Gift Nifty pointed to a positive open after yesterday's heavy fall.
- **Trump's State of the Union address** dominated the morning. He twice cited the stock market (boasting **53 all-time highs since his election**), defended tariffs ("taught countries that ripped us off a lesson"). The team read this as tariff policy continuing.
- New Trump announcement flagged as positive: a **"rate-payer protection pledge"** for AI data centers — big tech / data-center owners should set up their own power generation so retail/household power bills don't rise.
- **The AI-vs-software fear** is the central theme. Citrini's report (AI will cause huge disruption) drove the Indian IT index to a **30-month low** (Financial Express headline). **Anthropic/Claude** tools are seen as the disruptor — e.g., Anthropic's pledge to modernize **COBOL** (which still runs ~80% of in-person US credit-card and ~95% of US ATM transactions) sank **IBM -13%**.
- Counterpoint: an **HSBC report** rebutted Citrini — "software is already eating AI"; AI spreads only via software, so software won't go to zero.
- Currencies: rupee weakened to **~90.95** (RBI support kept it just below 91); Chinese yuan strengthening vs dollar. Gold steady/"resilient" (cited above ~$5,200/oz), warned against shorting given fragile geopolitics (possible Iran attack).
- **US imposed ~120% import duty on solar imports** — hit Indian solar exporters: **Waaree Energies, Premier Energies, Vikram Solar all ~10% down** at open.

## Indian markets
- Yesterday: heavy fall, **Nifty -288 pts (~-1.1%)**; **IT index -5%**, individual IT stocks down 6-8% (Persistent, Coforge crashed). Nifty IT logged its **worst monthly fall in ~23 years** (since dot-com 2000-02).
- Selling was **retail-driven panic**, not institutional: **FIIs net-sold only ~Rs 100 cr**, **DIIs bought ~Rs 3,000 cr**. TCS -44% and Infosys -46% from peaks, yet long-term holders mostly stayed invested.
- Breadth very weak: **~2,900 decliners vs 1,344 advancers**; stripping penny/no-volume names, fewer than ~300 active stocks actually rose.
- 18-month context: Nifty stuck around **25,400** since Sept 2024; Feb saw mild mid/small-cap outperformance (+2-3%) before the AI scare resumed.
- **Today's open: positive** — Nifty +120-143 pts, Sensex +400, **IT index +2.7%** on short-covering (huge short positions had built up). Power Grid +1.2% (Cabinet raised its subsidy investment from Rs 5,000 cr to Rs 7,500 cr). Broad gains across IT, metals, realty, FMCG, defence, energy.
- Corporate: **Tata Sons** board (esp. Noel Tata) weighing conditions for **Chandrasekaran's third term** as chairman — watch Tata group stocks. **IRFC offer-for-sale opens today at Rs 104** (5% discount).
