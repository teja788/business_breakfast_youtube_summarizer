## Global backdrop
- **Venezuela** dominated the open: the US attacked, removed the country's president (and his wife) to America. Vasanth frames it as a move for control of resources, not "narco-terrorism."
- Venezuela holds the **world's largest crude reserves (~300 billion barrels)**; Saudi second at **267 billion**, Iran **209 billion**. It also holds **161 metric tonnes of gold (~$22.55 billion)**, the largest in Latin America.
- Venezuelan output has collapsed from **3 million bpd to under 1 million bpd**; **China** is the near-sole buyer, so the direct crude-price impact is small.
- Net read for India: **positive** — "drill, baby, drill" in Venezuela points to more supply, easing oil-price risk and reducing the need for Russian oil.
