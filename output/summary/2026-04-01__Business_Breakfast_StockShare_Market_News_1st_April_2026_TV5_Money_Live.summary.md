# Summary — Business Breakfast, 1 April 2026 (TV5 Money)
# https://youtu.be/VwB7ZJs-GMY

## Global backdrop
- **First day of FY2026-27.** A Trump statement that the US will withdraw from the Iran war "in two or three weeks" lifted global markets: US equities closed very strong (S&P's best day of 2026, though still its worst quarter in 4 years), crude fell from ~$112 to ~$105.
- Trump said the US has "achieved its objective" in Iran, won't seek regime change, and will pull back "without reopening the Strait of Hormuz." For the first time Iran's side signalled willingness to cease fire if Israel/US give firm guarantees. China and Pakistan floated a 5-point peace plan. Trump to address the nation (6:30am IST) on the war.
- Caveat: even if the war ends, after-effects will be "pretty big" — Iran is expected to start charging tolls (Rs 15-20 cr per large ship) at the Strait of Hormuz; global rates uncertain for 5-6 months; US midterms ahead.

## Indian markets
- **Gap-up opening** (~1.5% for Nifty vs 3-4% for Korea/Japan). At open: Nifty +~500-570, Sensex +~1,600-1,800, **Bank Nifty +~2.6% (slight outperformer)**. 49 of 50 Nifty stocks up (only ONGC down on lower crude). BSE breadth: 2,349 advances vs 335 declines.
- **FY26 was bad:** Nifty -5% for the year, with March alone -11.5% wiping out the year's gains; rupee -9.88% (to ~95). FIIs sold ~Rs 1.6 lakh cr (Rs 11,000 cr on Mar 30 alone); DIIs bought ~Rs 8.5 lakh cr (Rs 14,815 cr on Mar 30) — that support is the only reason the index isn't near 17,000-18,000.
- **Historical hope:** since 2004, every year the Nifty fell, it rose the next year (FY09 -36% → FY10 +73.7%; FY20 -26% → FY21 +70%). April has been positive in 9 of last 10 years.
- IPO market raised a record Rs 1.79 lakh cr in FY26, but 71% of IPOs trade at a loss; Sai Parenterals managed only ~1x subscription.
- Disinflation in food (potatoes -40%, onions -50%, tomatoes -80%, wheat -10%, rice -5-6%) due to lack of exports to the Gulf. ATF prices +15% (only foreign carriers, not domestic — InterGlobe +3-4%).
- HDFC Bank active (~1.25 cr volume, +2.5%) after Atanu Chakraborty's resignation news; could lead a rally.
