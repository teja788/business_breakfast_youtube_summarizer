# Summary — Business Breakfast, 2 April 2026 (TV5 Money)
# https://youtu.be/1Ce40ajVvw4

## Global backdrop
- **Trump's ~6:30 am address wiped out April 1's rally.** He said "Operation Epic Fury" will be intensified over the next **two to three weeks**, that Iran's army and navy are "completely destroyed" and the leadership dead — while simultaneously claiming talks with that same leadership are ongoing, and that the war will end "very soon." The anchor framed the previous day's rally as Trump having made "April Fools" of the whole market.
- **Crude exploded on the statement**: from **~$97-101** to **$104-106** within an hour (**+4-6%**), and Brent was quoted at **$115** by the time the market opened. Kutumba Rao flagged the number most people ignore — the **Dubai spot at ~$124-125**.
- **~3,500 more western troops** reached West Asia; power plants and other Iranian assets are threatened if no deal is struck. No sign of the problem going away.
- **Global economy sliding towards stagflation** — slower growth, higher inflation, higher unemployment. Historic US war spend cited: **$3tn (Iran-Iraq)**, **$2.1tn (Afghanistan)**.
- **AI disruption** running alongside: **Oracle cut ~20% of staff** (**8,000+ shown the door in a single day**); **OpenAI valued at $850bn**. Kutumba Rao's striking datapoint: a year ago **India's total market cap was double Nvidia's — today they are equal**.

## Indian markets
- **Gap-down open.** GIFT Nifty pointed to a **300-450 point** Nifty loss; the market opened near **22,225** (**-450**), **Sensex -1,350**, **Bank Nifty -1,350 (-2.67%, underperforming)**. Pre-open print **22,383**.
- **Breadth collapsed**: NSE **273 advances vs 2,019 declines** — the exact mirror of April 1 (2,700-2,800 advances / 200 declines). In the **Nifty 500 only one stock (Aditya Infotech) was green**; not a single Nifty or Bank Nifty constituent was positive.
- **Losers on the open**: Capri Global **-7%**, InterGlobe Aviation **-4%**, **BPCL** and OMCs on Brent at $115, plus SRF, Bank of Maharashtra, AU Small Finance, Hindustan Copper, Garden Reach, Tata Motors, Anant Raj, Ramkrishna Forgings (**-4% to -6%**). PSU banks, pharma, capital market, defence and financials all red.
- **FII/DII (April 1)**: FIIs sold **Rs 8,331 cr**, DIIs bought only **Rs 7,172 cr** — domestics not absorbing the supply.
- **Rupee**: closed **94.83** on April 1; opened **93.53** (stronger — possibly an RBI decision or pre-**April 10** position covering, per Ramakrishna). Kutumba Rao nonetheless expected a **close above 95** given the crude spike.
- **Macro was the one bright spot**: March **gross GST +8.3% YoY**, **net GST +7.1%** (~**8.4%** including refunds); strong Q4 auto/two-wheeler numbers (Hero, Bajaj Auto, TVS, Tata Motors, Hyundai); good Q4 updates from **Indian Bank** and **South Indian Bank**. Factory expansion slowed slightly.
- **Key levels**: **23,000** must be crossed before the market improves (Kutumba Rao, echoed by Ramakrishna); **22,000** breaking signals "an even more terrible fall". Ramakrishna's near-term line: **22,300** recent swing — losing it opens a further **2-3%** cut (it was already cut on the open). **RSI 37** (up from **~27**, a March-2020-type reading); **VIX -10.32% to 25** — comfort only nearer **18**.
- **Three-day weekend risk**: markets shut Friday for Good Friday; carrying positions to Monday was strongly discouraged.
- **IPO listing**: **Sai Parenterals** (Rs 408 cr raise, band **372-392**, allotted at **392**) listed at 9:55. Subscription: **QIB 1.7x**, **HNI 2.36x**, retail thin; ~**1x overall**. Anchor book **Rs 122 cr** including **Morgan Stanley** and **Kotak Mahindra Life**. A below-issue-price listing was seen as likely.
- **Workshop plug**: viewers told to bring condensed portfolios (**under ~50 stocks**) for review; Kutumba Rao announced he will give his book (**English only**) free to all attendees. A viewer one-liner — "we are in a **Trump market**, worse than a bear market" — was adopted on air.

- **Kutumba Rao (present)** — carried the main market view and most of the viewer Q&A. Thanked by name in the close. (Note: the auto-captions render his honorific "Kutumba garu" as "the family"/"Family Governor" — those passages are his.)
