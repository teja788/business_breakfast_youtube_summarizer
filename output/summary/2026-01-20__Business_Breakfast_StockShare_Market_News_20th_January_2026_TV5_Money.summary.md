## Global backdrop
- **US markets closed** Monday for the **Martin Luther King** holiday; **Asian markets mixed**, **GIFT Nifty** slightly positive.
- **European markets fell heavily** (DAX, CAC) on the **Greenland threat** and talk of **retaliatory EU tariffs worth $108 billion** if the US imposes a **10% tariff on EU goods from February 1**.
- **Trump** grandstanding at **Davos** (set up a "USA House", speech due tomorrow); threatening tariffs over not getting the Nobel Peace Prize. A **US Supreme Court ruling on Trump tariffs** could come any day.
- **Gold** trading above **$4670** (futures **Rs 1,48,100**); **Silver** near **$94.55**, crossed **Rs 3 lakh** in India (~Rs 3,28,000 MCX futures). Both at record highs. **Bitcoin** fell to **92,000**.
- **IMF** reiterated a conservative India growth target of **~7.5%** (5–8% range) — a potential positive for long-only FPIs.

## Indian markets
- **Nifty** very weak: closed **25,585** yesterday, dipped below 25,500 intraday (low **25,494**), forming **lower highs / lower lows**. **RSI ~37** (oversold), **PCR 0.7**.
- **25,500** is the key medium/short-term support; held over the last 4–5 sessions. A decisive break risks a fall to the **200-DMA at ~25,100** and a "dire" scenario. Resistance: **25,900**; nearer-term **25,638–640** (Ramakrishna). Swing support **25,450–25,479**; break → 200–300 point drop.
- **Bank Nifty** relatively stronger (higher low; opened +86 pts) — the only place with some safety for longs.
- **FII selling non-stop** — **Rs 3,263 cr** sold yesterday (20th consecutive negative day); **DIIs bought Rs 4,234 cr**. FPIs have sold **>Rs 2 lakh cr** this FY; FII stake in Indian equities at a historic low of **~17%**. **DII/SIP inflows of Rs 45–50,000 cr/month** are the key cushion.
- **Heavyweights dragging**: **Reliance −10%** this month (Q3 growth below expectations; needs to hold 200-DMA), **HDFC Bank −6.4%**, **Bharti Airtel −4.5%**, ICICI Bank weak.
- **Earnings season weak**: Business Standard headline — **worst earnings growth in 17 quarters**. Banks' revenue and profits down; **CASA ratio** falling (HDFC Bank ~40% → ~30%) as savers chase higher FD/other rates. No big positive surprises.
- **Mid/small-caps already in a bear market** — portfolios down **50–60%**, only ~40–50 resilient names. Today is **expiry/settlement day** → some volatility expected; consolidation likely to continue.
- **Bear-market caution**: both analysts warn that if the **Budget** or **Q3 earnings** disappoint, large-caps could follow into a bear market. Historical analogs cited: **1992–2000** and **2009–2013** long bear phases. Signs present but no confirmation yet.
- **Advice**: **protect capital**, avoid **revenge buying / averaging down**, but **do NOT stop mutual-fund SIPs** — long-term SIP discipline (e.g. Rs 10,000/month for 25 yrs ≈ Rs 1.33 cr at 12–15% avg).
