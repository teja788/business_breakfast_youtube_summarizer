## Global backdrop
- **Major geopolitical shock over the weekend**: a US-Israel strike on Iran, with the anchor reporting the killing of Iran's Supreme Leader **Khamenei**. Iran retaliated with missile strikes on US-aligned Gulf states — **UAE (Dubai/Abu Dhabi), Bahrain, Oman, Qatar, Kuwait**.
- **Strait of Hormuz** in focus — carries ~**20%** of world crude; Iran is reported moving to close it, having already hit ~**3 ships**. A prolonged closure could push **Brent to $90-100**.
- One read: the strike is partly aimed at hurting **China**, which sources ~**9-10%** of its crude (of ~70% total imports) from Iran.
- **Commodities/global equities**: Brent ~**$76-77** (not yet past $80). Gold at a 1-month high **~$5,332/oz**; silver near **$93.5** (peaked ~$95). UAE capital markets **suspended 2 days**; **Hang Seng -2.7%**, Nikkei and Taiwan indices lower. **Dubai airport closed indefinitely**, disrupting India-Gulf flights.
- Historical framing: like Gulf War, Kargil, Russia-Ukraine, last year's Iran-Israel flare-up — markets react negatively first, then recover over 1-2 months as fundamentals reassert.

## Indian markets
- **Gift Nifty** indicated ~160-170 pt gap-down; **pre-open showed Nifty ~-800/-880 pts**, Sensex ~-2,743 pts (~-3.4%), Bank Nifty ~-1,324 pts. **Rupee very weak at ~91.25** vs dollar.
- **Actual open milder than feared**: Nifty opened ~**-1%** / ~245-290 pts; Sensex ~**-1.2%** (-929); Bank Nifty relatively better, defending **60,000**. Heavy selling in **mid/small-caps** (indices ~-2%).
- **Breadth deeply negative**: ~**159 advances vs >1,000 declines** (~1:7); on NSE only **68 stocks** advancing vs ~2,000 declining. **India VIX +14-15%**.
- **Sector moves**: gainers — **ONGC, Oil India** (upstream, crude up), **defence (BDL, BEL)**, **Hindustan Zinc** (silver), **Muthoot Finance** (gold), Sai Life, Hindalco, Sun Pharma. Losers — **OMCs -4-5% (IOC, HPCL)**, IT stocks, tyre/paint (crude raw material), logistics (**Allcargo -7%**), InterGlobe Aviation, Reliance Power/Infra.
- **Macro is supportive**: strong **revised GDP series**, **excellent GST collections**; FIIs turned net buyers (~**Rs 22,000 cr** in Feb) post US-India deal. Domestic funds flush with cash. Key risk flagged: if crude breaks **$80→$100**, India faces a "double whammy" (FPI selling + rupee depreciation + CAD pressure).
- **Note**: tomorrow is a **Holi holiday** — low volumes expected. Today's close is technically pivotal.
