## Global backdrop
- **GIFT Nifty indicated a 50-60 point positive opening**, but the anchor doubted it: weakness across most Asian indices (ex-Taiwan), and **US markets closed lower overnight**. Friday **weekend factor** in play.
- **West Asia war continues.** **Brent crude jumped ~$4 to $106-107** (from ~$102 the previous day), with the effective landed cost put at **$110-120**; India imports **~5 million barrels/day**, so this is a large economic drag. **Strait of Hormuz** situation not improving — Trump issued a **"shoot and kill" order** against those laying mines; he also asked for the **Israel-Lebanon ceasefire to be extended three weeks**. Iran's hardliners (**IRGC**) now fully dominate the government and are not backing down.
- **US software wrecked overnight by the AI narrative**: **Salesforce -9%**, **IBM -8%** (despite fine results), **Globant -9.2%**, **EPAM -6.5%**, **Accenture -6%**, **Cognizant -6%**, **Microsoft -4%**, ServiceNow lower. **Infosys ADR -4%** (results disappointed), **Wipro ADR -3.8%**. Nasdaq futures slightly positive this morning.
- **JP Morgan downgraded India to underweight** — reasons included coming earnings decline and, notably, **"no next-generation tech shares here."** **HSBC also cut India from neutral to underweight** (earnings, higher inflation, elevated energy prices, potential domestic-demand slowdown).
- Extended non-market segment on **Trump's derogatory "birth tourism" / "hellhole" remarks** about India and China (Times of India / Truth Social), discussed by the anchor and Kranthi.
