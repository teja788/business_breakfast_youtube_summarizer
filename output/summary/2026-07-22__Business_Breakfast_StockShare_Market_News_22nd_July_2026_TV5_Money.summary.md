## Global backdrop
- **Global cues positive, led by a fresh AI/semiconductor rally.** **Kospi +~5%**, **Nikkei +2%**. Overnight in the US: **SK Hynix ADR +14%**, **SanDisk +14%**, **Micron +12%**, **Intel +8%**, **AMD +8%**, **Nvidia +2%** — earnings season revived the chip trade after the **Philadelphia Semiconductor Index had fallen 20% into suspected bear-market territory** and Korea had sold off.
- **Magnificent-7 still mixed** (Amazon), with nervousness before **Google's results today**. **Traditional software — Cognizant, Accenture — fell 3-4%.** The read-across: when the AI trade is on, the **"anti-AI trade" hits Indian IT**, so selling is likely in Indian IT stocks.
- **Brent crude back at $92** on a **Red Sea shipping crisis**: Yemen's **Houthis** threatening to block Saudi and other oil through the Red Sea; **three oil tankers rerouted yesterday** (one bound for India, one for Pakistan, one elsewhere). **$100 can't be ruled out** — **Iran struck Kuwaiti islands and US bases**, and **Trump is threatening a suspected Iranian nuclear site ("Peacock Mountain" [as heard])**.
- **New Trump tariff song — pharma.** US **generic drug imports: zero tariff for two years from August 2026**, then **100% from 1 August 2028**, then **200%** — to force manufacturing onshore. Even if Trump is gone by then, successors may keep it, so **pharma got a knee-jerk sell-off** today.

## Indian markets
- **The Friday breakout is failing.** Nifty had escaped the **23,800-24,300** range on Friday; the show now says confirmation is not coming and **a retest of 23,800 would be no surprise**.
- **HDFC Bank is "the villain"** — **-5.5% on Monday's results reaction, another -2% yesterday on volumes**; now a **sell-on-rally stock** with "no hope trade" left. ICICI Bank supports somewhat, but banking/financials are net sellers, and with **IT (anti-AI trade)** and **pharma (tariffs)** also selling, financials cannot carry the index.
- **The one bright spot is the broader market** — mid/small caps moved well yesterday **backed by strong numbers**: **Sagility** good, **InfoBeans Technologies** good, **Trident** fine (revenue up YoY and QoQ, EBITDA marginally up). **MedPlus** (Hyderabad-based) hit by finance cost, depreciation and higher employee expenses.
- **Technicals (Ramakrishna)**: Nifty **0.8% down over the last month** — a "blanket lying where it was spread." Nifty **above all short moving averages except the 200**. **24,000 is very strong support** (Fibonacci + MAs); if it breaks, **23,800 is the big support** — above it this remains a **buy-on-dips market** and positional traders can build longs. **Bank Nifty** above all MAs but printed a **gravestone doji** yesterday (selling at higher levels) and trades in an even narrower range than the Nifty. **RSI ~54, MACD just above its signal line, red histogram bars** → consolidation-to-weak bias. **New-series OI concentration highest at 24,200 (both calls and puts)**. **Falling VIX over 3-4 sessions favours option sellers** (straddles/strangles); buyers are hurt by fast time decay. Intraday: **only try a short if it gets to 24,120-24,125** (spot ~24,085), i.e. after a 30-40 point rise. Consolidation may last **another week, into the monthly settlement**.
