## Global backdrop
- **GIFT Nifty indicated a gap-down opening of 100+ points (roughly 120-130 points).** The single cause: **crude**. **Brent snapped back to just under $96** after the **Houthis destroyed two Saudi oil tankers in the Red Sea**; the show called it a **March déjà vu** — crude has walked **70 → 80 → 85 → 90 → 95 → toward $100**.
- Both chokepoints are now in play: the **Red Sea / Bab-el-Mandeb** as well as the **Strait of Hormuz**, with **Saudi supplies "literally at a standstill."** Crude is at its **highest in six weeks**. A rising crude basket is a direct negative for India's macro; LPG-type supply-chain problems (as in March-April) could return.
- **Trump vs Iran escalation**: Trump threatens a bridge or a power plant "including Tehran" for every ship attacked; the Iranian foreign minister answers with **"an eye for an eye"** and threats against US Gulf allies. Trump's line — **"this is the beginning of the war, not the end"** — is what the markets followed down.
- **Tariff cliff**: the **10% tariff imposed under Section 122 of the Trade Act for 150 days expires tomorrow**, and a fresh 10% on many countries is being discussed — **a potential negative for Monday's open**.
- **US markets**: **Nasdaq -0.57%** [as heard], Dow and S&P better. **Google Q2 beat** across search, YouTube and cloud, but the **raised capex forecast was disliked**. **ServiceNow steady**; **Accenture, EPAM, Globant, Cognizant all closed lower**. **Tesla -3-4%** — missed profit expectations and **negative free cash flow**, hammered after hours. Other Asian markets were fine — **India was the underperformer**.

## Indian markets
- **Nifty lost ~200 points yesterday** and the **Bank Nifty closed weak**; the breakout above **24,300 failed** and **23,800** is now the line that must hold. Kutumba Rao: **24,200 was the key level; below it, 24,000; below that, 23,800 — and if 23,800 breaks, "markets are in for a very rough time," with a further 500-600 points of downside possible.**
- **The buy-on-dips regime ended.** The moment the index traded below 24,200 and reached 24,000, **"it's not a buy-on-dips market now — until 24,200 is crossed it is a sell-on-rallies market"** [captions render this as "buy on rallies"]. **Ultra-caution advised.**
- **Both Bank Nifty and IT are weak simultaneously** — so a Nifty recovery is hard. Banks plus financials are **~1/3 of Nifty weight**. **ICICI Bank posted good numbers but the stock didn't perform**; the **casualty of the day was Bandhan Bank** on very weak management commentary (rising cost of capital, bond yields, NIM compression) — near its **52-week low**. **SBI numbers come tomorrow** and will decide the Bank Nifty's direction.
- **Rupee at 96.57**, its **second-weakest close ever** (weakest **96.83 on 20 May**) — watch for a break of **97** / a new low.
- **FIIs and DIIs were both sellers yesterday for the first time in three months** — a combined **Rs 1,200 crore**. Small in size, but the simultaneity is the signal.
- **Opening**: Nifty **-95 to -100**, Sensex **-350**, Bank Nifty **-350 to -390**; breadth **603 advances vs 994 declines**. Losses **across oil & gas, banking, pharma, PSU banks, capital markets, financials**. By late morning losses narrowed to **45-50 points on the Nifty**. Gainers: **NTPC Green, OFSS, UTI AMC, Carborundum Universal, Saregama, Dr Agarwal's Health Care, United Spirits, Sagility India, Lemon Tree Hotels, Azad Engineering (+3.5%)**, plus **Eternal, Tata Consumer, Bajaj Auto, ONGC, Coal India, SBI Life, ITC**. A mooted **airline policy change** — letting airport operators own more than **10%** of an airline — is being watched: GMR is uninterested, but **Adani could use it** (InterGlobe promoters have repeatedly sold stakes).
- **Pharma**: Trump's fresh tariff talk on generics (effective only **after two years**) hit almost all pharma names, especially mid/small-cap pharma — whether it is a one-day reaction is the question.
