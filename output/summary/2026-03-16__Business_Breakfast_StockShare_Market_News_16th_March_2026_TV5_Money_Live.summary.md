## Global backdrop
- **War / crude dominates.** Over the weekend the US-Israel coalition struck **Kharg Island**, Iran's main export hub (~**90%** of its crude exports). Iran is readying retaliation and launched a new "Ashura" missile, signalling a possible shift from a short war to a **prolonged conflict**.
- **Brent crude above 104 dollars**; vessel movement through the **Strait of Hormuz** has halted, with **11 vessels stuck since March 1**. The IEA is preparing to release **400 million barrels** of reserves. Experts warn that if crude holds above **100 dollars for ~10 days**, **150-180 dollars** is likely.
- **Dollar index above 100**; **gold ~5000 dollars/oz** (dipped below 5000 this morning), **silver above 80 dollars**.
- **Fertilizer supply risk** flagged as the next worry: China has halted phosphate exports, Europe's nitrogen supply is near a standstill; could force US crop rotation (corn → soybeans) and hit the global food chain.
- **Bond yields tightening globally** — 2-year yields up **34-52 bps** across US, Canada, UK, France, Italy, Spain, Portugal — raising the risk that central banks **hike to fight inflation** (negative for equities).
