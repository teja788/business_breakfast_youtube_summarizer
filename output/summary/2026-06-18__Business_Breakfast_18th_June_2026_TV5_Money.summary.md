## Global backdrop
- Modi and Trump met in France after a 16-month gap. Little of substance, but Trump was warm on India ("great friend," called Modi "an angel" and "a killer" negotiator). An Iran–US ceasefire MoU was signed; the formal agreement is due "tomorrow." India–US relations, frosty three months ago, look back on track.
- **FOMC:** Kevin Warsh chaired his first meeting and was hawkish — rates held, but a hint of **one rate hike in 2026** (9 of 18 officials expect it; markets eye ~40–50 bps, possibly October). 10-yr yield ~4.5%, Dollar Index back above 100.
- **US equities fell**, led by tech/AI (Nvidia, hyperscalers); Nasdaq down. Focus shifting to the ~$700bn AI capex cycle (Microsoft, Alphabet, Meta, Oracle) and the debt-vs-equity question behind it. Brent ~$78.

## Indian markets
- 4th straight gaining session: Nifty +~100, Sensex +350, **Bank Nifty the standout (+~1.3%)**. Nifty poised near a breakout around 24,088 (next resistance ~24,247 / 100-DMA ~24,232).
- **Heavy IPO/primary-market pipeline:** NSE IPO filed (~₹30,000 cr, likely the largest ever), Jio Platforms (~₹38,000 cr) papers expected this week, plus ~$26bn of lock-in shares releasing over coming weeks. Reliance AGM tomorrow.
- India became the **6th-largest market cap (~$5T)**; South Korea edged ahead, Taiwan still above (~$5.55T).
- Theme: large-caps are institution-driven (valuation-sensitive); mid/small-caps are retail-driven (story-driven, valuation-blind) — watch the hype.
- **CtrlS** (Hyderabad/Telugu-promoted data-centre firm) got a ₹7,000 cr investment from Canada Pension Plan. India–UK trade deal effective **15 July** (positive for liquor, auto, textiles/garments, chemicals).
