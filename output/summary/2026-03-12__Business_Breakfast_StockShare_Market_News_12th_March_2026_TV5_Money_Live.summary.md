## Global backdrop
- **Gift Nifty** pointed to a gap-down of **200+ points** after Monday/yesterday's **~400-point** Nifty fall; a gap-down open materialized.
- **Brent crude crossed $100** (~$100.53-100.55, up ~55c overnight), having been ~$89-90 during the prior session. Whipsawing wildly — $120 a few days ago, then $80, back to $100. Anchor sees a chance of a move back toward **$120**, with $150 still possible if the war escalates.
- **IEA pledged to release 400 million barrels** of reserves, but that covers only ~4 days of world demand and physical commodity logistics take time, so prices stayed firm.
- Only ~**20% of global supply** disrupted (Strait of Hormuz vessels), yet crude is up ~**50%** (from ~$70 to ~$100).
- Reserve days by country: **Japan ~220 days** (highest), **India ~70 days**. US is insulated as the world's largest oil producer; Japan, South Korea, Taiwan, China relatively safe.
- **Inflation sensitivity** flagged: current ~**2.1%**; could reach **~5.1%** if oil holds above $100 for a sustained period, **~4.2%** near $80, **~4%** near $70 (RBI's target zone).
- **Iran** told mediators it will halt the war only if attacks on it stop; a **drone strike on Dubai International Airport** injured four. Trump claims "nothing left to target" in Iran but is reviving tariffs via **Section 301** (investigations on 16 countries including India and China) after the US Supreme Court struck down his earlier IEEPA tariffs.

- **Bank Nifty** (55,735 prior close; ~55,270 Monday low) now falling faster than Nifty (yesterday -2.13% vs -1.6%), losing its prior resilience.
- **Midcaps under-performing** (-1.8% vs Nifty -1.2%); advance-decline ~**592 / 1,889** (≈85% declines).
- **FII/DII flows**: FIIs sold **Rs 6,267 cr**, DIIs bought **Rs 4,965 cr** — DIIs seen pulling back somewhat.
- **India VIX ~22**. Sensex printed a **death cross** (50-DMA below 200-DMA); both indices trading ~**6% below the 200-DMA**. Anchor frames it as a **bear market since Sep 2024** and a **man-made "Black Swan" / manufactured supply-chain disruption** — urges capital protection over fresh longs.
- **Sectoral churn**: stove/appliance makers **TTK Prestige, Butterfly Gandhimathi, Stove Kraft up 7-10%**, Bajaj Electricals +3.2%, Adani Total Gas up ~13-20% on panic LPG-substitution buying; crude consumers (InterGlobe Aviation, Asian Paints), gas distributors (IGL, MGL), M&M (-3%) and L&T (-3%) weak.
- Anchor's thematic ideas: favorable — **oil exploration, pharma, energy/renewables (solar, wind), manpower-services**; avoid — **oil marketing, gas distribution, crude-consuming** names.

## Policy / corporate
- **Central cabinet eased FDI via Press Note 3**: investments from land-border countries up to **49% to be approved within a time-bound 60 days** (vs prior 6-12 months); funds (PE/VC) with up to **10% Chinese LP exposure** now cleared via the automatic route — removing a long-standing bottleneck. Indian majority ownership still required. Seen as a positive for **electronics, semiconductors, solar manufacturing** and tech transfer/jobs (India targeting **500 GW** renewables).
- Cited example: **Dixon Technologies–HKC JV (~Rs 7,624 cr)** for display modules (Dixon subsidiary 74% / HKC 26%); MeitY recommends, **DPIIT** gives final approval.
- **LPG crunch**: National Restaurants Association (Hyderabad) flags commercial LPG supply effectively halted; ~50% of members ordered gas with no delivery, ~40% have 2-3 days left. Commercial 19 kg cylinder rose from ~**Rs 1,750 (28 Feb)** to ~**Rs 2,150 (9 Mar)**; black-market rates **Rs 4,000-5,000**. Hyderabad F&B revenue ~**Rs 10,750 cr/yr** (~Rs 30 cr/day); industry employs **~8 million** directly. Restaurants moving to limited menus / bulk biryani; NRAI seeks "essential service" status and **~50-65% rationed supply**.
