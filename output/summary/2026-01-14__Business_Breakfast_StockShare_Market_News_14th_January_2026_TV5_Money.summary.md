- **US markets** closed lower last night; global cues **mixed**.
- **Geopolitics** centred on **Iran** — Trump signalling support to anti-government protesters ("help is on the way"), seen as a regime-change push (compared to Venezuela; Greenland flagged next).
- **Commodities super-cycle** in focus: **crude ~$65** (expected to stabilise at the higher $60–65 band as supply, incl. Venezuela, keeps pace); **gold** touched ATH **$4,625** then eased; **silver ~$89.99**, eyeing **$100 this quarter** (~Rs 3 lakh/kg); US reportedly weighing **refined-copper tariffs**; **aluminium** at a 3-year high.

- **India–US:** Jaishankar–Rubio phone call covering defence, energy, trade and critical-minerals supply chains; **trade-deal negotiations continue** with no blueprint yet.
