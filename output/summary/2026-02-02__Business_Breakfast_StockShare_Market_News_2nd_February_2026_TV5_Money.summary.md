# Summary — Business Breakfast, 2 February 2026 (TV5 Money)
# https://youtu.be/v2OVhLrFPUs

## Headline
- **Budget Day (1 Feb)** disappointed markets. The single sore point: a hike in **STT** for an estimated **Rs 15,000 crore** of extra revenue, which the panel says "destroyed" market sentiment.
- **Nifty** fell ~700 points intraday, touched **24,570**, recovered to close ~**24,825** (about a 500-point loss).
- Otherwise the budget was rated "decent" / growth-oriented; the complaint is purely about it ignoring capital markets.

## The budget — what was good
- **Capex raised ~10% to Rs 12.1 lakh crore**; fiscal deficit kept under control.
- **Defence capex +18%** to **Rs 7.87 lakh crore**; **railway capex +10%**; **power capex +18%**.
- **Data centres: 21-year tax holiday** — seen as a magnet for large FDI (few players: Amazon, OpenAI, Meta-type firms); benefits equipment makers (**ABB, Hitachi**).
- **Semiconductor mission**, basic customs duty cuts on many goods, **Rs 10,000 crore for bio-pharma / research**, employment-creation push for youth.
- Economic Times' **Swaminathan Aiyar**: a "critics' choice rather than crowd-pleaser" budget. FM framed it around **Mission 2047**.

## The budget — what was bad (STT)
- STT hike called counter-productive — invokes the **Laffer Curve** ("higher taxes, lower revenue").
- F&O active traders already fell from **~45 lakh to ~20–22 lakh** after SEBI's curbs; **95%** of speculators lose anyway.
- STT collections: FY25 rose ~63%; FY26 target **Rs 78,000 crore** (Kutumba Rao expects only **~Rs 62,000–63,000 crore**); FY27 target cut to **Rs 73,700 crore** — first-ever STT target cut, yet he doubts even 75–80% will be met.
- "Killing the machine that funds the economy" — IPO market already weak (60–70% of recent issues underwater); hurts the govt's own **Rs 80,000 crore disinvestment** target.

## Indian markets — internals
- **VIX spiked from ~10–10.5 to 15** (highest since June 2025) and stayed elevated (~15.09) even post-budget.
- **PCR (Nifty put-call)** collapsed to **0.59** — lowest since December 2024.
- **Bank Nifty:** every single constituent closed negative (rare) — Bank of Baroda worst, Kotak least bad. No commentary on banks in the budget.
- **IT outperformed**: Wipro, TCS, Infosys closed marginally positive.
- **PSU stocks hit 5–6%**: BEL, ONGC, SBI, Coal India, NTPC; metals (Hindalco) down.
- **Trading range now ~24,300–25,300**; needs to hold above 25,200–300 to re-attempt 26,000. Risk of 24,000 if 24,400 support breaks.
- **FII/DII selling was light (~Rs 588 crore net)**; today (2 Feb) seen as the real tell on how big players read the budget.
