## Market view
- **Worst is over for IT (short term)**, unless a fresh disruptive AI tool emerges (US/China, à la DeepSeek). Expect **consolidation with a mild upward trend**, not a runaway rally. IT valuations/yields at historical bottoms → he had flagged **contrarian buying** (including Monday). IT stocks should **stabilize at current levels**.
- Markets here are **stock-specific and news-driven**; the **AI Summit** dominates news flow. Clarity on AI's impact on Indian software (and survival strategies) is emerging from statements by Vinod Khosla, Nandan Nilekani, etc.
- **Stay invested — buy-on-dips market, not sell-on-rallies.** No decisive directional move until **FII buying turns significant**.
- **Stability beyond IT:** metals (Hindalco, Tata Steel) recovered ~2% correction; ITC, Adani Enterprises (AI push), Asian Paints and some beaten-down FMCG performed better.
- **Small-caps:** no broad-based rally from here; favour **individual companies of Rs 5,000–10,000 cr market cap at ~10–12 PE** — precision engineering, autos, capital goods, manufacturing, R&D/patent-rich names — with a **2–3 year patient view** for great returns.
- **Levels:** trading zone **25,500–26,000**; safety above **25,800**; calls at **26,000**, puts at **25,000–25,500**; **PCR ~1.12**, **VIX <13** (bull comfort up). Rupee ~**90.72**, may cross 91.

Sammaan/Sammaan Capital still in ban list.

Beneficiaries — **energy (green/grid/power), cooling equipment, EPC, hardware, cloud-tech** companies; 2–5 year view can deliver big/multi-bagger returns (multi-baggers "just happen," can't be forced).
