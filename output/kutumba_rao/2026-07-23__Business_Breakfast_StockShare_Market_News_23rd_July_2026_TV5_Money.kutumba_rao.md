## Market view
- **The buy-on-dips regime is over.** "Until yesterday we said buy on dips. But the moment it traded below 24,200 and reached 24,000, **it's not a buy-on-dips market now — until 24,200 is crossed it is a sell-on-rallies market**" [captions render this as "buy on rallies"]. **Be ultra-cautious**; don't buy indiscriminately.
- **Levels**: **24,200 was the key level** flagged yesterday; below it the downside tests **24,000**, and below that **23,800** could not be ruled out. **Today the whole question is whether the Nifty defends 23,800** — if it can't, **"markets are in for a very rough time," with signs of a further 500-600 points of downside.** Today is also the **Sensex (BSE) expiry**, so volatility will be higher.
- **Crude is the enemy.** Earlier only Hormuz was the worry; now **Bab-el-Mandeb is closing too** and **Saudi supplies are literally at a standstill** — crude is heading back toward **$100**, its **highest in six weeks**.
- **Rupee at 96.57**, the **second-weakest close ever** (weakest **96.83 on 20 May**). Whether it crosses **97** into a new low is now the clear question — another reason to be ultra-cautious.
- **Tariff cliff tomorrow**: the **10% tariff imposed under Section 122 of the Trade Act for 150 days** (after the Supreme Court ruling) **expires tomorrow**, and a fresh 10% on many countries is being floated — **a negative for the weekend and a possible big negative at Monday's open.**
- **Flows**: for the **first time in three months, FIIs and DIIs were both sellers** yesterday — **Rs 1,200 crore combined**. A small number, but both institutional categories selling together is **definitely negative and can't be ignored**.
- **Valuation caution**: many companies that gave **good results are already on high PEs, not cheap PEs** — don't assume a new leg of rally that doubles them. Wait for **attractive valuations**, for the news to be discounted and things to stabilise, then pick stocks off management commentary on future earnings. **"Buy on rumour, sell on news"** — names already at **52-week highs have more scope to correct** after good results. No need to buy out of over-enthusiasm just because a result was great. Still, **stock-specific action will dominate** in earnings season and must not be ignored.
- **Options/positioning**: once below 24,000, **call options are piling up there** — the market must **cross 24,000** to stand, since **max call writing is there**. Downside **puts at 23,800**; **put writing mostly at 24,000**. **Unless 23,800 is defended the market will be very negative.** **PCR fell to 0.84** from above 1 — at 0.8 or below, **positional/contra traders get an opportunity**. **VIX above 13** is a discomfort (it jumped **>6%** yesterday), but the VIX isn't a criterion for positional traders. Speculative **longs**: **Nestle, Astral, L&T Finance, Supreme Industries**. **Shorts**: **Bandhan, SRF, Blue Star**. Conviction **delivery**: **Bharti Airtel, Axis Bank, Apollo Hospitals, Bajaj Finance**. **Ban list: only Kaynes**, and no big changes expected as settlement nears.
- **Sentiment**: fear is again growing in the undercurrent; the worry is whether the mid/small-cap bull case **starts correcting once the results season ends**. Maximum alertness needed.
- **Macro/policy watch list**: an **OpenAI-type model reportedly hacked a startup** — the danger of an AI model going "rogue"/criminal must be recognised. **Mauritius has accepted the Principal Purpose Test (PPT)**, giving Indian tax authorities confidence and powers under the **DTAA** — if they exercise them in these weak markets **it will be a big negative for FIIs**. **Sugar**: sowing area down, stocks depleting, prices at **multi-month highs**; a **government quota system before the festival season** is possible — **high-inventory sugar companies could benefit**. **Rice exporters** may be hit as West Asian shipping routes are disrupted in the peak export season. **Airlines policy**: the rule capping an airport operator at **10% equity in any airline** may be relaxed — **GMR** has never been interested, but **Adani** could "eat up players," and **InterGlobe's promoters have repeatedly sold stakes**; watch whether the rule was changed for one group.
