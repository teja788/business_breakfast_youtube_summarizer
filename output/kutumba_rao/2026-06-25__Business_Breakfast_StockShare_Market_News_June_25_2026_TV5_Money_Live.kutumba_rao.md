## Market view
- Confirmed the **"buy on dips"** stance he repeats even during weakness — yesterday proved it, with dip-buyers making gains, especially in front-line, index-influencing stocks.
- Attributed yesterday's rally clearly to the **RBI Governor's statement** — no hurry on rate hikes, continuing the interest-rate regime, less inflation fear. A shift from hawkish toward dovish is a **positive for markets**; banks rebounded and pulled the index up. Today's question is whether the rally will be **led by global cues**.
- **Crude**: Brent/crude at the lowest since the war started, futures **below $70** — a **big positive for India** (large importer; helps current-account deficit). No doubt lower crude is an advantage.
- **Infosys**: helped by Nandan Nilekani's GBM comments (AI facilitates, not doomsday for services companies), giving IT stocks some relief. But IT's Nifty market-cap share is at a **record low** ("under-ownership"); getting back to at least equal weightage will take time — recovery on his statement alone is a secondary question.
- **Gold/silver**: correcting after large rallies from '24-'25; gold at a **7-month low**. Globally **$4,000/oz is the key point** — a breach could bring further losses, but may not break quickly; if it does, whether central-bank buying boosts it is hard to say. **"Wait and watch."**
- **Rupee**: stabilising ~**94.5**, small recovery — modest relief warranted. But the **dollar is very strong worldwide** (sharpest monthly gain in a year); watch for possible currency crises in some countries when the dollar index is this strong.
- **SIP/MF momentum** likely to continue with the recovery; overall market should gain some stability. Govt OFS collecting one of its highest amounts, boosting cash reserves.
- **US/global**: Micron strong post-market; global markets positive, especially Japan (~+4%). Expects markets to stay stable over the long weekend — **"buy on declines."**
