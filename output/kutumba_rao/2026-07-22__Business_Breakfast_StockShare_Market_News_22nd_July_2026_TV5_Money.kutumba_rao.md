## Market view
*(The macro framing below is the show's — mostly the anchor's opening wrap — with Kutumba Rao's own observations marked.)*

- **The show's frame**: the Friday breakout out of the **23,800-24,300** Nifty range is **failing**, and a retest of **23,800** would be no surprise. **HDFC Bank is "the villain"** (**-5.5% Monday on results, -2% yesterday on volume**) and is now a **sell-on-rally stock** with no hope trade left. With **IT** hit by the anti-AI trade and **pharma** by Trump's generics tariff, **financials cannot hold the index up**. The one bright spot is the **broader market**, where mid/small-cap moves are **backed by real numbers** (Sagility, InfoBeans, Trident good; MedPlus weak).
- **Kutumba Rao's own reads inside the Q&A:**
  - **Financials/NBFC holdcos are due a recovery** — Jio Financial has disappointed for two years, but peers like **Aditya Birla Capital** and the **Poonawalla** group name have been rising, so recovery will come at some point.
  - **Infra execution hurt this quarter by fuel** — "starting from the diesel problems," **availability of fuel** hurt infra companies at the execution end; order books (NCC) are still good and the next two years should be strong.
  - **AMC stocks: the good results are already in the price** — on Canara Robeco AMC's jump in net profit (**Rs 75 cr vs Rs 41 cr in March** on flat revenue) he said "**the market has discounted it already**."
  - **Food companies look weakish only because of the tariff quarrels**, but the business is consistent — wait for results.
  - **Pharma large caps are still the safe end** — Dr Reddy's is one of the country's bigger giants, "no problem," accumulate on sharp declines. But the **defence/power small-cap fancy is over** — on HBL: "**the best of times are over, the stock is being rationalised**."
- **On starting an investing journey** (to a first-time IT-employee caller planning Rs 1-1.5 lakh a month for her daughter): for the child's goal use **safe, government-guaranteed products such as Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (~8.2%)**; mutual funds are market-oriented and carry risk, as Kranthi said, but many funds average **15-20%** across good and bad years — **select such schemes and start SIPs**.

- **Canara Robeco AMC** — **Hold**. He had not followed the result himself, but for Canara Robeco and the other **asset management companies** he would say hold: the stock is already at **274-275**, having rallied to near **350** and corrected from there, and is "getting decent enough." - **Poonawalla Fincorp** — **Hold**. The results are "not bad." Management **keeps infusing capital**, and in finance companies the more the **base capital** rises the more the **lending power** rises, and with it the business they can write.

- **LTIMindtree** — **Hold; no fresh buying**. (The mail's "LTIM" was first misread as Paytm.) If you hold it, just hold; **fresh buying is not advisable**.

- **Dr. Reddy's Laboratories** — **Accumulate on sharp declines**. A blue chip and one of the bigger pharma giants in the country — "no problem." (Asked on a day pharma was selling off on the Trump generics tariff.)
- **HBL Engineering** *(HBL Power Systems)* — **Avoid / don't buy, not even on dips**. He has said this repeatedly, "even from Rs 1,000": **the best of times are over**, the stock is being rationalised, and it **can go down to 550-600 over the next few months**.
