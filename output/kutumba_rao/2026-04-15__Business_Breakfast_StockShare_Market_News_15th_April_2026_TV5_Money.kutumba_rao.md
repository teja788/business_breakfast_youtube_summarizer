## Market view
- Market is **taking it for granted** that the West Asia conflict subsides — everyone pulls back out of self-interest. Even after the Hormuz blockade threat and Iran's actions, markets didn't change much; the **only real change is crude falling substantially** (futures ~$91 this morning vs the $104–105 knee-jerk) — "very positive," already discounted.
- Today should **cover the holiday gap** and recover Monday's losses. But **don't expect a substantial up-move** immediately — still a broad trading range; the short-term **23,500–24,000 range may widen to 23,500–24,500**. Highly positive indication if it opens and closes above 24,000; then resistance band 24,300–24,500.
- **IMF sees ~6.5% GSDP growth** — India remains the world's fastest-growing large economy → a better region for investment. FIIs sold for over a year (and the first four months of this year) but will return / make modest buys at some point.
- **Key macro risk = monsoon.** Skymet flagged **~80% drought probability** two weeks ago; severe April heat already. A large El Niño effect would clearly hit **consumer stocks and agri-related stocks**.
- **Margin-trading facility has shrunk a lot** (even as cash turnover stays resilient) → risk-taking ability has fallen → he reads this as **positive** (lower MTF = stronger investors). Keeps cautioning against MTF (positions cut easily, losses and transaction costs mount).
- **Don't take the recent bounce for granted** — it continues only in select/stronger stocks and **fizzles out in weaker stocks**. **Stay invested only in stronger stocks; exit weaker stocks** — Q4 results will show which stocks have visibility.
- Nasdaq's longest winning streak since 2021; oversold software-services stocks can bounce. He'd earlier flagged **TCS and Infosys** as choices; spillover may put software on better footing this week, but **don't expect continuous rallies**.
- Bitcoin made a four-week high (~$75k, up ~10% from 68) — a sign global risk appetite is rising. If Nifty crosses 24,500, a **25,000 attempt can't be ruled out**.
- **Short-term stance flipped to "buy on dips"** (from the earlier "sell on rallies").
