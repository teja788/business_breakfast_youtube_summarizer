## Market view
- **Odds favour further weakness.** The long-overdue US correction (Magnificent Seven / Nvidia-led AI) could be severe. Last time it corrected and went to new highs, so that's possible too.
- **Mega-IPO caution:** historically big IPOs hit capital markets everywhere — Visa IPO (2008) → S&P −38%; Agricultural Bank of China (~$22bn, 2010) → Shanghai −22%. With the SpaceX IPO now, markets holding would be surprising; a natural correction may be setting in (perhaps a day early). When the US (largest economy/market) catches a cold, the world catches it.
- **RBI/FII debt concession = "a very, very bad decision."** It gives tax-free entry to FIIs on high-yielding gilts/bonds while ignoring domestic investors/funds who have supported the market. Expect it to be short-lived; the rupee's Friday strength may fizzle back to 96–97 within 1–2 months. With a depreciating currency, high rates alone won't attract investors. Indian debt market also lacks depth.
- **Broader market is the strength.** Only 12% of the 3,343 sub-₹10,000-cr-cap stocks were positive before March; 70% (2,358) are positive now — a mega rally since April. Not pure liquidity: breadth, delivery buying, good earnings and improving risk appetite drove it. Sub-₹10k-cr recovery likely continues; select correcting names may move to the mid-cap range over the next year. **Stock picking is most important from here.**
- **Levels/positioning:** 23,000 puts jumped (even 22,500 puts built up). Today may open ~200 pts down; sustaining needs Adani group / select tech / banks support. Hold above **23,300 (EOD) = safer**; near **23,000 = prepare for more weakness**. PCR realized to ~0.77 → positional trades possible. VIX ~16 (rising VIX would turn negative). Speculative longs: Vishal Mega Mart, CMS, Policybazaar, Eternal; shorts: Swiggy, Trent, Dalmia Bharat; conviction delivery: Marico, LIC Housing Finance, UltraTech Cement. Cash turnover at 22-month high → operator distribution risk. Don't chase "cats and dogs"; sell weak stocks but don't trade them speculatively. **Caution is the watchword.**
- **Workshop themes:** best four-quarter earnings growers (10%+ revenue & profit); "magnificent seven sectors" of last FY (tourism/hospitality and real estate outperforming in Q4 GDP) plus a new sector from this quarter; a new ~90-page all-colour strategy book to be launched.
