## Market view
- After the big post-US-trade-deal rally, markets stay in **consolidation mode, no runaway rally** from here — "reality plays out." No change in the global geopolitical situation; volatility has surged in risky assets (Bitcoin from near 75,000 down to ~60,000) and even precious metals (silver fizzled, gold into correction). **No reason to give a premium to equity valuations.**
- **RBI MPC = a non-event.** No significant change expected; rate-cut cycle may have **temporarily paused** (1.25% cut over the past year, banks following on deposit rates, supportive stance, Rs 50,000 cr OMO infusion yesterday, soft inflation, strong growth). Added caveat: the **GSDP/CPI base-year change** (to 22-23 / 24) will quietly cause dramatic shifts in macro indicators next year — markets don't yet appreciate this.
- It is a **stock-specific, sector-specific market** — and will be for the **next six months**; no dramatic runaway rally in any sector.

## IT / AI disruption (his headline theme)
- Software/IT faces **real disruption** from agentic AI (Anthropic's Claude Cowork, OpenAI's AI co-workers). Indian IT is stuck on the **age-old low-end outsourcing model**, hasn't moved up the value chain, confined to HR-type functions (recruiting, code, selling).
- **"Wealth creation from now by IT companies is so doubtful."** Don't carry the attitude that "Infosys/TCS are great, what's the worry." Study the sector's tech changes carefully; investors ignoring this will taste losses. **Only companies that adapt/reinvent will survive.** Past growth rates are "very, very questionable" in this disruptive era.
