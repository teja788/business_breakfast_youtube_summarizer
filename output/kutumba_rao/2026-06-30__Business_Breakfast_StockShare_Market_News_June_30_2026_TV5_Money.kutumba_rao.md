## Market view
- **July may not be much better** than the May–June consolidation. Q1 results start tomorrow and will clearly show the war/supply-chain impact (April–May–June). Expect **more stock-specific moves**; index moves need heavyweights (Reliance, HDFC Bank) to participate.
- **Negatives**: driest monsoon since 2009, **~40% June deficit**, **kharif sowing area down ~23%** → rural-economy hit; consistently weak rupee; external debt up to **$762bn (~20.8% debt/GDP)** → possible September mid-course correction by the government. Market speculation that the Finance Minister may change (talk of **Shaktikanta Das**) could move markets this week.
- **Positive**: **IIP at a 5-month high of 5.1%**, led by manufacturing and electricity — "light at the end of the tunnel." A US–India trade deal announcement (final legal text being changed) could be a big trigger, terms-dependent.
- **Corporate actions punish/reward stock-specifically**: Persistent Systems -11% on the Nagarro takeover (target's growth < Persistent's); Astral fell on chemical-division demerger; Pricol announced an auto-castings demerger (watch). Netweb (>120 PE) punished on fund-raise; Apollo Micro Systems rewarded on the same. "Listen to what the market says — don't write your own poetry."
