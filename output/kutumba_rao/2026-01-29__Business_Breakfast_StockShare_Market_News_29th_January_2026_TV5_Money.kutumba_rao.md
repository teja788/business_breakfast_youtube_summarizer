# What Kutumba Rao said — Business Breakfast, 29 January 2026 (TV5 Money)
# https://youtu.be/2fuGeEo9U_g

Kutumba Rao returned after a month-long (largely personal) US trip and framed the session around the Budget (Sun 1 Feb), the gold surge, and US political risk.

## Macro / market view
- **US sentiment is dejected** over the President's actions; Trump's second-term approval is at its lowest (~19% decline from his win), corroborated by the Economist. ICE/immigration crackdowns are raising democratic-rights concerns.
- **Fed independence at stake.** The **Lisa Cook** removal case (SCOTUS negated Trump's order) is "one of the most important legal cases in Fed history." Powell held rates and warned successors to stay out of elected politics; loss of Fed independence would destroy central-bank credibility. Read as a **dollar-weakness signal** — dollar at 4–5 year lows vs most currencies (strong only vs INR).
- **Gold thesis:** the rally is **central-bank-driven**, not retail — central banks began stockpiling gold as a reserve asset after Russia's 2022 Ukraine invasion and the seizure of ~300t of reserves. Gold doesn't rise ~4% in a day (first in ~6 yrs) "for no reason" — a big global asset-class shift. Rally likely to continue while Trump-driven uncertainty persists.
- **Markets hostage to "Trump tantrums."** Cited Greenland as a "TACO trade" (Trump Always Chickens Out) — markets fell 800–900 pts then recovered to new highs. Watch for a possible US government shutdown.
- **EU–India FTA** praised as bold and a win-win; India signing FTAs serially (Mauritius 2021 → UK → EU). Trump's tweet on it was "egoistic"; the US deal may come in 1–2 weeks but "soon" has been heard for six months.

## Budget framing (sees it as decisive)
- **Capital-gains tax stability is the "biggest trigger"** — clear signals already visible; any STT simplification + capital-gains stability would lift markets even absent other positives. Watch whether LTCG/STCG is tweaked and whether changes apply only to FPIs or to everyone.
- Wants **large infra capex**, targeted PLI/DLI for high-growth/new sectors (AI data centres, clean energy, defence, railways), FDI tie-ups — to shift labour from agriculture (46% of working population, 16% of GDP) into manufacturing. Cited Apple-phone manufacturing hub success under Atmanirbhar Bharat.
- Echoed Modi's "three D's" — **democracy, demographic dividend, demand** — and demographic-dividend/population-management theme.
- Economic Survey due today; results season continuing.
