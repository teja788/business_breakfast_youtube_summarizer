## Market view

- **Wednesday open looked positive**, but Kutumba Rao called yesterday's bounce largely **short-covering** rather than genuine buying, since it was a settlement day after a continuous fall. He noted **resistance above 23,600** where the index struggled to hold and slipped at the end.
- The stocks that rose were **not encouraging** — not strong enough counters to sustain the market. Eternal rose, Tata Steel rose, M&M in autos; but the one lagging sector was **IT** (Wipro, Infosys, TCS), and **consumer stocks weakened** too (Tata Consumer, ITC, Nestle, HUL). So yesterday's hold was more **index management**.
- **Today is the key/critical session.** With a wild gap-up at open, if Nifty can hold **above 23,600** there is some relief, and the market may stick in a **23,400–24,000 trading zone**. Dow futures positive (~120 pts), Nikkei strong (~2%), Hang Seng flat.
- **Crude:** regular (heavy/high-sulphur) crude India actually uses is below **95 dollars**, down ~2% this morning; Brent above 100 but India barely uses Brent (only ~2-3% for specific purposes). If India's crude moves close to ~90, the market can stabilise more.
- **US Fed** commentary is now the focus. India's Chief Economic Advisor (Nageswaran) said higher oil prices are a clear threat to the economy — if crude stays high, **GDP could slip to ~6.4%** from the 7.4% estimate, and **inflation could rise to ~5.2%**. Two macro points investors must keep in memory.
- **FIIs keep selling** — until their positions reverse, the market won't find much strength; their impact is felt on specific stocks/sectors.
- **Rupee** back near **92.40**, close to all-time low, pressured by crude and sustained FII outflows.
- **Gold** falling on higher dollar prices, **below 5,000 USD/oz** this morning; speculators warn of a possible **600–700 dollar** sharp correction in 2-3 days if it stays under 5,000. **Stay away from gold / precious metals** for now; no need to chase it as a safe haven.
- **VIX** cooled below 20 (slightly positive for bulls); more comfort around 15-16, better below 18 in the short term. **PCR above 1 (1.09)** and positive breadth are generally positive to observe.
- **Options:** highest call OI now at **24,000** (only attempted if Nifty holds above 23,600); put OI heavy at **23,000** — a slip below 23,500 risks a re-test of 23,000.
- Speculative longs seen in Premier Energies, HFCL, Nykaa; speculative/persistent in Tata Elxsi, United Spirits, BPCL. Conviction buying seen in RBL, Cholamandalam Finance, Max Health.
- **MOIL** rose (~20%) on an investor presentation + analyst commentary; last-quarter result was flat but he saw signs of disinvestment / a possible QIP. He likes **ore/metal PSU stocks** (Coal India resilient, MOIL jumped, NMDC could jump next) but flagged that besides Govt of India, **state governments (Maharashtra, MP)** hold stakes in MOIL and could exit if they need cash.
- One-day 20% moves both ways in OCTL (Oil Country Tubular). Selective good-volume rises in Jyoti CNC, ideaForge (over 1 crore shares). Shaily Engineering (~20%), Raymond Realty (~15-16%), Webel Solar, Capsite jumped — broader-market activity improving **ahead of Q4 results**; but remember "**buy on rumour, sell on news**" as earnings visibility rallies fade.
- **Bottom line:** Stay invested, no need to sell in a hurry, but **keep exiting weaker stocks** on any small relief rally.
