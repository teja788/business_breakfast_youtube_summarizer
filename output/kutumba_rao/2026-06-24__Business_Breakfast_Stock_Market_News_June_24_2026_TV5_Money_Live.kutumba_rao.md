## Market view
- Yesterday's weakness (from ~11:30 to close) is a **negative**: it landed right at support. From **24,100 to 24,000 in just 15 minutes**, and the damage continued to the close once 24,000 broke.
- **24,000** is the pivot / **major resistance** now (max put pile-up); once below, sharp falls follow. **Below 23,800 → cautious**; 23,600 is minor support; below that, support only in the **23,200-400** zone. Below 23,800 there's a possibility of touching **23,500**.
- Options/sentiment: call OI shifted from 24,500 to 24,000; puts piled at 24,500. **Put-call ratio dropped 1.12 → 0.88 (<0.9)** — nearing a buying indicator for positional traders. **VIX jumped ~9%** = rising uncertainty.
- **Biggest negative = El Nino / weak monsoon spread.** Could mean severe drought; Q1 already hit, and if Q2 is hit, H1FY earnings look finished → earnings re-ratings. **Watch monsoon progress daily**; good monsoon = market revives.
- **Precious metals** in severe correction: gold **-12%+**, silver **-18%** — decade-worst (gold worst since Dec 2016; silver worst since June 2022). Watch if **gold breaches $4,000/oz**. (Context he gave: 2024 silver +22%, gold +28%; 2025 silver +150%, gold +65%; Jan–Mar ~+10%.)
- **Crude falling (~70s)** is a positive for India; crude shocks unlikely, but the monsoon shock is harder to recover from.
- Momentum continues in good stocks despite broader-market fear; buy carefully in stocks with momentum + results visibility.
- Caution these 2-3 days (Friday holiday; only Thursday trades + Sensex expiry; weekend factor).
- **Bottom line:** wouldn't say "sell," but if Nifty **closes below 23,800, avoid fresh buying this week** (revisit next week). Stay invested, pick and choose good stocks; **trim positions in stocks already in profit** (earnings season ahead); **exit weaker stocks**.
