# What Kutumba Rao said — Business Breakfast, 29 April 2026 (TV5 Money)
# https://youtu.be/oRx5AEdjqvo

Kutumba Rao was present and named throughout the episode. He had been at the Vizag data-center foundation-stone event the previous day, gave his market view for the May series, defended the AP government's data-center concessions, and answered viewer stock questions. The technical view was given separately by **Ramakrishna**; **Vasanth** anchored.

## Vizag data center / AP concessions
- Very enthusiastic about the Vizag event: the **speed of doing business** (agreement-to-foundation-stone in the shortest time period) was a first in India and builds large-corporate confidence that "AP means business."
- Expects more such events in the next couple of months — electronic clusters on the **Tirupati** and **Kadapa** sides too. Vizag has a "full-stack advantage" (port, power, ample land, sea/naval base) and its face could change completely over the next 5–10 years.
- Defended the **SGST exemption** as a destination-based tax: since data-center consumption flows largely outside the state (global services), the actual loss to AP is far smaller than assumed — just as the Kia (Anantapur) SGST exemption he personally cleared turned out well. Called the **Rs 2,245 cr** GST exemption ~**2.5% of the ~$15 bn capex** — an investment, not a loss. Cited a needed third undersea-cable **landing station** (geo-diversity vs Mumbai/Chennai), **$15 bn** infrastructure, Vizag as a future **subsea gateway**, the 15-year compounding tax base, and **50,000+ direct/indirect jobs** as a strategic moat.

## Market view — May series
- **May series likely to stay in consolidation mode**; rollovers especially subdued, particularly in Bank Nifty.
- **Banks cautious**: RBI's revised forward-looking provisioning norms are negative, especially for **SBI** and treasury-heavy PSU banks (**Union Bank** corrected sharply). Expects banks subdued until Q1 FY27 results; only private banks may do better than PSUs now and then — wait and watch the rollout.
- **US Fed** likely a non-event (no rate change, outgoing chair); a surprise cut would spark a sharp near-term market reaction.
- **FIIs still in sell mode**, DIIs buying modestly; fresh rupee weakness (near 95 on higher crude) would aggravate FII selling.
- **Caution on new SIF (specialized investment fund) offerings** — don't assume outperformance; invest judiciously. Prefers the growing **ETF** options.
- **Election swing risk**: exit polls (tonight) are now 80–90% accurate; a sharp NDA gain vs incumbents holding will move the market. Watch the chain of events — higher energy prices → higher food prices → higher inflation/monetary tightness.
- **Power/IEX**: spot per-unit power crashed to **3–5 paise** (from ~Rs 10 a few weeks ago) on a solar glut — so not all power companies will post great Q1 results; only those managing offtake/volatility well will.
- **US tech**: OpenAI miss hit tech hard, but the recent Nasdaq/S&P rally was fueled by a second round of AI-stock buying — uncertain it continues.
- **Gold/silver range-bound** (gold near $4,500/oz) — no big move expected.
