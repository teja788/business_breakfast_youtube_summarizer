## Market view
- Frames the US-Israel strike on Iran as a **"known unknown"** — anticipated for weeks and already largely priced in. The uncertainty over *whether* the strike would happen was "more damaging to market sentiment than the actual event." So expect a knee-jerk move, then the market digests it and **stability returns**.
- **"This is NOT the time to panic / liquidate."** Wait and watch. A gap-down was expected, but since Friday already saw a big jerk, today may be less negative than feared.
- Markets will **split vertically**: commodity exporters (Brazil, Mexico, Chile) may benefit; oil-importing/consuming nations (India, China, Japan, Korea) take some impact.
- **Macro is supportive**: yesterday's GST numbers showed excellent growth; the revised GDP series gives realistic numbers establishing India as the **fastest-growing economy**. View the market "two-way."
- Acknowledges bear case: deeper impact on India via crude dependence, possible **FII/capital outflow**, wider **current account deficit**, and an already-**weak rupee**. But **domestic funds are flush with cash** and keep investing; with equity an underperformer vs precious metals (now expensive), flows should rotate back to equity.
- **Doubts gold/silver hold higher levels**: large central banks (China, India) that bought gold earlier won't buy now under CAD pressure — so without central-bank buying, only investor/ETF buying may not sustain previous highs. "I will be surprised if they hold."
