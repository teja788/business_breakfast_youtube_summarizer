## Market view
- **Yesterday was just consolidation** — the market corrected from higher levels and closed only ~**30 points** positive. Breadth was okay in the broader market, but there was **distribution**: a lot of stocks are not able to sustain higher levels. That was the one clear negative.
- **24,000 is the line.** Today is also **BSE/Sensex settlement day**, so it must be watched. If **24,000 holds for a third successive session**, the market proves itself **resilient against global uncertainty**. Below 24,000, further weakness comes.
- **Options**: no big changes — heaviest weekly **calls at 24,200**, heaviest **puts piled at 24,000**. **PCR has slipped below one to 0.95**; **VIX fell marginally but stays above 13, at 13.27**. With the weekend factor arriving, **tomorrow is more important than today**. Verdict: **wait and watch — the bulls still have confidence, it is still a buy-on-dips market. Stay invested, but stock selection is the key.**
- **Retail is the only factor that dominates the Indian market** — not the UK, US, Chinese data or the Korean fund-flow comparison. SIP money is what sustains it (headline that day: SIPs growing even as cancellation rates rise), and DIIs support on the back of it. How long that lasts must be watched, because there are **indications FIIs will go back into selling mode**.
- **Rupee is the big risk**: it fell badly to **96.32**, and going by forex contracts it looks like **97 will be conclusively breached in the next two-three weeks** — "a big negative".
- **Crude**: Brent standing above **85-86**; West Asia is simmering with no sign of subsiding. If it holds above 86, expect an impact on the Indian economy **from next month if not this one**.
- **Macro/policy**: China's slowest growth in three years is a positive for India as the fastest-growing large economy, so hopes of foreign inflows rise. Yesterday's announcements — **chips, mobile phone manufacturing, urea** — look like the **last steps before the 20th Parliament session** opens; with the Women's Reservation and Delimitation Bills, **economics will take a back seat** to politics, and any global event landing at that time can hit the market.
- **GIFT City** is "slowly becoming a country within a country" — new delisting norms, direct-listing facility and continuous concessions. Strong indications that **volumes will shift there**, with FIIs possibly avoiding the NSE and BSE.
- **Sectors yesterday**: **financials literally saved the market** from mid-session — **Shriram Finance, SBI, SBI Life, HDFC Bank, Bajaj Finance** all rose. **IT** showed consolidation-to-slight-weakness (Infosys and Wipro each ~-0.5%); if IT consolidates here and rises without sharp falls, it becomes **a supporting pillar** for the market.
- **Results to judge today**: **Wipro** and **Tech Mahindra** for the IT read; more important, **Jio Financial** and **BHEL** (stocks retail investors ask about regularly), plus **Polycab** and **South Indian Bank** (RBI just approved its new CEO, a Canara Bank management person shifting in) — watch the commentary. **Tata Elxsi** was weak yesterday exactly as flagged at result time.
