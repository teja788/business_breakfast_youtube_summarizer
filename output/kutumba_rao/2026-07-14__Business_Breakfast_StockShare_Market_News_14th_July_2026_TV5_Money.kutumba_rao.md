## Market view
- **Yesterday moved along expected lines.** The gap-down open came as expected, and he had said that **as long as 24,000 is not breached this is a buy-on-dips market**. 24,000 held in the first half-hour, the market then jumped, filled in all the morning losses and closed marginally positive — "how much strength there is internally in the market was clearly reflected."
- **The strength came from IT, not the frontline broadly.** The IT stocks considered weak for several months supported the market last week and again yesterday: **TCS back near 2,200** (had gone below 2,000), **Infosys crossed ~1,100** (had gone below 1,000), **HCL Tech +5% to 1,225** on better-than-expected results. **Buy-on-dips is visible in IT because of under-ownership** after the post-AI-theme sell-off; the impact has eased and the stocks are recovering slowly. Not "completely out of the woods" yet, but the quarterly results imply they are **stabilising**.
- **India standing up while Korea washed out** (that market fell another **6-7%** yesterday) is "special".
- **Macro turned clearly negative and can't be ignored**: retail inflation **4.35%**, above the RBI's target; **trade deficit $30bn+**, widest in ~4-5 months; **rupee weakened to closer to 96** even after the Finance Minister met the bank chiefs — raising doubts that the government's **FCNR-deposit steps** are playing out. **Don't be surprised if the rupee crosses 96 again.**
- **Crude is the trigger**: once Brent jumped back to **84** (almost **85** after Trump's statement about a **20% levy tied to Hormuz [as heard]**, "because America will protect it"), global markets turned weakish; regular crude ~**80**. Watch how the geopolitical scenario unfolds.
- **Gold** has fallen closer to **$4,000**; **below $4,000 he expects a sharp $300-400 correction**. Watch carefully.
- **All the action is in mid/small caps** — both indices at **lifetime highs**. FY27 so far: **mid cap +21%, small cap +29%, Nifty only +8.5%**. Retail is continuously bullish there and **FIIs are few in those categories**, so repeated FII large-cap selling has left them unaffected; visible in **SIP/mutual fund flows jumping again last month**. Examples cited of names up **40-60% or even 100%**: Bharat [Forge], Laurus, Prestige, Godrej Properties, and smaller mid caps like Aegis Logistics, Welspun, Neuland, Himadri Speciality. **But**: "just because there is a rally in mid caps and small caps, buying whichever stock comes along is not advisable" — **stick to stocks with fundamentals**.
- **Options / settlement**: today is **settlement day**. Highest call OI at **24,500** — he thinks there is **no chance of 24,500 being touched today** given the weak global open; anything else would be a surprise. Calls also piled up at **24,300**; **puts mostly at 24,000** — if 24,000 is retested and **broken**, there are definite indications the market goes into a **weak trend**. **PCR jumped to ~1.43**, one of the highest in recent times, driven by small/mid-cap activity — more bullish activity — but **VIX also rose and closed above 13**, so there is some uncertainty too.
- **Bottom line**: "strictly fundamentally sound stocks — don't go to weaker stocks. **Still it is a buy-on-dips market.**"
