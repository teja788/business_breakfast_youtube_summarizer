Kutumba Rao anchored the macro open, framed the budget setup, and read the viewer mails/calls. In this episode the per-stock viewer verdicts (Hold/Add/Profit-book) were almost all given by **Kranthi/Vasanth**, with **Ramakrishna** handling technicals — see the note at the end. Kutumba Rao's own market view and the calls clearly his are below.

## Market view
- **Global/macro:** Dollar index very weak (~96), driving gold above **$5,200** and silver ~**$1,154** (+~8% overnight); gold/silver ETFs running hard. Rupee among the weakest major currencies (~91.92), negative for imports/travel/education.
- **Budget (1 Feb)** is now the big event with the India–EU FTA done; he cautioned the FTA won't show in earnings for over a year, and that the budget — like the trade deals — will likely be a one-week reaction at most before focus returns to sentiment. Auto premium imports (Audi/VW) under the EU deal could be a negative for the likes of M&M.
- **Pre-budget weakness historic:** Sensex -3.9% over the month (weakest since 2016); small-cap index -~7%. Real damage in mid/small caps where good results are being ignored — a sentiment problem, not fundamentals.
- **PSU re-rating:** SBI overtook ICICI Bank to become the 2nd-largest bank by market cap — a "massive psychological shift" toward PSU banks. Bank-cap ranking now HDFC > SBI > ICICI > Axis (4th) > Kotak.
- **Levels:** Nifty Jan low 24,919; close 25,175; needs 200–300 pts more to reach the 25,440 upper end. Highest call OI 25,500, put 25,000 → expect a ~500-pt range into the budget; 26,000 the upside if the budget pleases.
- **FIIs** net sellers (~Rs 1,952 cr); **DIIs** rescued the tape (~Rs 7,760 cr). A bull rally needs FII selling to stop.
