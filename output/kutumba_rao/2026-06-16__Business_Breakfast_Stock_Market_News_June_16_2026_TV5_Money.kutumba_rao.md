Not surprising to him.
- Good sign: market is consolidating. It had nearly tested 23,000, came back to ~24,000; if it consolidates between 24,600 and 24,000, the next leg could move easily to 24,500.
- Ifs and buts from globals: G7 meeting on now (watch Ukraine/Iran, Trump/Putin statements). Next big catalyst is the Indo-US trade deal — said to be in the final leg; an India-favorable announcement (even with tariffs lower than previously expected) would be a big booster and the market would take off immediately.
- Negatives behind yesterday's caution: new-series WPI at 9.7% (multi-month high) on a revamped index with a higher fuel component — be careful. New Service Provider Price Index launched (~7 sectors incl. banking, exchanges/exchanges, insurance, railways, telecom); within it, exchanges grew 2.8% QoQ despite F&O turnover declining — cash volumes rose, sign of active investor participation. Unemployment rate at an 11-month high (~5.x%), especially rural — watch for government steps.
- FM said India will take more steps for foreign inflows; FPIs will watch how proactive these are. Don't get carried away with optimism.
- Rupee at ~5-week high of 94.45 vs dollar; if it reaches and breaks below 94, psychologically supportive for the market and positive for importers.
- Options/positioning: calls jumped at 24,500 yesterday, but as morning resistance appeared, contracts above 24,000 and the 24,500 were cut and shifted to 24,000 — now 24,000 calls hold highest open interest; market must cross 24,000 and hold. Good supports at 23,500 (highest, major support). PCR went to 1.41 Friday, fell below 1 in one day (to 0.99) — abundant caution from the afternoon. VIX cooled moderately to ~14.35 (below 15, close to 14) — no big concern, market stabilizing.
- Derivatives reads: speculative longs in Radico, Motilal Oswal, Tata Power, Cement stocks. Shorts in Bajaj Auto, Cochin Shipyard, ONGC, Tata Elxsi (consistently weakish — observe carefully). Delivery-based conviction in Jindal Steel, APL Apollo, Marico, Indus Towers; Unilever / FMCG building delivery positions slowly.
- Globals: SpaceX IPO rose from listing to ~200 then 224 after-market (~2.3 trillion dollar market cap; ~4,400 millionaires, ~400 billionaires) — feared crash didn't happen; huge appetite. Watch market reaction after bulk IPOs and the correcting US small-cap (Russell) index for possible spillover.
- HCL Tech's 150 million dollar / 10.46% investment in Sarvam AI (valuation ~1.5 billion dollars; a new unicorn) is a good positive trigger for IT. Companies hold record cash (~13 lakh crore); liquidity keeps hunting for opportunities (SpaceX abroad, Sarvam here). A liquidity crunch will come eventually — part of the game; don't sit fearing it, stay on the search for opportunity.
- Overall stance: market needs to "digest" the ~800–900 point two-day rise (like a buffet); a 150–200 point correction is no cause for panic. **Stay invested; it remains a "buy on dips" market.** Watch out for companies doing aggressive QIPs — check floor prices (which determine moves): KIMS priced 799, Craftsman 8,966; watch Waaree Energies; assess whether floors become benchmark vs peak prices.
