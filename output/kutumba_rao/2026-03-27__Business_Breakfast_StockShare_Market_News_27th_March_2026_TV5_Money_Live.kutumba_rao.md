## Market view
- Expects the market to get **locked in a broad range: 22,500 (downside) to 24,000 (upside)** — could last "some more weeks."
- **Two scenarios:** (1) everything settles, Iran fully surrenders, crude normalises → oil could even go below $50/bbl; or (2) the **most probable** — a **"new normal" of $100 crude** (Iran leverages its position, premiums/tolls), so companies and countries must prepare for costly crude.
- The new normal exposes weak economies, **including India** (import-dependent → current-account deficit hit, rupee depreciation, falling remittances).
- **Stock selection is key** — some stocks are resilient with growth momentum and "particularly held up" (he flagged Bharat/Bayer-type energy names, some metals, the Poona group's Finolex/Fincorp, even Kalyan Jewellers on sustained turnover).
- On broking downgrades: not surprised Goldman cut the Nifty target to 25,900; **the year's high won't be retouched**, in effect. Suggests a **Nifty Top 10 Equal Weight Index** as a better bet in a sell-off (e.g. DSP equal-weight portfolios) for "slightly reasonable returns."
- **Tech / Meta court judgments must not be ignored** — content-addiction/harm rulings could spread across countries (bans or licensing); ~$200bn market-cap lost in two days by Meta/Nvidia; indirect impact on all tech stocks. Track that news flow.
- US **private-credit industry problem** — huge redemptions; even BlackRock could take a hit. **Best of times for the US market are over** → lower tops, lower bottoms, global impact.
- **Bulls/bears:** if Nifty goes below 23,000 (where puts have piled up), the old scenario could re-emerge and the 800-pt bounce could fizzle. Be careful; alertness essential.
- **Stay invested**, focus on stock-picking and homework over trading; **even in a bear market there are outperforming stocks/sectors** — "to think you can't make money in a bear market is foolishness."
- Indian tech's only consolation is **rupee weakness** (94/$) → possible exchange gains; FII selling eased to ~₹1,800 cr, DIIs bought aggressively (~₹5,500 cr).
- **Forex-loan risk premium up 200% in a month** — a potentially big blow to corporates, HNIs, even education-loan borrowers.
