## Market view
- **Still a buy-on-dips market.** Last week the Nifty crashed almost 550 points in a single day, then recovered, and Friday brought a ~250-point rally that reclaimed 24,000 and closed above 24,200. "It is not a market where you have to panic and sell." **Stay invested, but keep a trailing stop** to protect recent gains.
- **Weekend geopolitics undid the optimism.** News that **Hormuz [as heard] would close again** lifted **Brent to ~$80/bbl** and regular crude to **~$74-75**; US markets flattened, **US futures ~-180**, Asian markets weak, **gold futures near $4,000**. "Geopolitical issues have come onto the screen again."
- **Key level: 24,000.** As long as the Nifty holds above 24,000 "there is no need to be afraid"; the moment it is cut and it trades below, further weakness is indicated. Post-open he confirmed it was "expectedly taking support at 24,000" — keep it as the benchmark.
- **Rupee** weakened **10 paise week-on-week to 95.32**; watch whether fresh weakness in the broader market feeds through.
- **Monsoon is the big positive** — it has now covered the majority of the country, which dictates rural demand, inflation and agriculture output. Watch whether it stays vigorous and whether reservoir levels rise; also watch how **FMCG** benefits.
- **Earnings will dictate the week, more than global cues**: **HCL Tech, Wipro, Reliance Industries, HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank**. After TCS, the steam in IT strengthened — the sector everyone thought was negative outperformed.
- **IPO drain is a negative for the secondary market**: three big mainboard IPOs may take away **more than Rs 10,000 cr** this week, including **SBI Funds Management** (he "would not be surprised" if subscriptions ran to a lakh crore). With that much money going to the primary market, the secondary market could stay subdued.
- **Track stock-specific news carefully** — it is what plays out in the market: **MRPL +9%** (ONGC strategic oil reserves), **Indian Bank** Q numbers lifting the whole PSU bank pack, low-profile **Lux Industries +10%** on a Reebok licensing deal.
- **F&O**: **VIX 13.36** (bulls more comfortable below 12, but that looks unlikely quickly); **PCR ~1.1** — a jump to **1.3** brings selling back, a fall to **0.7-0.8** could bring buying in. Call OI built well at **24,500**, but with weakness plus **tomorrow's weekly settlement** expect morning unwinding there and some monthly unwinding too. Puts heavy at **24,000**; a test of 24,000 would spike the **23,800 put**.
- **Positioning**: speculative **longs in CDSL, Biocon, Paytm**; speculative **shorts in Mankind, Torrent Pharma, Havells, Radico**. **Delivery with conviction** visible in **Jubilant Foods (~361, after a long time), SRF, Apollo Hospitals, HDFC Bank, Kotak Bank** — worth observing. Ban list essentially empty. **FIIs and DIIs both net buyers**, but watch whether FII buying continues if geopolitical news worsens.
- **Post-open read**: IT better again (**HCL Tech, Infosys**); drags were **IndiGo** (crude impact), autos and metals (**Tata Steel**). **Just Dial [as heard] jumped 11% with volumes** — the odd big gainer. "Wait and watch; it is not the time to immediately jump into the market and trade."

## Stock calls (viewer Q&A)
- **Standard Engineering** — **Hold (with trailing stop)**. Bought ~254; a stock that was at 110-120 until recently went to 300 in a month and is now consolidating at 280-285, at a 52-week high. For short-term momentum, compulsorily keep a trailing stop loss; for the medium term, keep a wider stop and hold.
- **Capstone Services** — **Hold (with trailing stop)**. Bought ~350; it jumped and is going back into consolidation mode, also at a 52-week high. Same advice as Standard Engineering — trailing stop if playing momentum, wider stop and hold for the medium term.
- **Astra Microwave Products** — **Buy**. One of the better companies in micro-electronics and defence electronics, at its 52-week high, with an extremely good last quarter. The demerger announcement and board meeting are already done, so whether post-demerger value unlocking delivers further returns is hard to say — but "definitely a buy," and definitely no need to sell.
- **AstraZeneca Pharma** — **Hold**. An MNC and a decent company, but it only gives returns in the long term — don't expect great short-term returns. High-priced (above ~8,000, a Rs 2 paid-up share); steady performance, but not a stock where extraordinary wealth creation happens.
- **IDFC First Bank** — **Hold**. Called out as an investment idea long ago near 30-40 and it doubled; then a Haryana fraud/nexus scandal knocked it from near 200 to below 60. It is recovering, now 80-plus with the 52-week high at 87. Warburg Pincus and other majors get majority equity by next September — it will become more like a multinational bank than a domestic one. Hold.
- **Banaras Spinning (BSL)** — **Hold**. Ex-rights the stock fell below 20 and is stabilising at 27; "the worst is over." Textile companies are slowly improving — with patience it should get back to the mid-30s to 40 [as heard], where profits can be booked.
- **Sical Logistics** — **Hold (trailing stop ~78-80)**. Activity increased recently — from 60-65 around June 1 it went to 85-95 and is consolidating at 90 — but whether that activity sustains is hard to say. Keep a trading stop loss; it is somewhat far at 78, but keep it around 80 and hold.
- **NHPC** — **Hold**. Something of an underperformer: it touched 100-plus last year, corrected below 70, and is stabilising at 80. Remember a weak monsoon hurts NHPC — despite diversified green power, hydro is still the maximum revenue generator, so mild weakness is possible. But there is a regular large dividend play with a good yield: hold it as a steady dividend player rather than writing it off. Should not be sold at this rate.
- **Vedanta** — **Hold**. After the demerger the stock has come to its true value, but it is at low levels and has gone extremely low. If the metal cycle turns again it will easily reach 330-340. Hold.
- **LIC** — **Hold**. Stabilising at 440 post ex-bonus. Since listing it gave a selling opportunity only a couple of times (1,000-plus). More or less a reflection of the Indian capital market — LIC is itself the biggest domestic institutional investor in it. For the extreme long term, "definitely hold, without any doubt."
- **Olectra Greentech** — **Buy (on a 5-10% dip)**. The stock plays a continuous band: it comes to 1,250-1,240 and runs back to 1,460-1,470, where it faces resistance. For **momentum**, buy only after **1,450-1,460 is crossed**; for the **long term**, buy only when it comes near **1,275-1,280**, i.e. closer to 1,300.
- **Akums Drugs** — **Hold**. Recovered from 450-460 to 700; a generic player rather than a specialty-drugs company, and generics have notched up on good turnovers. Momentum is there, so hold — but if bought for the short term, definitely keep a trading stop loss, because in result season any disappointment can correct the stock immediately.
- **Piramal Pharma (PPL Pharma)** — **Buy (long term)**. Buy for the long term; short-term returns may not be great and the stock is still consolidating. Wait for the result — the last one was very disappointing but expectations are good this time; if a better result comes, start accumulating then.
- **Vishal Mega Mart** — **Avoid**. "The best of the times are over in it." It corrected close to 100, rose after the equity divestment to 130-140, and is consolidating at 117-118. Unless large retail names diversify well into quick commerce and sustain it, they give steady gains but there is "no scene of them creating spectacular wealth" — be it DMart or Vishal Mega Mart.
- **KP Energy** — **Buy**. A good promoter; the stock has corrected a lot recently and is attractive now. On the indicators the bottom is done at 250 and it is slowly inching up. Start accumulating slowly — but look at the result once. "Buy — and definitely KP Energy."
- **Vinati Organics** — **Buy (long term)**. "A huge wealth-creating stock" — definitely for the long term, definitely not for the short term.
- **"Ganga" [as heard]** — **Sell (book partial profits)**. A recently listed IPO stock that languished, then doubled from 120 to 200-plus in the April-June quarter alone. Booking partial profits here is advisable; for the rest, keep a trailing stop loss and hold, as there is momentum in the stock as of now.

**Deferred / no firm call:** **EV theme (viewer wanted two EV names for a few lakhs)** — he pushed back on the premise: investing blindly on an "EV" label is wrong, since the space spans module manufacturers, automakers and charging-station companies, and chasing whatever runs under an EV/AI/semiconductor theme is bubble territory. If already committed to staying invested in EV he named **TVS Motor** (two-wheelers), **M&M** (passenger vehicles) and **Tata Motors** as the good stocks — but as illustrations of quality within the theme rather than a fresh buy call, so they are excluded from `buys.json`. **Anant Raj (568), BDL (1,617), Canara Bank (157), Hindustan Zinc (732)** — a mailer 20-40% down on many names: "not-very-bad stocks, but the entry point was extremely wrong"; hold patiently and, as soon as his price is crossed, keep a trailing stop and protect capital — a general portfolio answer with no per-stock verdict, so excluded. **Mutual funds** — told a caller who had been sold Tata Momentum and a small-cap fund that speculative/momentum funds don't create long-term wealth; go for **multi-cap or flexi-cap** (HDFC, Nippon or Parag Parikh) — not a stock call. **SBFC Finance, PNB, Jai Bharat Maruti, First Fintech, Kalyani Forge** were asked but never answered on air.
