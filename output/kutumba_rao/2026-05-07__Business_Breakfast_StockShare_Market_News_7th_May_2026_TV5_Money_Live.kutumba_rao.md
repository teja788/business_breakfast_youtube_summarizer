## Market view
- **Yesterday's rally was late but genuine.** The market was dull — even negative around 1 o'clock, barely positive at 2 — and then, once crude-price-fall reports hit and European markets opened bubbly, **the Nifty jumped ~200 points in 10-15 minutes** and *held* there. "That is one positive thing; we must appreciate it."
- **Levels**: selling was expected between **24,300 and 24,500**; the market absorbed it and held. If it **sustains 24,300** and stays above, "we are clearly on the way to **25,000**." Unless **24,500** is crossed, don't call it decisively positive. Today's open may not bring the big jump everyone expects, since GIFT Nifty already ran in the last leg.
- **Options**: "the market is singing for good times." Calls piled at **24,500**; **25,000 became the highest OI** — in weeklies *and* monthlies — a possible indication **the rally continues all week** (not confirmed, observe carefully). Puts piled at **24,000**. **PCR reached 1.20** → bull confidence rising. **VIX fell to ~16**.
- **Yesterday's leaders**: government emergency credit line for airlines drove **IndiGo +6.7%**, **Tata Motors ~7%**; unusual movers **Asian Paints, Bajaj Auto +3-4%**; **Motherson Sumi** in Nifty Next 50; **Vedanta +5%** ("a good bet after the demerger", as he's said continuously). F&O: **a dozen stocks +5%** — **Coforge ~10%, SRF 8-9%, Force Motors 7%** — "not so easy."
- **Macro/flows**: Services **PMI at a 5-month high** = robust domestic demand. **S&P cut India GDP growth to 6.6%** — 6.6-7% is "decent enough." **DIIs bought ~Rs 7,000 cr**, highest in a month; FIIs buying via bulk deals. **Rupee** closed 70 paise stronger at **94.61**, best single-day gain in a month — watch whether it goes back to 94 or below 93.
- **Crude is the swing factor**: if no new Gulf negative surprises come and crude falls **below $100**, markets go forward more confidently.
- **The AI/MSCI gap**: **India's total MSCI weight is 11.94% vs TSMC alone at 14.2%**. Asia's two trillion-dollar companies (**TSMC, Samsung**) are both tech; when India gets one is "left to the imagination." **South Korea $4.18tn, Taiwan $4.61tn vs India ~$4.8-4.9tn** — "barely a striking distance", and "we should understand how shameful that is." Wants the government beyond the pari-passu 50% semiconductor mission funding to **back Indian companies making global takeovers**, or India reaches "an irrecoverable stage."
- **Cap-shifting is not to be ignored** — it drives mutual-fund buying/selling and allocation between small/mid/large. Demoted **mid → small**: **Kaynes, Physics Wallah, SJVN, Hexaware**. Upgraded **small → mid**: **Sona BLW, AIA Engineering, Navin Fluorine** (strong delivery buying recently). Upgraded **mid → large**: **Indian Hotels, Lodha Developers, Max Healthcare, Hitachi Energy, BSE, Jindal Steel**; **Groww** is now a large cap. Watch which sector's activity rises and which leaders in it rise — that's how you select outperformers.
- **Sectors**: **cement** is showing delivery buying — bigger infra push, and with an **extended summer / late monsoon**, offtake and **Q1 results will be good**.
- **Flow reads**: speculative longs in **HDFC Life, Amber, Nippon Life AMC, Coforge**; fresh shorts in **L&T Capital [as heard], United Spirits**; **conviction delivery buying in Apollo Hospitals and insurance — ICICI General, ICICI Prudential, HDFC Life, SBI Life**.
- **Bottom line**: **stay invested; good stocks can still be bought; buy on dips — but strictly quality stocks.**
- **Live flag**: paint companies rising suddenly on morning volumes — **observe carefully**. **Kansai Nerolac** good numbers; **Kansai, Berger, Apcotex +3-4%**, **Apcotex almost +20%**. Also noted **SMS Pharma** strong at Rs 421-422.

## Stock calls (viewer Q&A)
- **Havells** — **Buy**. Can definitely be bought for the medium-to-long term. He treats it as a **seasonal stock**: with their branding across fans and the rest, **the highest sales are in this summer**, so **Q1 will be good**. (Ramakrishna added a technical caveat — see closing note.)
- **AGI Greenpac** — **Buy**. Announced good numbers, and **since the results the stock has been doing better**; it is **forming higher tops and higher bottoms**. "Definitely buy."
- **Granules India** — **Buy (on decline)**. Not at the current price — it has run to its **52-week high**. It broke out of a long consolidation at **~600** and has since added ~150; **technically he wants a fall below 700** before picking it up. The **second generation of management is doing extremely well** and the shift to a younger management may bring changes. Market cap now near **Rs 20,000 cr**. A **silent performer** that Hyderabad-pharma coverage under-plays vs SMS, Laurus, Natco, Dr Reddy's.
- **Carborundum Universal** — **Buy (medium-to-long term)**. A **stable Murugappa Group stock**. "There is no excitement in this, but there will be stable returns," because the engineering space has only two or three stocks of this kind. Buy only for medium-to-long-term stable returns.
- **EPAM Systems** — **Avoid**. Asked by an EPAM software engineer about a 15% ESPP discount, he was "not in favour of investing in EPAM." Principle he repeats in workshops: **you know your own industry better than outsiders — rationalise your investments with that knowledge**; the mailer already knows his company is an underperformer, which is why he's seeking a second opinion. "Work in EPAM, take your salary there nicely, and invest that money in some other company."
- **Inox Wind** — **Sell (partial / book profits)**. Book **partial profits** on a holding bought at Rs 87. He doesn't think the stock performs greatly in the short term — **recent regulatory changes are not favourable to wind power companies** ("not negative, but not favourable either"). The move from **75 to 110** is a relief rally; better for the holder to exit somewhat here.
- **Micron (US)** — **Buy**. Asked about investing in US memory-chip makers, he said "certainly do it." **Micron could be a $1 trillion company in future and we needn't be surprised** — that fast-growing. The growth story looks likely to continue for **another three to five years**.
- **Kaynes Technologies** — **Sell / Exit (don't average)**. Don't average. **Exit even close to the buy price or at marginal losses**: ever since a broking house put out a **negative report**, the stock has recovered but **cannot cross 4,500**. Still a **high-PE stock at ~120 PE**.
- **Hospital / healthcare pick (Rs 50,000, medium term)** — **Buy**. Many hospital stocks are performing splendidly and naming just one is hard; he'd rather take **two, three or four**. **KIMS is one good choice**; also **Fortis Healthcare** and, in a leadership role, **Apollo Hospitals** — "all of these have created great wealth."
- **Visakha Industries** — **Hold**. Results aren't out yet, but **some interesting developments are happening** per his sources. Candid on governance: **the promoter is more busy with politics than managing the company** — he is a **minister in Telangana**, and both father and son have left the company aside. Even so, the stock **can touch 100 again**; **hold until the 52-week high**.
- **Vimta Labs** — **Hold**. Contradicting the anchor's "nothing great", he called them **definitely good results** — from a flat base it showed **~15% growth**, and on a **full-year basis the company achieved record revenue for the first time**. Growth from that record-revenue base "will be very easy." **After the recent correction the stock is looking very attractive at current levels.**

**Not counted as his calls:** **Ambuja Cements** — the caller asked about it but the description ("one of the leaders, in a leadership role in the cement sector") was the anchor's; Kutumba Rao's answer explicitly covered "these two stocks" (Havells, AGI Greenpac), so Ambuja is excluded from buys.json.
