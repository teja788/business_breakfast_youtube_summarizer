## Market view
- **Wait-and-watch until clarity.** This is a "known unknown" — the war was telegraphed for a month; running unhedged portfolios (no puts) is "reckless." Too early to call the bottom or to gauge how the war escalates / whether it broadens toward a world war.
- **Israel/US noise vs. economic reality.** Israel/US claim they killed Iran's top leadership and crippled its air force/navy, but there will be **far-reaching direct and indirect economic consequences**.
- **Gas/LNG risk > oil for India.** India's biggest dependence is **LNG from Qatar**; if LNG supply falls, the gas-dependent companies built over the last two decades face raw-material and production-cut risk. Crude impact is a given.
- **Inflation / oil shock.** Government assurances on stock have failed before in prolonged shocks — "a matter of time." ECB has already cautioned on a fresh inflation spike from the Middle East conflict. Watch if **crude crosses $100** — many negative consequences for the economy.
- **War economics.** US burning ~$4m missiles to intercept Iran's ~$20k drone-type missiles — watch whose fire-power lasts. US mid-term elections add pressure (Republicans "might lose very badly" if no resolution).
- **Flows / levels.** Monday selling was **not** as aggressive as feared — FIIs sold only **~Rs 3,300 cr** vs DII buying **~Rs 8,600 cr**; expect domestic support at lower levels. Monday pre-market was -800 pts but closed ~-300; today ~-550. If today's low breaks, **22,000 is on the radar** (already >8% off the top, bearish territory).
- **Gold / dollar.** **Dollar strength** is driving markets (esp. commodities); gold couldn't hold gains, trading ~$5,000. Doesn't expect a hyperbolic gold move unless central banks panic-buy and the dollar weakens. "Observe the dollar carefully."
- **Numbers check (vs Ukraine war).** At the Ukraine-war start Nifty was below 17,000 with crude ~$95 and **rupee 75/USD**; now rupee is **~91-92**, Brent ~$83-84, index still >24,000 — judge by the numbers whether we're still in bull territory or have correction scope.
- **Outlook:** **significant recovery likely after March** (Q4 results + war forecasts will reset expectations).

## Sector / tactical guidance
- **Tranche buying:** deploy ~¼ of intended capital per ~5% leg down; "cash is king" — preserve cash for rainy days.
- **Looks safe now:** **defense stocks, upstream oil companies, banks/financials/PSU banks**; selected non-precious **metal stocks**; good-earnings mid/small-caps — but very slowly.
- **Avoid:** **precious metals** for now (even safe havens saw selling).
- **Caution:** small **defense** stocks are over-played — India is still at the lower end of defense export capacity; auto sector and consumption may slow on higher fuel/energy costs.
- Buy only **deep value** with a **1-1.5 year** minimum horizon; stay on the sidelines and observe otherwise.
