## Market view
- Markets are **range-bound at the index level, but the broader market is strong** — clearly since May, with an amazing number of stocks giving **20–50% returns**. Wherever value shows up, investors are coming; index-only traders were disappointed over the last two months, but stock-specific traders "made ample money."
- **Old industrial groups** (Birla, Bajaj, Kirloskar; in AP: KCP, Andhra Sugars) are asset-rich; once **family disputes resolve** and a new generation reinvents the management, value unlocks and sharp/outperforming moves come (cf. Murugappa, TVS group). He flagged the **Kirloskar group** 3-4 months ago on this thesis; Kirloskar Oil hit upper circuit on a data-centre order.
- **Caution on large IPOs**: SpaceX's collapse (lifetime-high ~220 → ~150, within ~10% of issue price) is a warning for NSE/Jio-type Indian issues — avoid over-exuberance; book listing gains; buy only at correct valuations however good the company.
- **Reliance** leads the index most of the time; papers give a re-rating (Jio ~35% of embedded value, targets up to 1600). For the index to rise, Reliance (plus HDFC Bank / Bajaj group / Bharti Airtel) must rise. Otherwise stocks that rise one day don't rise the next.
- PSU-defence rally may signal a **moderate trading rally in beaten-down railway stocks** — only a trading rally, not an investment rally; observe carefully. Watch the **series roll-overs** (Q1 results ahead will reward/punish stocks). Options writing more at 24,500.
- Fresh weakness: **Varun Beverages, Asian Paints and other paint stocks**. Gainers: **Mankind**, delivery/AB Capital counters near new highs.
- Overall: **buy on declines / buy on dips**, no panic; broader range **23,800–24,600**; watch VIX and PCR. Expects crude cooling (maybe below 70) to help India.
- **Recent stock-specific winners he cited** (past picks / favourites, performance recap over ~1 month — not fresh calls today): Allcargo Logistics +50%, Kirloskar Oil +42-43%, HFCL +41%, Tejas Networks +34%, **Netweb Technologies +33% ("my favourite")**, **IFCI ("Kutumba Rao's favourite")**, Inox India, Kirloskar Brothers, New India Assurance, Sirma SGS, Redington, Ultra Green Tech +26%, **Standard Glass/Standard Engineering** (132→200; he'd said buy Standard Glass over HLE Glascoat). **GNG Electronics** and **Clean Max** — targets already hit, still steam / good potential.
