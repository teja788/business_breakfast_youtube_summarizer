## Market view / levels
- A **fear has entered the market**, especially after the PM's austerity remarks; the feeling that the true state of the economy is weak is spreading.
- **23,500 was cut yesterday** — "very negative," because the maximum put options were piled up exactly there. Now there's a real possibility of slipping into the **22,600–23,500** band. If today doesn't close **firmly above 23,500**, "we are in for a very rough time" in the extreme short term.
- Mode has flipped from **"buy on declines" to "sell on rallies"** — and closer to **24,000** it becomes sell-on-rallies. Stay alert/cautious, **protect profits, set trailing stop-losses**. In value stocks, buy only **token quantities** — do not buy aggressively just because "value has come."
- **Crude**: even if the war stops, crude won't reset to old Rs 50–60. With the **Strait of Hormuz** shifting from free to paid travel, **80–90 dollars/barrel** could be the new normal; the whole economy must reset to it.
- **LNG**: now ~**70 dollars/MWh** — "dangerous"; a sharp rise would hit LNG-using industries.
- **Fuel inflation** will come through — if not today then tomorrow — hitting the common man via transport costs.
- Market is now **totally dependent on the monsoon**; if El Nino hits in this weak macro, another **1,000–2,000 Nifty points** down "shouldn't surprise us" (all ifs-and-buts, not fear-mongering).
- **Trump's China trip today** is the one speculator-positive; a successful trip could let global markets recover and give some relief.
- **Gold**: "forget it" — demand falling will become normal; people will prioritize **capital preservation over safety**, and capital preservation "cannot be done just by buying gold." Expects a **gold policy** from the government — notes PSU banks' ~Rs 1.9 lakh crore profit leans on a ~Rs 13 lakh crore gold-loan book (~Rs 35–40k crore of profit), with the interest burden falling mainly on semi-urban/rural borrowers.
- Mid/small-caps fell more than benchmarks — earlier exuberance (BSE 52-week highs filling ~15 pages vs no 52-week lows) had warned of correction; that correction has now arrived.
- Calls for "**economic patriotism**" and **cost optimization** at the family level, not just for companies.
- On Prabhu Kishore / Varun Group (guest): suggested they **institutionalize the business** and consider going public to prepare for the **generational change** — a public issue "is not only for getting money."

## Stock calls (viewer Q&A)

### Infosys — Avoid / Sell on rally (held since 2004, ~300 shares)
- "Come out of these IT stocks" — repeated; **sell Infosys on a rally**. The caller has held through a prolonged period and can't catch the sector shift in time, so **lighten the position on rallies and widen/diversify the portfolio**.
- Reasoning: things are changing; IT won't behave as before unless companies reinvent their business models. Not a time to clutch one multi-bagger.
- Where to redeploy (his preference): a little into **Adani Group** stocks (e.g., a couple — **Adani Ports** or similar), a couple of **power stocks** (rates moderating, peaks reached), and some **new-generation stocks** (mentioned a name transcribed as "Factor Analytics").

### Dixon Technologies — Hold (long-term query)
- Rose after a **weak** result because the result was **discounted in advance**; normally such a weak/sharp net-profit miss should have dropped the stock ~8–10%, but it instead recovered with ~1% gains today — the market is discounting the **future**.
- **Hold** as long as it holds the **10,000** mark; the stock can rally to **11,400–11,500**, where he'd say **exit**.

### Retail strategy (Davaluri Mohan, by mail) — keep some cash, don't stop SIPs
- Always keep **some portion of cash in hand** for opportunities. But **do not stop SIPs** — the whole point of an SIP is rupee-cost averaging (buy cheaper when markets fall, higher when they rise). Stopping SIPs in a bear market means "you haven't understood equity markets."
