## Market view
- **Markets will be weak and on the lower side.** Thursday's fall did not surprise him after the first hour — he had flagged that if Nifty couldn't hold above **25,800**, a re-test of **25,500** was likely, and that is what happened.
- Multiple negatives stacked up — **rising crude** (fiscal-deficit pressure), a **strong dollar index** — producing a "mild panic / twister." Whether it was overdone will be clear over the **next 2–3 sessions**: markets climbed gently over three days "like steps," then dropped in one day "like a lift."
- He would **not rule out a fresh downward move** after an actual strike on Iran; **confidence comes only after a bottom**. The US is also running out of time in the Gulf (two carriers deployed 7–8 months; rarely beyond nine), so an **imminent strike is possible any time** — markets "dance to Trump's steps," so guessing is hard.
- **Stance: wait-and-watch; aggressive buying is unwarranted** until Middle East developments give clarity. Sharp moves will be **stock-specific**, not a broad directional market move, given current **risk aversion**.
