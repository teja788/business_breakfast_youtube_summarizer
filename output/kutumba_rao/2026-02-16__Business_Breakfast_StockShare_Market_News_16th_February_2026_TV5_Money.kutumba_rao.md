## Market view
- **No significant recovery expected today, but it may stabilise.** Friday's sell-off was more than expected, and the weekend gave the market time to digest it.
- **IT is the whole story.** General consensus from analysts and industry experts: **the worst is not over, but panic selling at the current stage is not needed.** How far the "**Anthropic shock**" extends is very difficult to say. One broking house called IT services firms the "**indispensable plumbers of the tech world**" — and after this correction **TCS and Infosys are positioned in their best place in a decade on dividend yield**, better than even Covid. **Value is emerging**; these companies have **huge reserves**, and the market will watch closely how they absorb the shock and reinvent. **Once IT stabilises, the market will slowly travel back toward 26,000.**
- **Metals** also corrected badly (Hindalco cited). **Once metals recover a little, the market stabilises.**
- **Levels**: expects **25,400 to sustain** today; a mild recovery **crossing 25,600** puts markets **definitively into the positive zone**. Options: max calls at **26,000**, then **25,600** — only a 25,600 cross brings steadiness. Puts piled at **25,500**; if it stays below 25,500 for long, he thinks the market **attempts 25,000 once** (both call and put writing plus put buying are heavy there — "observe that"). **PCR is at 0.65, well below 0.7** → a good advantage for **positional traders; contrarians can buy here** — purely for a positional trade. **VIX up ~13.37% to 13.3** → "a little fear has started"; watch whether the weakness gets prolonged.
- **The "ifs and buts"**: the **US is still moving more navy ships toward Iran** — a sudden **US attack on Iran** is a live risk.
- **Mutual funds turned net sellers — Rs 4,100 cr, the first in three years** (no selling since roughly '23). "**This is not a small thing**" — where they used to buy Rs 25,000-30,000 cr, net selling of Rs 4,000 cr is a **weak signal** and proof the funds themselves are being watchful because the market's direction is unclear.
- **Flows**: **FIIs sold Rs 7,400 cr Friday** (not a small quantity); **DIIs bought Rs 5,500 cr but could not stop the fall**. Still, **FIIs are net buyers in February so far** — that is somewhat positive.
- **From here markets move stock-specific more than sector-specific.** After the **Indo-US deal internals** emerge, expect **reworking and sharp moves in some sectors**.
- **Discipline**: **stick only to stocks with fundamentals that are generating cash, for the long term. Value-driven discipline is very important now.** Do **not get carried away and buy into false recoveries**. Overall: **stay invested; contrarian buying; attempt positional trading in tech stocks a little at a time — but with a strict stop loss.**
- **F&O positioning**: speculative longs built in **PPL Pharma, Motilal, Persistent, SBI**; shorts in **Coforge, Muthoot, Tata Elxsi [as heard], Swiggy, HUDCO**; delivery buying in **HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, HDFC AMC, L&T**. Little activity overall — the **dollar** moved in a very narrow band, closing **90.66** (next reference 90.25).
- **On today's open**: only **25-30 points** of weakness — the market should even recover; **Wipro, TCS, HCL Tech performing better** (will they sustain?), **Infosys weak** — analysis seems to be running on which company within IT is more impacted by AI. **Pharma better** (Sun Pharma, Cipla). Market "looking very flat", making a positive attempt.
- **Results punishment on display**: **Penar Industries -15%** at one go on a weak result — "if the result is weak, one can understand what the punishment will be like". **Blue Jet -10%**. A recent listing he had **cautioned about at listing** ("companies like this do not stand") is now **-8%**.
- **Mutual funds / SIPs (caller Q)**: **"Don't stop your mutual fund investments or your SIP — continue them."** His remarks were about the funds' *investment pattern*, not against them; they must invest optimally and only where there is value, so buying is naturally lighter at results time.
- **On being asked to name "two mid/small caps on the spot"**: refused — **"saying a couple of stocks immediately, whatever comes to the mouth, is not correct."** Pointed viewers to the researched New Year / Diwali special-programme recommendations, and said to **buy those where the story is unchanged and results came in line with expectations**. Cited **December picks GMR Power, NLC, MTAR, Sagility** — **MTAR** rose strongly and touched a **52-week high** even in a weak market.
- **Heritage / Sakshi**: a long defence against allegations tying Heritage to **Indapur Dairy** and **Bole Baba Dholpur Dairy**. Key facts he gave: Heritage has **15 manufacturing + 5 co-manufacturing locations** (4 of the 5 in Maharashtra, 1 in Telangana), all disclosed on the website and in the annual report, with new agreements reported to the exchanges — **total transparency**. Indapur is **only a co-manufacturing location** (a B2B firm, owner of **Asia's biggest milk spray dryer**, whose clients include HUL, Britannia, Patanjali, Lactalis); Heritage makes only **skimmed milk powder, UHT milk, paneer and cheese** there — **no ghee at all**, so the TTD-ghee allegation is baseless. **Dholpur Dairy issued a rejoinder** denying ever supplying ghee to anyone or any temple; the **CBI matter concerns a different Bole Baba dairy** (the Jain family's). He called the paper's conduct that of a "**digital terrorist / cyber terrorist**", said **defamation and criminal suits from the company and from investors are inevitable**, and praised **Minister Nara Lokesh** for pursuing his own defamation case intensively while Sakshi takes the postponements.
