## Market view
- No major recovery expected for today.
- The market defended the 23,000 level on the downside; if it holds again today, watch whether Nifty crosses 23,300 on the upside.
- The market is slowly developing an "unknown fear" / hidden weakness, so caution is rising — caution should be the watchword (as said the prior morning).
- Today likely range-bound; even if it opens with mild weakness and recovers, fresh positions are NOT advisable until Nifty crosses 23,300.
- Stocks react selectively even against a weak market — examples cited from the prior session: CCL Products (+~10%, closed near 52-week high), Thomas Cook and other travel names rising, Exicom (speculative counter, ~+10% on ~7.5 cr volume), and OCL (one of the largest sulphur producers; ~+15% on sulphur-price appreciation post-results). One must distinguish one-off moves from sustainable ones.
- Long-term context (citing Mowniika Halan's chart shown in his workshop): investing Rs 1 lakh/year for 35 years even at each year's Sensex peak turns ~Rs 35 lakh into Rs 3 crore+ (~11% CAGR) — better than the "safest" options like Sukanya Samriddhi. SIPs can disappoint over a 3–4 year down cycle but outperform over a long horizon. Present market has multiple unprecedented adverse factors (Trump-driven tariffs/war/global scenario shifts not seen in 30–40 year cycles), so traditional thinking won't work; his upcoming workshop covers out-of-the-box ideas, risk management, and how to value stocks in a rough market (presentation ~30–40 slides).
- Mazagon Dock / Andhra Pradesh shipbuilding cluster: the news has been around ~1.5 months. AP already has central-government shipbuilding-cluster clearance for the East Coast. Not only Mazagon — possibly another Korean company and even a Russian company may participate. Roughly a Rs 28,000 cr cluster project. The East Coast is set to become the gateway for central and northern India; Tamil Nadu's loss is Andhra Pradesh's gain.
