## Market view
- **No surprise in the gap-down.** Markets are swinging purely on geopolitical newsflow. With Trump on top, "you can expect more and more news like this" — and where there is **uncertainty rather than certainty, markets cannot trend**, they behave exactly like this.
- **Crude is the transmission channel**: it fell to **~$97-98** yesterday, then jumped to **$104-105** (**+4-5%**) on the speech; Brent **~$107**. The number people ignore is the **Dubai spot at ~$124-125**. Because of logistics and proximity/dependence on the Middle East, **the crude bomb hits Asian economies harder** — India, China, Japan. This impact "is not going to go away right now."
- **Macro is only consolation.** GST at **~8.4%** growth (with refunds factored in) is fine, but **"the worst is still ahead"** — it is decided that the markets and the economy will be impacted. **Alertness is very much needed.** FII selling **-Rs 8,300 cr** vs DII **+Rs 7,000 cr** is the simple illustration.
- **The India-vs-Nvidia datapoint**: a year ago India's **total market cap was double** US-listed Nvidia's; **today they are equal**. Meanwhile US companies move into the safety zone fast — **Oracle** cut jobs and is restructuring globally.
- **Two issues will dictate the Indian market for all of FY27**: (1) **geopolitics** and (2) **AI disruption**. IT services has already been hit by both.
- **Sectors**:
  - **Banks** — corrected a lot; deposit repricing may let NIMs sustain, so they have "literally come somewhat to the bottom"; could be somewhat better, but real upside needs **private capex** to pick up.
  - **FMCG** — raw-material costs stabilising and agri-commodity prices down, so **FY27 earnings visibility is improving**; not outstanding outperformance but **stable**. Watch segments like **edible oils** carefully.
  - **Oil marketing companies** — **assume the worst days are still ahead**; however much the government manages until the elections, after that "the oil bomb" will inevitably be placed in the public's hands.
  - **IT** — down **>24% in 2026 alone** and **>20% YoY**. Only companies that **reinvent themselves** rather than sticking to traditional IT services will sustain; among them **Infosys, HCL Tech, Tech Mahindra** could do a little better, and in mid-tier **Coforge, Persistent**.
  - **Pharma** — resilient so far, but **cost pressures will rise**. Patent-expiry plays (e.g. semaglutide) may add turnover but he doubts meaningful **margins**, given competition and rapid commoditisation. Only those that **sustain margins as costs rise** will perform.
- **Levels**: **until 23,000 is crossed the market will not improve**; on the downside, **if 22,000 is cut there are indications of an even more terrible fall**. "You cannot take anything for granted in the market at this time."
- **Rupee**: certain to **close above 95** today — rising crude brings purchase and forex pressure. **Precious metals** look like they will improve a little.
- **America has washed its hands of it** — set the fire in West Asia and escaped; its **credibility is gone** and it risks becoming a **loner in the global economy**. Stagflation will reach the US too; a couple of private finance firms there are already going bankrupt, and a cascading effect could hit the finance market. **If America starts to sink, prepare for a long bear phase.**
- **Strategy**: **stay invested only in fundamentally sound stocks** that can sustain a bear market — companies that have already done their capex and where returns have come, or where earnings visibility exists despite the uncertainty. **For accumulation, prefer companies with large cash reserves**, which must sustain until this phase is crossed.
- **Distrust the news-driven pops**: shipping names (**Garden Reach** and the like) rose **15-20%** yesterday, but he doubts they sustain — such short-term rallies "come back to realistic prices and settle." Outstanding returns from here also need good **quarter-on-quarter results**. **Caution is the watchword.**
- **Timeline**: unless the Republicans take a big beating in the **US mid-terms**, Trump's attitude may not change — so prepare for **six to eight more months** of uncertainty-driven markets.

## Stock calls (viewer Q&A)

## Closing note — calls by OTHER analysts (NOT Kutumba Rao; excluded from buys.json)

Indian IT went **complacent** over the last 3-5 years, fell behind on new technology, neither invented nor acquired, and **wasted time on buybacks**. They do have good cash reserves built over 15-20 years and **can adapt — but they have missed the best of times**. The **next one to two years are crucial**; only after they state their roadmap clearly should investors think about them.
- **Vedanta** — **Hold** (viewer holds 150 shares at 495 into the demerger); view unchanged from earlier.

- **Home loan vs market** (non-stock) — if the loan is at a **premature stage** the interest component is higher, so **clear it first**; if it's near the end, investing in the market is better.
