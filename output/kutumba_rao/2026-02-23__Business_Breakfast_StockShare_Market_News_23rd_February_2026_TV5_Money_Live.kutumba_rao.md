## Market view (weekend developments / tariffs)
- Just when stability and certainty seemed to be returning, **uncertainty is back on screen**. Even after the US Supreme Court struck down Trump's IEEPA tariffs, he is routing around it with new sections, so the uncertainty won't disappear yet.
- **Positives though**: the **effective tariff rate fell from 16% (pre-judgment) to ~13.7%** after the 15% move — about **2.3% lower**. Nearly **90 countries** were at 10% and now move to 15%; countries like Australia, UK, Singapore, UAE (and the UK's 0% deal rate) all move to 15% — so they **come into India's level playing field**.
- **Section 122 is valid only 150 days** (ordinance-like). Beyond that, extension needs another section (unfair trade practices / Section 232 / 301) or a **Congress vote** — which puts focus on **US midterm elections** (tariffs blamed for inflation; a big question mark for Republicans). ~**$175bn** of collected tariffs may face refund-related court action. Treasury Secretary **Scott Bessent** flagged **Section 338 of the 1930 Tariff Act** (could allow up to ~50%). Net: trade-front uncertainty "might continue for some more time," and as the biggest economy the US sets the tune.

## Domestic / earnings view
- **Q3 results complete**: revenue growth has **stabilized** but **profitability is under pressure**. From here, **only companies that improve profitability and have earnings visibility will outperform**.
- AI-summit positive sentiment is bringing **billions in money commitments**, but whether benefits are temporary or long-term — and how the government tackles it — must be watched.
- **FIIs**: ~**Rs 16,000 cr** net buying in February, but a **second successive week of modest selling**. On the day FIIs sold ~**Rs 1,000 cr**, DIIs bought ~**Rs 2,700 cr** — not much to worry about. Last week Nifty/Sensex +~0.2%, mid-cap +0.4%, small-cap −~0.5%; **IT sold aggressively** by FIIs.
- **Rupee** depreciating again toward **91/$** (geopolitics + crude); even crossing 91 shouldn't surprise. India is a large crude importer, so a stronger dollar + higher crude is a risk to the deficit.
