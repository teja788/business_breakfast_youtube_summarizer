## Market view
- Market stays in **consolidation mode**, tight **23,500–24,000 range**. A breakout needs a significant geopolitical development (Islamabad talks, Lebanon/Israel) or crude closing **at/below 90**.
- **Crude is the key swing factor:** Dubai spot ~$124–126 (vs futures) — physical premium is high; until spot falls toward ~110/below, hardships continue. Local refiners restricting fuel pumps (Reliance: don't dispense >Rs 1,000) signals how deep the problem is. *"Don't just look at headlines — understand the ground reality; markets discount in advance."*
- **Second big negative: the monsoon.** Forecast ~40% below normal → ~30% drought risk; agriculture has been a strong pillar for 3–4 years. Also fertiliser shortage + West Asia effect → margin pressure on that sector.
- **Rupee strong** on RBI forex restrictions, but the April 10 long-exit deadline is today — watch if rupee weakness returns from tomorrow.
- **Weakest-sold sectors:** financials (led by HDFC), then autos, then construction/metals — watch where recovery/institutional interest returns first. [Poonawalla] QIP (~Rs 2,500 cr @ ~390) subscription is a tell on whether financials have bottomed.
- **Stabilisation overall;** if crude eases over 2–3 months, market stabilises and slowly trends up — caveat is the monsoon.
- **Recruitment tell:** Info Edge (Naukri) Q4 billings "very muted" → corporates cautious; "tough times running." Watch the billings drop, not just the broker downgrade.
- **Benchmarks:** above **24,000** = strengthening; below **23,500** = weakening (most calls at 24,500/24,000, most puts at 23,500). PCR fell below 1 to **0.97** (profit-booking after the 5-day rally); **VIX ~20.43** — bulls confident only if it closes toward 15.
- **FII selling easing** (modest funds flow). US markets flat/wait-and-watch; Asia slightly positive (Japan +~0.5%) — possible spill-over, Nifty may re-attempt 24,000 intraday.
- Speculative longs: **Nippon AMC, Motilal Oswal, Cochin Shipyard.** Shorts: RKEC, [Fintech], VMH, [Andhra] Enterprises. Delivery: **United Spirits** (RCB sale + election liquor sales → possible decent short-term return), Asian Paints, Cholamandalam Finance.
- **Be careful with new/low-liquidity counters** running on news (ITDC +20%, ITI +10%, Apollo Micro +11% on 5cr-share volume from a bullish business update) — moves often fade; avoid "cats and dogs," stick to good-fundamental stocks.

## On large-cap IT
- **TCS** in-line in rupee terms, sequentially a bit better, but dollar/constant-currency weak; **"best of times for large-cap IT are over" unless they change the service model.**
- **Wipro buyback (~Rs 16,000 cr): "a very bad move."** Should conserve cash, do a big-bang AI expansion / let an AI firm take over and reinvent — returning money to satisfy investors one-time won't hold the price.
- Added fear: **Tata Group boardroom battles** (Tata Sons — Vijay Singh / Venu Srinivasan / Chandrasekaran extension) could weigh on the group's market cap over 1–2 years; rifts since Ratan Tata's passing.
