Kutumba Rao joined from Vijayawada and gave the fundamental/markets view plus viewer stock Q&A. (Technical calls that day were given by Ramakrishna, and one financials switch by Kranthi — those are kept out of his calls below; see the closing note.)

## Market view
- **22,000 on the horizon for Nifty** — reaffirmed a call he made on Monday. Frames it off the Ukraine-war base: when that war started (Feb '22) Nifty was only ~17,000; markets absorbed four years of war plus tariffs and made new highs above 26,000, so a correction back toward a 22,000 area is in the cards. Adds the lower scenarios being floated (20,000, even 18,000 if very pessimistic).
- **It's a one-sided, externally-driven fall** — until the external factors (war, crude) change, the market won't reverse; **catching this falling knife is very difficult.** Wait and watch; no daredevilry until things settle.
- **Crude/recession risk:** if a global recession comes it's because of crude prices, and America is the country most responsible and will suffer most (fuel at $2.60-$3.90/gallon in some US states). Iran now holds the war in its own hands; when it stops is uncertain.
- **Rupee at a lifetime low 92.37** — wouldn't be surprised at 95-96. As long as the rupee keeps falling, **FIIs won't come in even if pockets of strength exist.** DIIs buy only up to a point; once redemptions/withdrawals start, DII buying ends too. Yesterday **FIIs sold >Rs 7,000 cr vs DIIs bought Rs 7,500 cr** — how long DIIs can match is the question.
- **Banks re-rating:** flagged earlier that all five top banks opening negative was the indicator; the multi-year bank outperformance is unwinding, **large banks suffer first**, multiple banks hitting 52-week lows together. Macro hit via manufacturing slowdown → rising NPAs.
- **Options map:** Nifty calls building 24,000-24,300 (a dead-cat bounce needs 24,000 calls to build); heavy puts at 23,000; failure to sustain 24,500 → can attempt 23,000. Bank Nifty calls 56,000 / puts 55,000 — breach of 55,000 means more aggravated bank selling. **PCR ~0.886** (daredevilry only near 0.7); **VIX ~21.5-22** — very uncomfortable for bulls.
- **Strategy:** on small rallies exit weaker stocks, stick to stronger names; prefer minimal-crude-impact sectors — **pharma, home staples, consumption**; **stay invested, don't panic.** Expects a **temporary bottom around 23,200-23,500.**

## Energy / power theme (his strongest positive flag)
- Keep **energy/power stocks on the radar** — summer peak demand cited at **270.1 GW**, gas-fired output may be cut ~10 GW.
- Favors **merchant power producers without PPA** — **JSW Energy** (rose 7%) and **NTPC Green** (rose >12% to 97) — buy on declines/dips. Notes Adani names have more PPAs.
- **KPI Green** — said before the KPI Group is doing well; could be a good bet in a correction back to **370-380**.

## Stock calls (viewer Q&A)
- **Reliance Industries — Accumulate (slowly), long-term.** The US $300 billion investment is not the reason to buy; the case is sum-of-the-parts value (Jio divestment/possible IPO, retail) and AI developments that are relevant here will have more impact on the stock. "Start accumulating slowly" — it's somewhat resilient vs several other stocks — but with a long-term horizon. Noted Jio IPO preparations have begun and an NSC IPO rumor (~Rs 20,000 cr size, ~Rs 5 lakh cr market cap).
- **Mahindra & Mahindra (M&M) — Hold / Accumulate.** Don't view it as just an automobile stock; it has **defense exposure** and is a diversified engineering stock. Hold; fresh buying is fine; "if you ask me, accumulate." (Viewer holds at Rs 1,700.)
- **Gitanjali Gems / Kingfisher Airlines / Mercator / GVK Power / Lanco / Alliance — Avoid (untradeable).** Asked to add to the anchor's answer, he confirmed there is **no liquidity at all — these are completely dead and don't even trade.** No way to sell them (so they can't be used for an LTCG set-off either).
