Kutumba Rao was present and named throughout; he was the markets/fundamentals analyst answering Vasanth's questions and viewer Q&A (alongside Kranthi on fundamentals and Ramakrishna on technicals).

## Market view
- The **panic in oil markets will spill over onto economies**. This morning **Japan fell ~7%**, China ~3%+, **US futures negative by 1,000+ points**, and even **gold ~-2%, near ~5050 dollars/oz**.
- This **oil shock will have a medium-to-long-term impact** on every country in different ways; "ignore it at your own peril."
- The game changed once **Israel struck Iran's oil infrastructure**; the market was "too complacent" about supply-chain risk and **took a short war for granted** — by the time it realises this is long-term, "the auspicious time has passed."
- **Qatar invoked force majeure** on old gas contracts; the **UAE/Gulf safe zones are now unsafe zones** — "we are in for very, very rough times."
- He doubts the courage to "buy when there's blood on the streets": buying too early just builds losses; re-entering risks missing the bounce. The market is **totally red**.
- **Flows:** DIIs bought **>50,000 crore in the past week** (yet investors still sit on losses); **FIIs sold >20,000 crore in 4 sessions** and are in the "safer zone." **Recovery is difficult until FII support returns.**
- **Macro:** CAD likely to **jump/widen sharply**; every **10 dollars on crude ≈ ~1% GDP hit**, so crude up ~**60 dollars ⇒ ~3% GDP impact** (unless the economy proves more resilient). India is "**one of the most vulnerable nations, after Japan**."
- **Indo-US deal** likely pushed out **3-4 months** (US Supreme Court tariff judgment / refunds-revisions); the March deadline is effectively shelved. He says the US is the "one country responsible" for India's current problems and India has "been too soft." **Just wait and watch — no need to rush and buy.**

## Levels / OI
- **Nifty support 24,300, then 24,000** (puts heavily accumulated at 24,000); expects a **600-700 pt gap-down**, possibly testing **24,000**. If 24,000 breaks → "extremely rough"; he earlier flagged 22,000 on the horizon but is **not yet taking that extreme view** — hopes 24,000 holds.
- **India VIX ~19.88-20** vs bulls' comfort zone of **12-13** = a dangerous zone. **Put-call ratio ~0.84** (weak); wants to see it below 0.7.
- **Shorts built up** in resilient bank stocks: **Federal Bank, SBI, ICICI, Union Bank, Canara Bank** — be afraid of further correction there.
- **Selective short-covering** in large-caps that can benefit: **Reliance, National Aluminium, Mazagon Dock** (ignore the F&O ban-list noise; **Sammaan Capital** is in the ban list).

## Sectors he flagged
- **Pockets of safety:** **pharma** (named **Natco** and **Laurus** as likely outperformers this week) and **essential-commodity manufacturers**; broadly **defence, upstream oil, and partly sugar** (oil-proxy) can swim against the tide. Collateral damage everywhere else.
- **Avoid / under pressure when oil rises:** aviation, paints, chemicals, logistics; plus gas-dependent fertilisers, ceramics, autos, and EMS on supply-chain disruption.
- **Posture:** extremely defensive, **stock-specific, strict stop-loss, protect existing gains, risk management is key.** **Sell on rallies / dead-cat bounces** (the earlier "buy on dips" stance is over). **Do not catch the falling knife.**

## Portfolio hedging guidance
- For a caller (Satish) holding Bajaj Finance / Eternal etc., Kutumba Rao said these are good stocks and advised **hedging the portfolio with Nifty puts** — illustration: a ~1 crore portfolio could buy a ~**26,000 put (~8 lots)** and hold it until the market bottoms (like buying insurance). Stock-level option hedges (selling OTM calls / writing OTM puts) are possible **only with experience**. Don't hedge "just for the sake of it."
