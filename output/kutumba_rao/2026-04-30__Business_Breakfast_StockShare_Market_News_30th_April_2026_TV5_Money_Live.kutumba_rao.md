## Market view (open)
- Today's gap-down is **expected** — when Trump reposted (with an AI image) that the Strait of Hormuz blockade continues, crude jumped immediately. Rising crude is well-known to be very negative for India; **medium-term impact will be big**, so markets are reacting, though moves are **more stock-specific**.
- **April was extraordinary**: Sensex ~**+8%**, Nifty **+8.5%**; **small-cap index +18.56% (best month in 14 years)**, mid-cap **+13.84% (best in 6 years, best since Covid)**. Treat this as either a mini-bubble or a comeback rally from under-ownership. **Weaker, high-PE stocks with no earnings visibility have led** — use the rally to **exit those**, but don't assume small/mid-caps keep rising forever.
- Not isolated to India — example: a US "Car Rental" large-cap ran **$150 → $840** (late March to mid-April) then crashed to **$180** in 3-4 sessions; such sharp round-trips will show up in India too.
- **US**: Magnificent Seven results in line but after-market MSFT -2%, Meta -5%, Amazon -2%. Fed held; West Asia clouds inflation; chatter of a possible **hike** before year-end; **four FOMC dissents** (highest on record). **Mag-7 market cap ~$22 trillion** exceeds all other world markets combined; global FIIs crowd into AI; **South Korea's market cap overtook the UK's** on semis/AI.
