## Market view
- **No sustained recovery / rebound is ruled out.** Any rebound today is only **partial / technical** (echoing the Asian bounce — Japan +4% after 4 down days, Hang Seng ~+2%). It will be **intermittent and news-driven** and is likely to **fizzle out** unless there is a clear-cut closure to the war (e.g., a regime change bringing stability in Iran). He calls it a **losing situation for both sides** and warns the Middle East crisis is the **one piece of news governing the market**.
- **Crude / inflation:** as long as the Middle East crisis runs, there's an indirect **inflation effect worldwide, especially on oil-importing nations**.
- **Rupee:** opened **91.57** (~60 paisa recovery from 92) on **RBI sustained intervention** (RBI also selling bonds; concern rising for RBI). Watch how long intervention can last — weakness could set back in. A **~91.5 close** would be "no problem"; continued rupee weakness means no sustained market recovery.
- **US overnight** jumped euphorically at first but ended only ~+200 pts on Dow/Nasdaq, propped largely by **Bitcoin stocks** — not convincing.
- **Sugar:** crude-driven ethanol-mixing theme, but selective — depends on capacity, quota system (govt recently changed quotas), inventory. **Renuka is the largest producer** (+7% yesterday); broader sugar names didn't show sustained recovery, so they'll just **hold at higher levels**.
- **Breadth of the fall:** yesterday's sell-off was **broad-based** (metals, autos, cement, pharma, select consumption like HUL). If the recovery is not equally broad-based, it won't hold.
- **Strategy:** **stay on the sidelines, wait-and-watch.** Only in **deep-value** stocks buy about **25%**, and only with the willingness to **wait 1 to 1.5 years**. Trader Trump's tanker/Hormuz assurances don't yet inspire confidence (cited the Iran warship shot down by a US submarine in the Indian Ocean).
