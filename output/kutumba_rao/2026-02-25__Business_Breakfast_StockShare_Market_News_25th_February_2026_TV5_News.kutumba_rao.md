## Market view
- **No near-term recovery hope.** Markets will stay in a broad, pendulum-like trading range; global/tech events unfold faster than the market can digest and react. "It may take some more time, without any doubt — no need to be in a hurry."
- **Contrarian buyer.** "When there is blood on the street, that's when I buy." He was a buyer yesterday after 2:30. Will do bottom-fishing where there is value, but warns against buying merely because prices fell.
- **IT-sector damage is historic.** Nifty IT's worst monthly fall in ~23 years (since the 2000-02 dot-com bust); leading IT stocks down >20% on the month. Whether short-lived or long-term needs careful observation. Today's IT pop is likely **short-covering** — huge short positions plus delivery-based selling had built up.
- **Breadth very weak:** ~2,900 decliners vs 1,344 advancers yesterday; stripping penny/no-volume names, fewer than ~300 active stocks actually rose.
- **Panic was retail, not institutional:** FIIs net-sold ~Rs 100 cr, DIIs bought ~Rs 3,000 cr. Retail-investor sentiment now controls the market; everything else is secondary.
- **Momentum strategy** has worked, but only for traders / six-month position traders — not earnings-based investors.

## OI / levels / cross-asset
- Nifty call rollovers at **26,000**; huge call build-up at **25,500** (yesterday's closest strike, market closed below it). **25,500 may become a base** — if it holds above on a gap-up, market holds; if it can't cross 25,500, caution advised and expects a **retest of 25,000** (puts heaviest at 25,000).
- **PCR ~0.9** (below 1); "nothing to worry as of now." For position traders, **0.7-0.9 is a good buying zone**.
- **VIX still >14** despite a modest dip — a concern (flat, not cooled).
- Some bottoming feeling in **IDFC First, Premier Energies**. New-series short positions building in Saman Capital, Coforge, Eternal, Paytm — these likely to meet selling on rallies. Rollovers concentrated in fundamentally sound names (L&T, UltraTech Cement, Alkem, Max Health); no F&O bans.
- **Rupee** ~90.95 (RBI support kept it below 91); yuan strengthening vs dollar. **Gold** steady/resilient — do NOT short it given fragile geopolitics (possible Iran attack could push gold to retest recent highs).
- **Power companies:** good times due to data-center demand, but global backlash on rising retail bills; views Trump's "rate-payer protection pledge" (data-center owners run their own power) as a positive.
- Net stance: **buy on declines** hopefully if today's recovery lasts; do NOT attempt to sell after sharp moves at these levels.
