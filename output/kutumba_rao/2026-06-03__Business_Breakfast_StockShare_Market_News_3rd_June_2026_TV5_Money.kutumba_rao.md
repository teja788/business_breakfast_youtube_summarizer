## Market view

- **Nifty is stuck range-bound between 23,300 and 23,800.** Selling emerges as it nears 23,800; 23,300 has repeatedly acted as support (yesterday it took support there and recovered). **Watch 23,300 closely today.**
- **If Nifty decisively closes below 23,300, expect a big fall** — not a small one; there are indications Nifty could slide **600–700 points in a couple of days.**
- On the upside, **if it can cross 24,000 with volumes** — plus a more positive macro backdrop (West Asia calming, the US deal completed and the UK deal finalized) — there are indications of a **big rally.** Absent that, the very near term stays trapped 23,300–23,800.
- **Underlying weakness is settling in unnoticed.** The broader market looks strong (sector rotation, IT propping up Nifty), but breadth has been positive for two straight months in mid/small-caps, and where stocks are near 52-week highs, **block deals and large volumes** are appearing — unclear whether that is distribution or fresh buying.
- Flagged **Newgen** as an example: ~3.5 crore shares traded yesterday and the stock rose ~20%, but one must check whether genuine delivery-backed follow-on buying came. (Later in the show the anchor cited Kutumba Rao's figure: only ~27 lakh delivery against 3.5 crore volume — i.e. ~85% speculative.) He urged both traders and investors to watch whether deliveries accompany volumes.
- **What saved the market the last 2–3 days = IT stocks.** However much they're criticized, even after hitting a multi-year/52-week low, IT staged a "dead cat bounce" and supported the market; without it, the market might have breached 23,000 on the downside.
- Sees **more scope for weakness**, putting **~80% odds on an "El Niño effect"** rather than a rally — a weak/uneven monsoon hurting agriculture and rural-facing segments like microfinance institutions.
- **MSME early stress** is clearly visible — a key tell on whether the economy's undercurrent is weakening. Whether the government's new scheme overcomes it or stress increases matters greatly, since MSMEs drive maximum employment and exports.
- **RBI sold gold** (quantum unknown; said to protect forex reserves). Out of ~880 tons of reserves, if it emerges that e.g. 60–100 tons were sold, that could be a **panic trigger.**
- **WPI to be revised from June 15** — may be more realistic; if inflation reads higher, expect clear indications of a rate hike next round even if the RBI's MPC holds this time.
- **Friday is the most important trading day this week** — all eyes on the RBI meeting and commentary (economy, West Asia, any monsoon-impact remarks), with a weekend and two holidays following.
- **Worldwide we are becoming weak.** Korea has moved to 7th and India has effectively fallen to 8th in market cap; small countries/companies are commanding overall market cap. **FII selling is intense — almost Rs 400 crore an hour.**

## Trading framework / positioning

- Set aside the option clutter (puts at 24,000 / 23,000, calls): for **fundamentally sound stocks**, those with a **3–4 year view and patience** should buy according to results and earnings visibility, and sit tight.
- But this is **more of a trading market.** A big move like Newgen's ~20% in a week is a huge annualized return (~1,000% if 20% in a week); **book partial profits immediately and trail a stop** to secure the rest.
- **PCR signals:** 0.7 is the buying level — take positional trades as PCR nears 0.7; **book profits as it nears 1.2.** (Yesterday PCR rose from 0.7 to 1.03, and the market recovered to close 100+ points up.)
- **VIX** falling to ~15 is a positive for bulls; if 23,300 isn't breached, one can attempt a **downside positional trade.**
- Overall: **stay invested, but trade with small positions** — except for extreme long-term investors, don't take heavy positions given rising futures volatility.

## Stock calls (viewer Q&A)

- **Mass Chip (Maschip) — Hold (not for short term).** Viewer holds 2,500 at 215 for one month. Kutumba Rao: don't buy this for the short term at all. It went to 280–290 last Sep–Oct, fell to 145, then rose to 230 with a higher top. Use a trading stop to limit losses; a big rally needs a cross of 250, which may not come easily. Short-term holders can exit into a small rally — but it's a good **long-term (1–3 year)** hold with good potential.
- **Good Luck India — Hold (long term).** Viewer bought ~900; stock touched 1,400+, now ~1,300. Results weren't as great as expected, but the stock held up at higher levels. If bought lower, book partial profits and trail a stop. Low P/E supports holding long term.
- **Suzlon — Hold (no risk).** On the recent SEBI penalty (~Rs 28 crore), he sees no risk: it's already appealed to SAT and is routine (cf. an old Reliance Petrochemicals case later dismissed). For a company with quarterly profit above Rs 2,000 crore, a ~Rs 20 crore notice is nothing; the company is doing well — hold.
- **Honeywell Automation — Hold.** Viewer holds 12 shares bought at 37,000. Not a share to watch daily; a high-priced name that has frequently given good returns over time and isn't cheap — just hold.
- **MRF — Hold.** Viewer at ~6% loss. Once bought, "put it in the locker" and review after a year or two; not a stock to track daily.
- **Bata India — Avoid.** Despite the "discount," he won't buy: competition has risen sharply and results aren't great; the stock is still near its 52-week low (~650–660) even as peers like Relaxo rose. "Buy Bata footwear, not the share now."
- **Cello World — Avoid (could fall to 250).** Viewer wanted to put Rs 1 lakh each into Cello World and MV Photovoltaic on a China-import-restriction thesis. Kutumba Rao called that reasoning incorrect: Cello World can't withstand competition and has made lower-tops/lower-bottoms since listing; even after a somewhat impressive recent quarter it sits near its 52-week low. Betting on an unrelated macro tailwind is "foolishness"; he feels it could touch even 250.
