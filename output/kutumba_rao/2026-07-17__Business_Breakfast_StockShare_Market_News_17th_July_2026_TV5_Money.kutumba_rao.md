## Market view
- **Consolidation mood, with a bit of weakness possible.** Crude isn't falling — **stable near $85, definitely a negative for India**. It remains a **buy-on-dips market, not sell-on-rallies**; but a **volume break below 24,000 would turn it into a sell-on-rallies market**. **Stay invested in good stocks.**
- **IT vs Banks is the game.** The results season is coming to completion, and the index-dominating heavyweights report now — **Reliance today** (watch O2C/refining margins, Jio retail margins, Jio ARPU and customer additions; comes after market). Reliance can't stand above **1,300** despite the Jio-IPO announcement — watch for an indication in the results. **Tomorrow's bank results will decide Monday's direction.**
- **Flows/structure**: investors are becoming very quick-footed — as secondary-market opportunity shrinks they rush to the **primary/IPO market** (SBI Funds got ~**Rs 3 lakh crore** of bids). Domestic funds have **deployed most cash — MF cash holdings at a 5-year low** (a danger if a sudden global crash needs support). The market is **retail-dominated**; even **Adani Enterprises' FPI stake fell from 23-24% two years ago to 8.8%** — retail is holding stocks up, not institutions.
- **Won't move much on global cues** unless a **dramatic escalation (US-Iran or Ukraine-Russia)**. Watch **semiconductor losses globally** — reversal or sustained weakness. The **AI bubble has essentially burst**; whether that money rotates into value EMs/India isn't visible yet because the **rupee is weak (heading toward 97)**.
- **Macro**: monsoon dull again, El Niño strong; **Telugu states 26% rainfall deficit**, reservoirs extremely low → rural/agri impact risk. Positive: **EMS companies shining** on government policy; a new **Index of Services Production** now supplements WPI/CPI.
- **Options/positioning**: put build-up at **23,800 and 23,500** (possible retest of 23,800); **PCR ~1, flat**; **VIX below 13** but bulls want below 12 — no panic. Speculative **longs**: Siemens, Cummins, Sona Comstar, BHEL. **Shorts**: Torrent Pharma, ICICI General Insurance (post-results), HDFC AMC, RBL Bank. Conviction **delivery buying**: Apollo Hospitals, Indian Hotels, NTPC. **Ban list: only Kaynes**, nothing new. Warned that **B-group volume ≠ strength** and **positional trades without a stop-loss are highly risky** (F&O daily moves of 5-11%, e.g. ICICI General -11%).
- **Results read**: good — **GNA Axles** (auto-ancillary), **ITC Hotels** (beat estimates), **Polycab** (FMEG EBITDA **Rs 61 cr vs 10 cr** last quarter; may respond positive today), **News & Software, Menon Bearings, Muthoot Capital, WorkIndia, Jio Financial** (dividend income). **Sanwa Technologies** mixed (revenue uptick, margins down). **Bhachel** [as heard] good but broker-downgraded. **Prestige Estates** Q1 pre-sales fell YoY (vs Rs 12,000 cr) — one to watch (many Hyderabad projects).
