## Market view
- Once **bond yields start weakening**, "we are in for a crisis" — a possible **global recession / economic crisis bigger than COVID**, caused by US actions; no country is immune. US yields are surging and **Japanese long-term yields are at a multi-decade high** too, reinforcing the recession risk.
- **India double impact:** rupee will depreciate significantly — comfortably above **96**, with clear signs of **97–98**; this worsens the **current account deficit**. Don't assume the economy/capital market stays resilient just because of multi-quarter-high profits; a "nature reaction" will hit our market too.
- **Levels:** yesterday's move up to **23,775** was good (he had said only a cross of 23,800 gives comfort); India closed marginally negative but better than global peers. **If 23,300 breaks, "very rough time" ahead.** Many mid/small-caps have run to **PE 40–50** after the rally, so comfort at these levels is low.
- **IT-led hold:** three successive days of IT gains kept Nifty around **23,500–600**, but broader-market weakness is real. **MF exposure to IT is at a 7–8 year low**, so on rotation they may sell IT at higher levels.
- **Tata group:** been cautioning for ~6–9 months on **trust/governance issues**; group has lost **>Rs 10 lakh cr market cap since ~Sep '24** (Tejas, Tata Technologies, big stocks, Tata Sons). This most-trusted group's weakness could drag the overall market.
- **Oil refiners:** still Rs 10–14 losses (per BPCL chairman); breakeven needs **~$85/bbl** crude — unlikely near-term while the West Asian crisis persists. Higher inflation could be a curse for markets.
- **Flows:** FIIs and now some DIIs selling; markets held only because DIIs haven't done big-wave selling and retail SIPs continue. If DIIs shift toward debt/safety of capital, rougher times.
- **Derivatives:** calls/puts balanced (24,000 and 23,000 high OI); **PCR back to ~1**, **VIX ~19** (elevated, slowly fizzling). Watch speculative options activity around 24,000 / 23,800 / 23,300–500.
- **Discipline:** don't chase one-day volume spikes; only stay in stocks where volumes **sustain 3–4 days** (operators use results to lift then distribute). **Stay invested in fundamentally sound stocks; store cash for better valuations.**
