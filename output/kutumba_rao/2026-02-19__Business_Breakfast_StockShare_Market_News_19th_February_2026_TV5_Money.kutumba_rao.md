## Market view
- **No cues at all.** "By and large there are none." How **America's moves against Iran** turn out is the **next big geopolitical move** for markets. It's "there's the tiger, here's the tiger" — once the attack actually happens, the market discounts it. Until there's clarity on how far crude runs, whether there's real disruption, and whether it's only a short-term uptrend that then corrects, **markets will consolidate and run only on stock-specific and sector-specific news**.
- **AI summit fallout will be significant** for Indian IT and Indian AI. With the PM meeting country heads in person, hosting dinners, and meeting industry heads, "at least the government that has been asleep till now is being proactive on AI." Hope: the lost time gets recovered over **three to four years**. All said and done, **politicians decide the policy** — an industry only moves forward if they are proactive. On job fears: the summit narrative is **modernisation of jobs rather than significant loss**, but with the new tools **disruption is inevitable** and employees must watch how **skill sets get reset**. He flagged the **Galgotias University dog-robot copied from a Chinese robot** as "literally a shameful action from a university" — removed immediately, but the damage was done.
- **IT is where the money left.** FIIs sold ~**Rs 11,000 cr of IT stocks in early February**; FII IT holdings are at a **four-year low of Rs 4.49 lakh cr**, after withdrawing ~**Rs 75,000 cr from IT in 2025**. "Ahead of everybody, they were the ones who gauged the developments" — they gauged the future and cut positions. Cited **JP Morgan's "Looking Through the AI Fog"** as a very key report. Whether FIIs **buy back into select IT names** from here is the thing to observe.
- **Trade was extremely narrow yesterday** — no big significant moves; action only selectively in the cash group, with fresh volumes coming into stocks that had mostly been predicted the previous morning.
- **Levels**: calls piled up at **26,000** as usual, with settlement approaching. **Unless it reclaims above 25,800**, the market may stay **stuck in the 25,500-26,000 range**. **Bank Nifty looks stronger**; call **OI heavy at 62,000** — watch that carefully. **PCR 1.22** and rising; **VIX fell to 12.22** → **confidence rising among bulls**, market will stand firm. For the gradual recovery to sustain, **yesterday's low must not be retested or broken today**.
- **Positioning**: speculative longs in **Mankind, Premier Energies, CG Power, ICICI Prudential**; shorts built in **Aurobindo Pharma, Persistent, Waaree Energy, KPIT**; delivery seen in **NTPC, Axis Bank, Alkem, Pidilite**; ban list unchanged — **Sammaan Capital** and one other. **FIIs bought Rs 1,100 cr**, DIIs also bought. **Rupee 90.72**, no significant activity; **US Fed in no hurry to cut**, so the dollar gains and that needs watching.
- **Bottom line: "Stay invested, still buy on declines."**
- **Sector/newsflow**: **Defence stocks are in better shape, without any doubt.** Negative newsflow may hit individual names — **NCC** (NHAI ban on bidding for two years) and **Jindal Saw** (American API Institute suspension letter on quality standards). **TCS**: Chandrasekaran garu's term renewal is given news; the thing to watch is Tata Sons.
- **The E2E Networks lesson — "markets look at the headlines first; they don't look at the news inside."** Nvidia announced a **full-stack sovereign AI ecosystem** with E2E (computing → language models → applications) and the stock went straight to a **20% upper circuit**; E2E then told the exchanges it was **the same existing relationship announced in the first week of January** — nothing new. It closed with gains but out of the circuit, and **anyone who bought the upper circuit in a rush was caught**. It's a good company (**L&T took a ~15-20% stake at 3,500**) except that **the results don't reflect the expectations**. By contrast **Netweb Technologies is doing well on both results and expectations**.
- **Observations, not calls**: **Gokul** rose >10% after a big recent correction; **Bharat Wire Ropes** traded 64-70 lakh shares against a usual 5-6 lakh and rose >6%; **Kothari Products** (the paan-parag company) rose >6% on a large move, up two days running; **Cohance / Suven Pharma** moved after "Jasam Property" bought **70 lakh shares** from the **Jasti Family Trust** — the first such move after a long gap.
