# What Kutumba Rao said — Business Breakfast, 2 February 2026 (TV5 Money)
# https://youtu.be/v2OVhLrFPUs

## Market view
- **Sentiment won't improve** unless one of three things happens: significant government/institutional buying, a stronger rupee, or supportive global events. Otherwise the market will not rise.
- The **STT hike** in the budget "destroyed" sentiment — "like killing the goose that lays the golden eggs." Calls it the one flaw in an otherwise decent budget.
- Invokes the **Laffer Curve**: higher taxes → lower revenue. F&O active traders already fell from **~45 lakh to ~20–22 lakh** after SEBI's curbs; 95% of speculators lose anyway. He sees no logic in the STT move.
- STT numbers: FY25 collections rose ~63%; FY26 budgeted **Rs 78,000 crore** (he expects only **~Rs 62,000–63,000 crore**); FY27 target cut to **Rs 73,700 crore** — the first STT target cut ever — yet he doubts even 75–80% will be reached.
- Warns the budget hurts capital-market fund-raising (IPOs, the govt's own **Rs 80,000 crore disinvestment** target) and so the broader economy.

## OI / levels / indicators
- **VIX** jumped from ~10–10.5 to **15** (highest since June 2025).
- **PCR (Nifty put-call)** closed at **0.59** — lowest since December 2024 (he treats ~0.70 as a buy zone).
- Broad **trading range 24,300–25,300**; needs 2–3 sessions of large moves and a strong stand above **25,200–300** to re-attempt **26,000**. Be cautious.
- Budget is a "Test-match, not a T20" given global uncertainty (US trade deal, Trump crude restrictions on Iran/Venezuela).

## Stocks / sectors he flagged
- **IT** outperformed (Wipro, TCS, Infosys marginally positive). **PSUs down 5–6%** (BEL, ONGC, SBI, Coal India, NTPC). Every Bank Nifty stock closed negative.
- **Infra/railway** (budget push): RVNL, IRCON, Ashoka Buildcon, IRFC — could be better.
- **Data-centre ecosystem** (21-yr tax holiday): Orient Technology, Anant Raj, Cummins, Techno Electric.
- **Medical tourism** — large hospitals only (Apollo, Max Healthcare, Yatharth, KIMS, Narayana); small hospitals won't benefit.
- Cautioned against small pharma/biosimilar penny stocks (only Sun Pharma Advanced Research is well-known among them).
- Good Q3 results: **RR Kabel** (excellent), **Delhivery** (EBITDA Rs 209 crore), **Studds Accessories** (YoY better, QoQ flat). Results to watch: Hyundai, Bajaj Housing Finance, Ather Energy, Tata Chemicals, Thermax, Sundaram Finance, Indus Towers, PG Electroplast.
- Overall stance: market will be extremely stock-/sector-specific; not a time for aggressive trading; wait and pick good stocks during dull markets. Says it is now a "stock-picker's paradise" with only the Indo-US deal still ahead — favour companies that benefit indirectly from the budget and have completed expansion capex.
