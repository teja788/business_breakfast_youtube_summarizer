## Market view (opening)
- **Crude is the whole story.** Brent spiked to ~**119-120 USD/bbl**, then fell to ~**88-89**, trading ~**91.49** as he spoke. Fundamentally crude "should be 60-70 USD" on demand/supply; the premium is purely a Strait-of-Hormuz **supply-disruption / sentiment** move on the back of Trump's contradictory statements ("very complete… pretty much" vs "we could go further"). Iran is threatening any vessel through the strait. He calls Trump "the only rare kind of species" and says **don't trust the words** — be ready for gap-ups and gap-downs.
- Two factors drive everything: **crude price** and the **dollar index (~98.81**, almost touched 100).
- **FIIs keep selling** — ~Rs 6,345 cr cash, ~Rs 3,000 cr in futures (added 21,000 shorts vs 1,480 longs; long ratio 14.6% → **12.4%**). **DIIs bought ~Rs 9,000 cr** (Rs 3,000 cr more than FIIs sold) yet Nifty fell **400 pts** — sentiment overrides flows.
- **Correction zone:** Nifty down >10% from the **5 Jan** high; **52-wk lows 847 vs 20 highs** (BSE). From 5 Jan: 13 stocks up / 37 down; 22 down >10%, 7 down >20%; **~Rs 43 lakh cr** wiped out. Outperformers: ONGC +16%, BEL +9%, Power Grid +7%, SBI, Apollo. Underperformers: Wipro -26%, Jio Finance, Asian Paints (-20-25%).
- **Strategy:** Continue **SIPs**. Add **relatively strong** stocks (large caps like **SBI, RIL** showing strength; also resilient mid/small caps) — fine even in weak markets, will pay off over 2-3 years. Buy in small tranches on dips (~**5% of target**); nobody catches the exact bottom. He bought a little himself yesterday.

One of the largest producers of fast-moving electrical goods, OEM for many leading brands, good turnover, had PLI advantage. Today's news: a JV with a Chinese company in which **Dixon holds 74%**, to make **display modules**; broking estimates put a **~1% profit-margin uplift**. Do not buy with a short-term view.

- **Asian Paints — Avoid / wait.** As crude prices rise it's a **big negative** for paint companies; wait until crude cools before buying. New aggressive competition (Birla Opus, Jindals from ~1.5 yrs back) means market-share/leadership can shrink; it **won't create wealth like before** — a good sound company but expect only **steady returns**, not the old glory.

Result was **flat** (not great) yet the stock rose, maybe on the bonus/equity action; if it holds this level it shows strength. **Hold** — "operators are stronger in this counter." (Later re-confirmed: strong but **speculative** stock; strong operator + strong promoter is better.)
