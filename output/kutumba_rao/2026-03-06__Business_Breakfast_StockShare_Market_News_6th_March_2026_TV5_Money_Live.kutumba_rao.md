## Stock calls (viewer Q&A)

Only one caller, **Kiran from Hyderabad**, addressed Kutumba Rao by name. He asked about three stocks he was looking to buy. Kutumba Rao gave clear views on two of them:

- **DC Infotech and Communication — Avoid.** Low volumes (low enough that one should check for possible insider trading), only modest profits, and net profit margins extremely low at about 3%. Quarter-on-quarter the top line grew but the bottom line lagged. It is essentially a distribution/trading company — it manufactures nothing, distributing products of equipment makers like D-Link — which is why margins are so thin. Such trading companies should be avoided and not bought, unless they are fundamentally very sound with huge volumes like Redington. He said wait-and-watch and would not attempt a buy.

- **Sam Metalics — Watch / buy on dip.** Shows more strength than DC Infotech but trades in a narrow range around 800. Its recent result came slightly weak (top line held but bottom line dipped) and it remains a higher-PE stock. If asked, he would buy it only when it comes down close to 700, near its 52-week low.

- **Fidelity ("Facility") — not specifically answered.** The caller also listed this stock, but Kutumba Rao did not give a distinct Buy/Hold/Avoid verdict on it, so no call is recorded.
