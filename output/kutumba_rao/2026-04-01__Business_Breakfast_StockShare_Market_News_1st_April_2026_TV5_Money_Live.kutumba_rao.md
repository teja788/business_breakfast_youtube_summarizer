## Market view
- **FY27 should be a "relief year."** Starting from low expectations and the lower end of the cycle, with so many negatives already factored in; conservatively expects **~15-20% returns** for FY26-27 on a year-on-year basis.
- **DII support was decisive in FY26:** FIIs sold ~Rs 1.6 lakh cr while DIIs bought ~Rs 8.5 lakh cr — without that the Nifty would have slipped below 20,000 (toward 17,000-18,000).
- **Rupee recovery is critical from here** — rupee lost 9.88% in FY26 (to ~95) while Malaysia +10%, China +5%, Singapore +4%; only Japan (-7%) was comparably weak.
- **Food disinflation** (potatoes -40%, onions -50%, tomatoes -80%, wheat -10%, rice -5-6%) from a domestic glut as Gulf exports dried up → April/FY27 should be "much, much better."
- **War after-effects will be big, not small:** even if the war stops, Iran will charge tolls at the Strait of Hormuz (Rs 15-20 cr per large ship); global rates uncertain 5-6 months; US midterms ahead. Costs get passed to the consumer.
- **Careful stock/sector selection is key** — not everything rises in the expected wave (defence won't simply rise); pick fundamentally good stocks with good earnings visibility. Software hit by FII impact; OMCs and some manufacturers hit by cost pressure; **sugar/commodity industries benefiting** (ethanol, firm sugar prices).
- **Favours PSUs and pharma at lower valuations** — PSUs can do max capex in coming months and will be bigger beneficiaries; expects rapid pro-market/"war measures" (PLI-style), and RBI deferring capital-market-exposure regulation tightening → some stability.
- **Trader read:** highest call OI at 23,000, puts piled at 22,000; Bank Nifty highest puts at 52,000 → at least the first fortnight of the settlement should be on better footing. (He had earlier called buying the 23,000 put, which gave ~Rs 500-600 by Monday.) **VIX very high (~28)** — book profits regularly as gains can evaporate fast; relief if VIX falls into 22-24. PCR ~0.94 (comfort near 1.2). **Stay invested as of now.**
- **HDFC Bank** active on huge volume (~1.25 cr, +2.5%) after Atanu Chakraborty's resignation news; if it recovers it could **lead the rally**.

## Workshop / themes
- "Ideas Unlimited" workshop pushed; PEs that were 150 now 30-40; ~20 TAM-growth sectors to be presented. **CDMO pharma** TAM ~Rs 50,000 cr → ~Rs 1.5 lakh cr in 5 years. **EVs** — attention shifting after the oil shock; India to have surplus electricity in 5 years.
- On autos/EVs: **hybrids attracting more attention than pure EVs** (in China, BYD fell to 4th, VW rose to #1 on long-journey hybrid demand). **Maruti should outperform everybody else.** China 2025 EV sales tripled vs 2024.
