## Market view
- Expects the market to **consolidate**; yesterday's strong close above 24,000 is a "big positive." A new band may form with **24,000 as the base** if it holds above 24,100 and clears ~24,200. Highest call OI at 24,500; highest put at 24,000.
- **Big IPOs (Reliance/Jio, NSE) will absorb primary-market money** — markets can drift up mildly if globally bullish, but can't move fast: "primary markets are always a hurdle for the secondary market" (cash gets blocked 1–2 weeks).
- **Bulls in control as long as Nifty stays above 24,000.** PCR ~1.1 and VIX ~13 = comfortable for bulls.
- **The key macro risk is the monsoon / El Niño.** Food reserves (rice, wheat) at all-time highs, but a weak kharif crop pressures the second half — would hit rural demand, FMCG, two-wheelers.
- **Banks told to aggressively collect forex** (boost FCNR reserves; interest-rate facility through ~September): **HDFC** has first-mover advantage, **Federal Bank** (large NRI deposits) could be in the limelight.
- **FIIs** bought only a token amount (net short-covering); **DIIs/Indian investors buying continuously** → overall a **"buy-on-dips" market**.
- **Best time to exit weaker stocks now**, before Q1 results start (early July); war impact is mostly in Q1.
