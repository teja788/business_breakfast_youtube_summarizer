## Market view
- Today is **settlement day (Nifty weekly options expiry)** → volatility is certain.
- Nifty took support almost exactly at **23,300** yesterday (the cautioned level; below it, 23,000 then 22,600) and reversed ~300–350 points — a "good positive indicator," but to sustain it must **cross and hold above 23,800**.
- IT stocks (Tech Mahindra, Infosys, Wipro), Bharti Airtel and the Bajaj twins "saved the market." Alarming sign: recently-strong names (Maruti, Hindalco, Tata Steel) turned mildly weak.
- If markets **sustain the 23,600–24,300 band**, a positive West Asia development could spark a quick recovery.
- Macro caution: **inflation rising** (WPI/CPI up; RBI Governor warned of a possible rate hike) — harsh June commentary would hit rate-sensitive stocks. April's rally looked driven by **margin funding** (risky, only for experienced/positional traders — don't get carried away even if brokers incentivise it).
- Big-picture: **no Indian company in the world top-100 by market cap** now (a year ago there were three). Many IT stocks at **2008 sub-prime-crisis levels**.
- Geopolitics: stay cautious until the conflict eases; the PM's **Nordic visit** (more trade agreements → less supply-chain risk) is a positive follow-out.
- Options: high premiums due to volatility; call OI concentrated ~24,000 (no payout unless 23,800 crosses), puts ~23,000. PCR 1.24 (a sell-off possible near 1.3); VIX near 20 = more risk for bulls above 20.
- **FIIs** did small buying yesterday/day before — watch if their mood is changing (don't panic prematurely, but stay alert). DIIs buying. Note FII/DII figures were inflated by block deals (GQG in JSW Steel; SBI MF).
- **Rupee** weak above 96 — improvement near 96.2 is imperative or FII buying won't sustain; firmer dollar is a danger signal.
- Bottom line: **stay invested, be stock-specific, avoid cats-and-dogs, keep exiting weaker stocks.** Banks, NBFCs and autos are attractive + beaten-down — good buys for a **2–3 year view**.
