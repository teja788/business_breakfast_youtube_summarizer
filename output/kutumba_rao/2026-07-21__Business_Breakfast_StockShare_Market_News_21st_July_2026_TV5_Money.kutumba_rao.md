## Market view
- **Private banks vs PSU banks is yesterday's clear contrast.** PSU banks had been **under-owned** for a long time and buying interest is appearing; the private banks, where **fresh buying had come recently**, showed **margin pressure** and got sold after results. Yesterday **HDFC and even Axis fell ~4-5%+** while **SBI and PNB showed good strength**. **Maybe this is one of the dips** — brokerage commentary is not very negative despite the margin pressure.
- **The FCNR(B) push "might be a failure."** The government is pressuring banks on FCNR(B) deposits; private banks look likely to fall short, so more pressure goes onto the public sector. But **these deposits are mostly recycled out of FII flows, which is extremely bad** — so as long as FII outflows continue, significant FCNR(B) money won't come. **Even against a $60-70 bn target, crossing $30 bn would be great** — one should count that lucky.
- **West Asia is the new, un-ignorable risk.** The Houthi move on the **Strait of Hormuz / Bab-el-Mandeb** matters because **~7% of global oil supply moves over the Red Sea**. Iran has repeatedly pressured and indirectly funded/armed the Houthis. **If that gets shut, crude shows signs of crossing $100.** Watch whether the global powers focus on it or push for peace.
- **Trump's maverick moves continue** — **up to 50% tariffs threatened on Canada** as early as this month-end. It creates a **very unhealthy atmosphere** and puts the **untrustworthy attitude of the US** in everyone's mind; seeing how Canada was treated, **doubts return about how soon the Indo-US deal gets done**. The **US market is "on the edge" and due for a big correction**; Trump's **credibility is at its lowest** (booed at the World Cup ceremony). **If a US crash comes, all global markets get impacted — assuming otherwise is foolishness.**
- **"Magnificent Seven is old, it's history — from now on it's MANGO stocks."** Interest is shifting to the **coming trillion-dollar IPOs (OpenAI, Anthropic)**; once one of those lists, "everybody will start discussing MANGOs" — and another acronym will follow.
- **Politics**: the Parliament session opened noisily in Delhi, but **the NDA majority is not affected in the slightest** and **reform measures won't go backwards** (TMC broke and members slipped away). The **LTCG-removal rumour** was **denied by MoS Pankaj Chaudhary but not by the Finance Minister** — hopefully no tinkering, because **any comment or tinkering here would definitely be negative**.
- **Domestic structure**: small- and mid-caps still dominate; **liquidity stress in small-cap funds has literally disappeared**, with roughly the **highest inflows since February 2024**, driven by retail flows.
- **Currency/commodities**: the rupee is **continuously weakening** and hit a **two-month low (96.52)** — **97 is now a realistic target**, and once breached **a small panic could set in among importers**. Dollar near a one-week high. **Sovereign Gold Bonds maturing 2032 are up 44%** despite the gold correction; watch whether buying interest returns now that gold is **below $4,000/oz**.
- **Options/positioning**: **highest call OI at 24,500** — given the market's resilience against global moves, watch for a **24,500 attempt** (maybe next series rather than this one). **Puts at 24,200 and 24,000 are critical**; a **breach of 24,200 means going on alert** for a big downside move. **PCR fell 1.42 → 1.28** but is **still significantly above 1**, so the broader market is positioned positive. **VIX 12.98** and creeping higher — a further rise is a loss for the bulls. Speculative **longs**: Radico Khaitan, Torrent Pharma, NHPC, Coal India. **Shorts**: Axis Bank (newly built up), CDSL, Hyundai, Jio Finance. **Delivery conviction**: Policybazaar, GMR Airports, LIC Housing Finance.
- **Trading stance**: new action is starting in many stocks (**Tatva Chintan +20%** on good results, **Som Distilleries +20%** on news-driven activity, **Bhansali Engineering Polymers +12%**, **Tinna Rubber +20%**), but **whether the action is sustained must be watched carefully**. **Take a stock-specific and sector-specific view; don't chase stocks — the market looks slightly fragile, so caution is very much necessary.**
