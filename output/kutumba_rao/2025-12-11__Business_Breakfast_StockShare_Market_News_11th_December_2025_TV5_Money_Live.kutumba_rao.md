# What Kutumba Rao said — Business Breakfast, 11 December 2025 (TV5 Money)
# https://youtu.be/1LIuvoI_DeQ

Kutumba Rao was the fundamental analyst on the panel today, alongside the anchor (Vasanth), technical analyst Ramakrishna, and IPO guest Vikram Uppala (NephroPlus promoter). The detailed opening market monologue (Fed, Kaynes/PG Electroplast/IndiGo "accidents", breadth, accumulation advice) was delivered by the anchor before Kutumba Rao was engaged, so it is not attributed to him here.

## Market view
- On the viewer Q&A he characterised the market as **flat / not trending upward** and warned this is **not the time to "pyramid"** (add to winners): pyramiding should wait until Q3 results give earnings visibility and the market moves into an upward trajectory. In a down/flat market even outperforming stocks may not reward pyramiding — "be very cautious."

## NephroPlus IPO — his questions and verdict
- **Concern on pricing:** he echoed the anchor that the **offering price (Rs 460) is a bit high**, noting it's on an Rs 8 face-value paid-up share and that the company only just turned profitable (FY23 loss → FY25 Rs 67 cr profit) — over a 3-year FY23-FY25 view, pricing is on the higher side.
- **Two structural concerns:** (1) asset-light model relies heavily on **captive clinics inside private hospitals and PPP (government) contracts**, a large share of revenue — growth is exposed if renewal cycles weaken; (2) **receivable days** — with government contracts, receivables (~close to 35% of network on that side) could run high/be restricted. (Vikram answered that ROCE, not AR days in isolation, is the yardstick; ROCE ~24% already factors in working-capital cost.)
- **Verdict:** "**Good issue, no second thoughts — you can apply.**" It's a **niche, one-of-its-kind** segment (rising renal failures, more demand; asset-light; ROCE + growth focus). But **don't crave listing/short-term gains** — subscribe **for the long term**, and it's a company **worth accumulating even after listing** on good opportunities (compared it to Rainbow's niche women-&-child-care positioning). Wished the promoter all the best.
