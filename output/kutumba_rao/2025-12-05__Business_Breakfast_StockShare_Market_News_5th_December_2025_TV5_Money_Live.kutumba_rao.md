## Market view
- Today's market will be **decided entirely by the RBI monetary policy** (10 a.m.). The key questions: will there be **guidance / commentary** that ignores current rupee weakness; whether **CRR/SLR** are changed (net interest margins are stabilising post-Q2); whether the **25 bps cut** actually happens given inflation-linked rates. Inflation is **benign/soft** (though retail doesn't look that soft), with some talk of negative inflation.
- **Most crucial point: will the RBI revise GDP?** Many supportive factors — lower tax burden from the last budget, GST 2.0 reforms, earlier rate cuts, festive-season consumption, front-loading of exports ahead of tariffs. A **positive GDP revision** → market takes it positively; **weak commentary** → negative reaction can't be ruled out.
- **Rupee:** all the news is on rupee depreciation. RBI is **not supporting** the rupee (REER-realistic ~94.75). Export-based sectors (**IT, Pharma**) benefit; consumer durables and fertilizers face higher import costs.
- **Macro cues:** **Fitch upgraded GDP to 7.4%** (positive); **Marc Faber** disappointing ("coming year not great for equities, especially India"); **BofA** sees **Nifty 29,000** in CY2026 (~11–12% upside); **GQG** interested in the **Adani Enterprises rights issue** (positive — observe carefully).
- **Levels/OI:** markets range-bound; **sustaining 26,000 is key** — losing it sets up fresh weakness. Flat/weakish morning opening but positive if it holds. Call OI heavy at **26,500**; crossing **26,000/26,100** is key. **PCR 0.93** (below 1, slightly better); **VIX** down ~4% to **10.82** (comfort). Speculative longs in **Shriram Finance, Adani Enterprises, HDFC Life**; shorts in **Kaynes, Biocon** (-6%), **IndiGo** (below 5,500); wild shorts in **SBI** ahead of the policy. Conviction-with-delivery in **Torrent Pharma, LIC Housing, GMR Airport**. Band list: **Sammaan Capital, Bandhan Bank**. Rupee above 90 — no relief unless below 90.
- **Next one month driven by three events in sequence:** Indo-Russian meeting outcome → US Fed outcome → Indo-US trade deal outcome.
