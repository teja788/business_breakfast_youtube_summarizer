## Market view
- **Range-bound market.** He had said yesterday morning that it would be enough if the Nifty defended **24,000** — and it did, closing **24,052** despite a weak open. Sustaining that support has to be taken as positive, especially on weekly-options settlement day.
- **It isn't only the geopolitical news overhang** — broader macro issues are slowly coming onto the screen. The **rupee crossed 96** again; despite the Finance Ministry's FCNR(B) and related steps to attract deposits and lift forex reserves, it isn't able to stay attractive because the rupee keeps weakening. **As long as rupee weakness persists, FIIs may not buy aggressively** — they had stopped selling and were buying modestly, but there are indications that buying may stop again.
- **Inflation is continuously rising**: CPI rose the other day, and **WPI jumped to 9.9%** yesterday, closing just below double digits. There is a clear **monsoon overhang and an oil-price overhang** on inflation. He'd follow this macro point very carefully.
- **US**: the Fed's commentary repeated the 2% inflation aim, and yesterday's number was shown as modestly turning down — but how long consumer inflation sustains depends on crude; if gasoline prices rise again, inflation will jump too.
- **Options**: **24,500 calls unwound** a bit; position build-up is now mostly at **24,100**, then 24,500. **If it sustains above 24,100, weekly options lean toward 24,500.** Puts are expectedly piled at **24,000**. **PCR fell 1.43 → 1.02** (he'd flagged Monday's 1.43 jump as unsustainable); **below 1 means weakness has been generated**. **Volatility rising to 13.75** — comfort for bulls only near **12**; a **cross above 15 is danger**.
- **IT is the swing sector today**: IT stocks supported the market for six or seven sessions, but after the IBM result the ADRs (Infosys, Wipro) traded weak — so IT may drag today, unless Indian IT sustains on the momentum built up over those sessions.
- **Speculative longs**: **Radico, Alkem, Divi's Labs, Torrent Pharma** — a jump came in pharma; assume positions are being raised ahead of results. **Speculative shorts**: **Tata Elxsi** (expectedly), **HCL Tech**, **Eternal** (shorts built further).
- **Conviction with delivery**: **Nykaa, Petronet, HDFC Life, KFin Tech**. Ban list has nothing much except CANS.
- **Broader market is where the action is**: **midcap and smallcap indices are near all-time highs** while **Nifty and Sensex are still ~8% below** theirs. Wait carefully and deliberately for opportunities in the broader market and catch them. **Trade only stock-specifically** — don't lump the sector into one basket.
- **Caution on the froth**: Landmark Cars, Visitas (cement, +7%), Eureka (+11-12% on local speculation, up to 400), Harim Pipes, Wanbury (at 340, near new highs) — **be cautious with stocks like these**. **Just Dial** rising almost 20% to 800-plus was the biggest surprise, but whether the action continues has to be seen. He also said plainly: **don't take the "EDA" stocks**.
