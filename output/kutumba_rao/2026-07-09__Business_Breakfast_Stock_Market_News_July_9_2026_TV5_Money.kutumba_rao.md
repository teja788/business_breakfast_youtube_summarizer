## Market view
- **Yesterday's fall was overdone.** He had warned to "expect doubt" once Iran's chief spoke strongly against America, but didn't expect the peace deal to collapse so fast. A **300-400 point** cut was justified; **500+ points was unwarranted** given how European markets and US futures behaved — it should have been ~300-350. Today should recover only what fell.
- **Levels**: the key is reclaiming **24,000** and holding a couple of sessions — the market doesn't gain confidence otherwise. He'd flagged yesterday that a break of the **24,200-300 base** would drop it easily to **23,800**, so the fall was no surprise (24,200 broke at "super speed"). Highest **put OI at 23,500**; if it slips below 23,800, expect "extreme short-term" weakness. Calls unwound from 24,500 → 24,200-300; **more put OI than call OI** now. **PCR 1.38** (an entry edge for positional traders appears nearer **0.7**).
- **Rupee** fell ~**60 paise to 95.55** (biggest fall in a month); **VIX +26% to 14.68** (from below 12) → clear panic/uncertainty.
- **Crude is the swing factor**: elevated crude is a clear negative for India; no big market move until it comes down. Government hasn't cut petrol/diesel because there's still no price certainty.
- **Sectors**: only **metals, pharma, defence-metals** may rise near-term, but the metal rally depends on West Asia. **Monsoon** turned vigorous (floods in places) but uneven/structurally dislocated — watch Kharif sowing over the week.
- **Execution over order books**: in defence/aerospace, order-book exuberance ≠ all stocks rising — **execution is what matters** (cited Titagarh, Jupiter Wagons rising then fading to 52-wk lows when execution slowed). Markets will reward only **exceptional results**; **avoid broad-based buying, do selective stock-picking**, treat falls as buying opportunities.
- **IPO / newsflow**: **SBI Funds Management** IPO (Rs 7,300 cr, band 545-574, largest AMC) will keep unlocking SBI's intrinsic value; watch newsflow from Nalco, Suven/Suja Katco Pharma (South Africa expansion), HFCL (order book after a corrective phase).
